# THE MOVING TARGET — Beta Reader Guide

Thank you for reading THE MOVING TARGET. Your feedback is invaluable. Below are guidelines to help you give the most useful responses.

## What I Need From You

### 1. Reading Experience Tracker
As you read, please note:
- **Where you decided to keep reading** (the chapter where you were hooked)
- **Where you considered stopping** (if any)
- **When you figured out each twist** (chapter number, what gave it away — see Q-set 3)

### 2. Character Reactions (trust level, 1–10)
Rate your trust in each character at the three hinge chapters — **Chapter 10**, **Chapter 20**, and **Chapter 30**:
- Sloane Vance (the Architect)
- Jae-won Kang (the Hacker)
- Naïma El-Amrani (the Cat)
- Theo Harlow (the Grifter)
- Emeka Okafor (the Hitter)
- Vesna Janković (the "academic"/the Hunter)
- Marcus Reinhart (the Client)
- The Conductor (the AI)

### 3. Key Questions
After finishing, please answer:
1. At what point did you realise **the Leviathan was a front** and the real prize was something else? What was the clue?
2. When did you suspect **the Client (Reinhart) was the true villain**?
3. Did the **midpoint twist (Chapter 20)** feel earned? Why or why not?
4. When did you realise **the prize was a person** (the hidden compartment)? Did the "ghost headcount" tip you off?
5. Were there moments where you felt "this wouldn't happen" / "this plan has a hole"?
6. Which **crew member's POV** did you most look forward to? Why? Which least?
7. Did the **turn-the-train climax** surprise you, or did you see the inversion coming?
8. Did the **ending / ELVIA reveal** satisfy you? Did it haunt you?
9. Would you recommend this book? To whom?
10. What was the single **best line or moment**? The single **weakest**?

### 4. The Puzzle Layer (important — please fill in)
This book contains **solvable codes**. Your honest experience here is critical:
1. **The Timetable Cipher** — Did you try to decode the ciphertext (`QPMQDJUVXXKPHKOAPYTBRZ`) yourself? If so, when, and did you find the key? Was it solvable without help? Too easy / too hard?
2. **The ELVIA anagram** — Did you notice the signature "ELVIA" and try to rearrange it? When?
3. **The timestamp acrostic** — Did you notice anything about the chapter timestamps / minutes? (Five chapters' minutes spell a word.)
4. **The recurring numbers (7 and 600)** — Did you notice the pattern? Did it feel organic or forced?
5. Overall: did the puzzle layer **add** to your enjoyment, or distract? Would you want more / less of it?

### 5. Pacing Feedback
Mark any chapters that felt:
- **Too slow** (chapter numbers)
- **Too fast** (where you wanted more detail)
- **Perfect** (chapters you couldn't put down)
- Did the **chapter-end hooks** keep you reading, or did any feel repetitive?

### 6. Emotional Impact
- Did any scene make you tear up or come close?
- Did any scene give you chills or make you hold your breath (the boarding? the Vault Car crack? the car-to-car hunt)?
- The **rescue of Lina** — did it land emotionally, or should it hit harder?
- Were there scenes that should have been more emotional but weren't?

### 7. Spoiler-Free Feedback
If you discuss the book before all readers have finished, please avoid revealing:
- That the **Leviathan is a front** (the real prize)
- That **the prize is a person** (the hidden compartment)
- The identity of **ELVIA** and what the name means
- That the **terminus is a trap**
- The **turn-the-train** climax mechanic
- Any solution to the **cipher / acrostic / anagram**

## What This Book Is Trying To Be
A stylish, propulsive heist thriller in the tradition of *Ocean's Eleven*, *Lupin*, and *Bullet Train*, with the puzzle density of *The Da Vinci Code* — a single night on a never-stopping train, a crew of experts under cover identities, and a con where "the plan you're shown isn't the plan." The target reader enjoys being outsmarted fairly, and likes the option to solve the codes before the thief does. Tight 3rd-person, cinematic, clean (14+), translation-friendly.
