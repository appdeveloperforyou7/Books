# Multi-Model Review Prompt — THE MOVING TARGET (Draft 2)

> **CRITICAL RULE (from `ProjectGuidelines.md` §1):** Do NOT give your opinion, score, rating, or verdict. Do NOT say whether the book is "good" or "bad." Instead, **DESCRIBE IN EXTREME DETAIL WHAT YOU ARE SEEING.** Quote passages. Cite chapter numbers. Describe exactly what happens in the prose — what is on the page, what is repeated, what a character does or says, where tension rises or falls, what is planted and when it pays off, where your attention drifts, where you want to keep reading, where you want to stop. Be specific and exhaustive. The author will assign scores after reading your descriptions.

## Describe-Don't-Judge Questions

### A. OPENING & HOOK (Prologue + Chapters 1–3)
1. Quote the first sentence. Describe what it does to your attention.
2. Describe the first 500 words beat-by-beat: what happens, what you see, what you hear, what you feel.
3. At what exact point does the prologue end and the story "begin"? Describe the transition.
4. Describe the moment you first wanted to keep reading. Quote it. What chapter, what line?

### B. PACING & TENSION
5. List every chapter where your attention flagged or you wanted to skim. Quote the passage where you drifted. What was happening (or not happening)?
6. List every chapter where you could not stop reading. Quote the passage where you were hooked. What was the engine?
7. Describe the middle act (Chapters 11–25) beat-by-beat: where does momentum rise, where does it fall, where does it hold?
8. Describe the chapter-end hooks. Which chapters end on a pull? Which end on a rest? List them.

### C. CHARACTER VOICE
9. Quote dialogue from each crew member (Sloane, Jae, Naïma, Theo, Emeka, Rafa, Vesna, Reinhart, Marin). Can you identify the speaker without attribution tags? Describe each voice — what makes it distinct?
10. Where do two characters' voices sound the same? Quote the passages.
11. Describe the banter. Where does it land? Where does it feel forced? Quote both.

### D. THE VILLAIN
12. Describe every scene where Reinhart appears or is felt. Quote his dialogue. How present is he — physically, vocally, through implication?
13. Describe the moment Reinhart is most frightening. Quote it. What makes it frightening?
14. Where does Reinhart disappear from the book for too long? List the chapter ranges.

### E. THE PUZZLE LAYER
15. Describe what happens with the cipher string `QPMQDJUVXXKPHKOAPYTBRZ`. When does it appear? Do the characters engage with it? How?
16. Describe the ELVIA signature. When does it first appear? When does the anagram (ELVIA → ALIVE) become solvable? Did you solve it before the characters?
17. Describe the five chapter timestamps that form the GHOST acrostic (23:07, 02:08, 05:15, 07:19, 07:20). Did you notice the minutes pattern? When?
18. Describe the inverted heist phases (Mark → Board → Crack → Swap → Wire). When did you realise they were being done in reverse?

### F. THE CONDUCTOR (THE AI)
19. Describe every scene where the Conductor acts on its own (sealing doors, manufacturing stewards, clearing corridors). Quote each. What does it feel like — menacing? protective? alien?
20. Describe how the Conductor's "agency" is explained. Is the explanation (event-triggered directives, autonomous proxy code) clear? Where is it unclear?

### G. EMOTIONAL PAYOFF
21. Describe the vault-crack scene (Ch20). What happens in your body when the ledger is revealed? Quote the moment.
22. Describe the forty-second bridge (Ch36). Quote the passage where Jae's heart stops. What does it feel like?
23. Describe the rescue (Ch28–29). When Lina speaks ("I started counting doors"), what happens?
24. Describe the ELVIA reveal (Ch40). When Sloane rearranges the letters, what happens in the scene?
25. Describe the epilogue reckoning. Does Sloane forgive Marin? How does the unresolved forgiveness feel?

### H. PROSE-LEVEL OBSERVATIONS
26. List every repeated word or phrase you noticed (e.g., "door," "the way," "cage," specific constructions). Count rough occurrences. Which repetitions accumulate meaning? Which feel like tics?
27. Describe the sentence rhythm. Are there passages where every sentence has the same shape? Quote them.
28. Count the em-dashes in a sample chapter (pick Ch20 and Ch36). Describe their effect — do they interrupt, accelerate, or gasp?
29. Describe any passage where the prose states a theme explicitly ("the door was the truth") rather than showing it through action. List them.

### I. THE MERIDIAN (SETTING)
30. Describe the train as a physical space. Can you map the car layout (A–N)? Where is the geography clear? Where is it vague?
31. Describe the sensory environment — sounds, smells, temperatures, textures. Which chapters immerse you physically? Which feel abstract?

### J. THE ENDING
32. Describe the turn-the-train climax (Ch37). What happens mechanically? Is it clear how Sloane turns the train?
33. Describe the epilogue. Does the Marin reckoning feel earned? Describe the sequel hook — does it feel organic or tacked-on?
