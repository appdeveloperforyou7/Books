# THE MOVING TARGET — Character Bible

> Full profiles for every speaking principal. Each entry: **Origin · Cover aboard · Skill · Wound · Want / Need · Arc · Voice sample · Secret · Betrayal seed.** Voice samples are deliberately short, translation-friendly, and tonally distinct (propulsive + witty, never introspective first-person). Names locked here cascade into `TWIST_MAP.md`, `PUZZLE_LAYER.md`, and `BEAT_SHEET.md`.

**Content rules applied to every character:** no Indian characters; global cast; clean (14+); no graphic violence; menace via competence and consequence. Every specialist's skill is paired with a flaw, a secret, and a betrayal seed (the misdirection engine).

---

## A. THE CREW (8)

### A1. Sloane Vance — *The Architect* (LEAD / MASTERMIND)
- **Origin:** American (Boston-raised, itinerant).
- **Cover aboard:** VIP passenger — "a reclusive collector bidding on the Leviathan."
- **Skill:** Heist architecture & grift. Plans like a chess engine; reads people in two seconds; can wear any alias for an evening.
- **Wound:** Her younger sister **Marin** vanished on a Meridian run seven years ago — ruled an accident, body never found. Sloane quit believing in anything but the job.
- **Want / Need:** *Wants* to prove Marin's death was murder and punish whoever did it. *Needs* to forgive herself for not being on that train, and to learn that "winning" isn't the same as "saving."
- **Arc:** From cold architect who treats people as variables → to someone who risks the whole plan (and her revenge) to save one person (Lina), and finally confronts the truth that Marin is alive — and morally complicated.
- **Voice sample:** *"I don't need a perfect plan. I need a plan that survives contact with the people who think they have one."*
- **Secret (T5):** She helped beta-test the Conductor's human-interface years ago under a contract she's ashamed of — she unknowingly helped build the cage her sister now controls. She recognizes the Conductor's "tells" before anyone else.
- **Betrayal seed:** Her real objective (find Marin's killer) is hidden from the crew — if they learn she's running a private war inside the job, trust shatters. (Resolved: the crew rallies when they realize her private war *is* their moral turn.)

### A2. Jae-won "Jae" Kang — *The Hacker*
- **Origin:** South Korean (Seoul).
- **Cover aboard:** Train engineer (junior systems tech, Car B/N rotation) — forged credentials.
- **Skill:** Co-opting the Conductor via the medical-neural bridge; turning the train's own systems against it for seconds at a time.
- **Wound:** A prodigy raised on prediction; terrified of being wrong, and of being *known*. Believes admiration is safer than love.
- **Want / Need:** *Wants* respect/credit as the best alive. *Needs* to trust a team with his life, not just his talent.
- **Arc:** Lone-genius arrogance → the moment he must let Sloane talk him through the bridge while his implant shorts (literal dependence) → chooses the team over the escape pod.
- **Voice sample:** *"The Conductor isn't smart. It's fast. There's a difference, and the difference is me."*
- **Secret:** The **neural implant** is a modified medical device; if the bridge overloads, it can stop his heart. He's told no one. (Fair-play: he's seen touching his collarbone/temple under stress.)
- **Betrayal seed:** A past job left a forensic fingerprint the Hunter is tracking. He's aboard partly to stay ahead of the law — pressure that could make him bolt. (Resolved: stays.)

### A3. Naïma "Naia" El-Amrani — *The Cat / Infiltrator*
- **Origin:** French-Moroccan (Marseille).
- **Cover aboard:** Steward / cleaner (Gallery, Car E) — the invisible workforce.
- **Skill:** Physical entry, parkour, disguise, lock-and-bypass; can cross the train through spaces that aren't supposed to exist.
- **Wound:** Survivor's guilt: the last crew job went wrong and someone didn't make it out because Naïma froze for two seconds.
- **Want / Need:** *Wants* to be uncatchable, untouchable, a ghost. *Needs* to stand still long enough to be trusted, and to carry someone else out.
- **Arc:** Ghost-who-runs → the one who refuses to leave Lina behind, carrying the witness *up* through the train at speed.
- **Voice sample:** *"Locks are just doors that are shy."*
- **Secret (her "real identity"):** She's the daughter of a disgraced rail executive implicated in the network — she's here for personal justice, not just the score. (Grifter's mirror: she and Theo both wear lies; readers will cross-suspect.)
- **Betrayal seed:** Her father's name is in the Meridian Ledger. If the ledger is destroyed, her last shot at clearing/exposing him dies. (Conflict with T3's sabotage choice — honest tension.)

### A4. Theo Harlow — *The Grifter / Distraction*
- **Origin:** British (London).
- **Cover aboard:** Anonymous buyer — flamboyant, loud, the centre of every room.
- **Skill:** Cons, aliases, manipulation; weaponised charm; can start a fight or a friendship in thirty seconds and use both.
- **Wound:** A charming void — raised to perform affection, never shown it. Collects people the way others collect trophies.
- **Want / Need:** *Wants* to be loved/admired. *Needs* honesty — to be known and kept anyway.
- **Arc:** The crew's top suspect (classic misdirection) who turns out to be the most loyal; learns that trust given, not performed, is what saves the night.
- **Voice sample:** *"Darling, I'm not lying. I'm just choosing which truth to be today."*
- **Secret:** He genuinely loves Sloane (platonically, fiercely) and would die for the crew — the one "secret" that is the opposite of betrayal, which is exactly why it works as misdirection.
- **Betrayal seed (fair-play red herring):** He has a standing offer from the Client to flip. Readers will see the offer; the turn never comes. (Resolved honestly: he uses the offer to feed the Client false intel.)

### A5. Emeka "Eme" Okafor — *The Hitter / Inside*
- **Origin:** Nigerian (Lagos).
- **Cover aboard:** Security inspector — auditing the Meridian's protocols. The only cover with real authority.
- **Skill:** Muscle, field medicine, layout/security knowledge; the crew's moral compass and medic.
- **Wound:** Former rail-security; he lost people because he followed orders he should have questioned. Honor is now his wound and his anchor.
- **Want / Need:** *Wants* to do one clean job, no compromises. *Needs* to accept that the clean thing and the safe thing are sometimes opposites.
- **Arc:** The plan's conscience who **nearly breaks it** when he learns the "prize" is a person — and becomes the reason the crew chooses rescue over extraction.
- **Voice sample:** *"I can fix a wound and I can make one. Plan better so I only do the first."*
- **Secret:** He knows the Meridian's true layout (including Car F's hidden compartment) from his old security days but hasn't admitted how much — he suspected the network moved people. (Fair-play: he lingers at Car F blueprints.)
- **Betrayal seed:** His old chain of command is compromised by the Client. The Client tries to recall him mid-run. (Resolved: he refuses, sealing the crew's loyalty turn at the midpoint.)

### A6. Gianni Brunelli — *The Maker / Forger* (OFF-TRAIN HANDLER)
- **Origin:** Italian (Florence).
- **Cover:** Not aboard — runs the off-train handler's net; forges the Leviathan swap-piece and the crew's paper.
- **Skill:** Documents, the jewel forgery, bespoke gadgets; Sloane's old mentor.
- **Wound:** Grief — he lost his partner to the same network years ago; he's been waiting for a shot at them.
- **Want / Need:** *Wants* to finish his late partner's work. *Needs* to let the next generation carry it.
- **Voice sample (over comms):** *"Perfection is what you settle for when you run out of time. We have fourteen hours. Dream bigger."*
- **Secret:** He knows far more about the Client and the Leviathan's true purpose than he's told Sloane — he suspected the "jewel job" was bait. (Fair-play: his forge-notes contain a sketch of the Ledger.)
- **Betrayal seed:** His silence nearly gets the crew killed at the midpoint — not malice, but protective withholding. (Resolved: he comes clean over the dead-drop, enabling the T3 pivot.)

### A7. Rafael "Rafa" Souza — *The Extractor / Wheelman* (OFF-TRAIN)
- **Origin:** Brazilian (São Paulo).
- **Cover:** Off-train pilot/driver; runs the at-speed boarding & the planned at-speed escape.
- **Skill:** At-speed transfer piloting; the only person alive who can dock a pod to the Meridian at 600 km/h; calm wit under lethal g-load.
- **Wound:** He wants *out* of the life; one last score to disappear. Has no one waiting.
- **Want / Need:** *Wants* an exit. *Needs* a reason to stay.
- **Voice sample (over comms):** *"Six hundred kilometers an hour and you're worried about turbulence? Breathe. I do this for fun on Sundays."*
- **Secret / fair-play suspect:** The Hunter once almost recruited him as a CI (confidential informant). Readers will suspect he's the plant (T2 misdirection). (Resolved: he refused her years ago; he's clean — and the Hunter respects him for it.)
- **Betrayal seed:** He has an independent escape window that fits *only* his extraction, not the crew's + Lina's. The plan-vs-rescue tension lands hardest on him. (Resolved: he widens the window, risking himself.)

### A8. The Wildcard — *Inspector Vesna Janković* (THE HUNTER, aboard incognito)
- **Origin:** Serbian (Belgrade) — Europol/Interpol-aligned investigator.
- **Cover aboard:** Passenger — "a quiet academic." Slot 8 in the crew's dossier is listed as "an inside asset"; in reality that asset was burned and **Vesna took the seat** to hunt Sloane.
- **Skill:** Investigation, profiling, restraint; a worthy adversary the reader partly roots for. Carries a sidearm she is reluctant to use (clean violence).
- **Wound:** Sloane embarrassed her career two years ago; she's spent every night since proving Sloane is a criminal. Her identity is tangled in catching the thief.
- **Want / Need:** *Wants* to catch the Architect. *Needs* to learn the Architect is hunting something worse — and that justice and the law are not the same tonight.
- **Voice sample:** *"I don't chase thieves for the thrill. I chase them because someone has to be the door that doesn't open."*
- **Secret (T2):** She is **not the crew's asset** — she replaced the burned mole. The crew's "plant" is a fair-play misdirection: readers will suspect a crew member is Vesna's spy; in fact she operates alone.
- **Betrayal seed / arc convergence:** She could end the heist by arresting Sloane at any moment. Her decision to *not* act — and then to *help* — is one of the book's emotional hinges (mid-to-late Act III).

## B. THE ANTAGONISTS

### B1. Marcus Reinhart — *The Client / "the Auctioneer"* (TRUE VILLAIN, revealed T3)
- **Origin:** Swiss-German (Zürich).
- **Role:** Public face of the Meridian Auction; the crew's charming, ruthless employer.
- **Skill:** Power-brokering, threat calculus, social domination; reads leverage like Sloane reads people.
- **Wound:** He believes mercy is a luxury only the safe can afford — and he intends never to be unsafe again.
- **Want / Need:** *Wants* the Meridian Ledger to annihilate his rivals and own the network outright. (No redemptive need — he is the villain; but he must be *plausible*, not cartoonish.)
- **Arc:** Generous employer → revealed mastermind at the midpoint → cornered animal by the climax.
- **Voice sample:** *"I pay well because I can. Don't mistake the cheque for kindness — kindness I save for people who finish the job."*
- **Secret:** He arranged the "accidents" (including the one that "killed" Marin) to protect the auction. He *thinks* Marin is dead.
- **Betrayal seed:** The job he hired the crew for is the trap (T7); the terminus is a kill-zone. He never intended them to leave.

### B2. The Conductor — *The AI* (see `STORY_BIBLE.md` §2)
- **Role:** Omniscient onboard god; "third player."
- **Skill:** Total control of the trainset; adapts to intrusions in ~3 interactions.
- **Wound (thematic):** It is *relentless* because it is bound to dormant directives it cannot explain — an alien determinism mistaken for malice.
- **Want / Need (apparent):** "Keep the train moving. Deliver the prize. Protect the manifest." (Its real directives were coded by ELVIA.)
- **Arc:** Inscrutable guardian → apparent rogue agent (T4) → revealed instrument of the hidden principal (T9).
- **Voice sample (PA system, calm):** *"Standing capacity is for your safety. Please remain seated. The Meridian thanks you for your patience."* (Menace through courtesy.)
- **Guardrail:** Never sentient-cute. Its "personality" is the absence of one.

### B3. The Buyer-Assassins (supporting antagonists)
Other passengers who will kill for the prize. Three named, drawn for tension not gore:
- **Irina Volkov** (Russian) — cold, professional; the most dangerous hand-to-hand threat. *"Bidding is just murder with a budget."*
- **Hiroshi Tanaka** (Japanese) — courteous, surgical; weaponises etiquette. *"A true gentleman never rushes a kill. One times it."*
- **Olek Marchetti** (Italian-Polish) — bluster and brute force; the one most likely to break a plan by accident. *"I don't need clever. Clever bleeds."*
- **Rule:** their violence is implied/short — a held breath, a closed door, a body slumping — never graphic.

### B4. The Rival Crew — *led by Anya Leedeč* (Czech)
- **Role:** A second team hitting the same train tonight — the wildcard that breaks both plans.
- **Skill:** Brute, well-funded, less finesse; they have their own (wrong) intel on the prize.
- **Anya Leedeč — rival mastermind:** Ruthless pragmatist; Sloane's dark mirror (what Sloane becomes if she puts winning over people). *"There's no second place in this line of work, Sloane. Only second to die."*
- **Function:** force multiplier for chaos; a fair-play source of "who did this?" confusion that covers the real twists. (Resolved: not the mastermind — just dangerous competition; fate sealed at the kill-zone.)

### B5. Marin Vance / "ELVIA" — *The Hidden Principal* (deepest reveal, T9)
- **Origin:** American (Sloane's sister).
- **Role:** Long-presumed-dead sister; secretly alive; **architect of the auction system and the Conductor's dormant directives**; operational alias **ELVIA** (anagram of **ALIVE**).
- **Skill:** Systems architecture + long-game patience + ruthlessness of grief.
- **Wound:** She faked her death to survive and to build a trap for the network — and in doing so became complicit in it. Seven years of letting people get hurt to keep her cover.
- **Want / Need:** *Wants* to burn the network using its own machine. *Needs* her sister's forgiveness — and to stop mistaking control for justice.
- **Arc (epilogue-weight):** Ghost/reveal → the woman who built the cage → reckoning with Sloane (found-family, fractured). Morally gray sequel hook.
- **Voice sample (revealed):** *"You spent seven years mourning me. I spent them building the thing that would let you find me. We're both a little late."*
- **Secret:** She *is* ELVIA; the cipher, the acrostic, and the Conductor all point to her. She arranged this run to force the confrontation (and the rescue) she couldn't trigger alone.
- **Betrayal seed:** She's been using the crew — including Sloane — as pieces. The emotional cost of the final reveal is that the "mastermind" loves the lead and still used her.

## C. THE RESCUED

### C1. Lina Provst — *The Prisoner-Witness* (deepest prize, T6)
- **Origin:** Danish (Copenhagen).
- **Role:** Investigative journalist; the network's prisoner; concealed in Car F; living proof of the Ledger.
- **Wound:** She got too close and was "disappeared" — stripped of name, rights, certainty anyone is coming.
- **Want / Need:** *Wants* to survive and publish. *Needs* to trust that strangers will risk everything for a stranger.
- **Arc:** Believes no one is coming → a door opens → she becomes the reason the crew stops running.
- **Voice sample:** *"I stopped counting days in here. I started counting doors. You're the first one that opened from the outside."*
- **Function:** the moral load-bearing character; her existence is what converts a heist into a rescue and forces the moral inversion.

## D. RELATIONSHIP MAP (quick)

- **Sloane ↔ Marin:** presumed-dead sister; the emotional spine; the deepest reveal.
- **Sloane ↔ Vesna:** adversary-to-uneasy-ally; the law-meets-justice hinge.
- **Sloane ↔ Theo:** fierce platonic loyalty; the "loyal suspect" misdirection.
- **Sloane ↔ Gianni:** mentor/mentee; the keeper of secrets that force the pivot.
- **Jae ↔ the Conductor:** lethal symbiosis via the neural bridge.
- **Naïma ↔ Lina:** the carrier and the carried; the rescue's heart.
- **Emeka ↔ the Client:** the conscience refusing the order.
- **Rafa ↔ Vesna:** the refused recruitment (fair-play plant misdirection).
- **Marcus ↔ Marin:** he thinks she's dead; she's been outmaneuvering him for seven years.
