# 17 — Book Design & Layout Spec (Creative Brief for the Designer)

> *Target: a physical + digital object the younger generation will actually carry, photograph, gift, and
> re-read — as beautiful and tactile as it is profound. The design is not decoration; it is part of the
> pedagogy (file 16).*

## 1. Design North Star
**"A devotional object that looks like it belongs on the desk of a 24-year-old designer in 2026, not on a
1970s seminary shelf."** Reference the visual intelligence of: *The New York Times Magazine*, Apartamento,
*Kinfolk*, Penguin Modern Classics deluxe editions, In Parenthesis publishing, DK illustrated references,
and premium wellness books (The School of Life, Eckhart Tolle special editions) — **without** the kitsch.

Three words: **Calm · Layered · Living.**

## 2. The audience's design expectations (Gen Z / younger Millennials)
- **Scannable & modular** — not walls of text. Short blocks, breathing room.
- **Visual hierarchy** — eyes must find the path instantly.
- **Tactile & ownable** — a book worth photographing (#bookstagram, #currentlyreading).
- **Multimodal** — expects audio/link integration; print should feel like the *premium* layer of a system.
- **Honest typography** — real type design, not default fonts; Devanagari treated as art, not ornament.
- **No cultural kitsch** — no stock-mandala clichés, no cartoon gods. Sacred, but contemporary.

## 3. Color system — **FULL COLOR, premium production** (see file 18 for KDP specs)
A restrained, sophisticated palette (no neon, no "temple orange" cliché). This is a **premium full-color
book printed on KDP Premium Color 60# white paper** — color is used **structurally**, not decoratively.
- **Base paper:** warm off-white / cream (uncoated, ~100–120 gsm, matte, tactile).
- **Primary ink:** deep indigo-black (not pure black — softer, "ink" not "laser").
- **Accent 1 — Saffron-amber** (`#C8761A`-ish): reserved for the **Bhakti/devotional** thread & the sacred.
- **Accent 2 — Sage/jade green**: the **Karma/action** thread & nature verses (10.x, 15.x).
- **Accent 3 — Slate blue**: the **Jñāna/knowledge** thread & philosophical verses.
- **Accent 4 — Plum/maroon**: the **Dhyāna/meditation** thread & Sādhana boxes.
- The four path-colors **color-code verses** by their dominant yoga throughout — a quiet, learnable system
  that lets readers find "their" path visually. *(This is a genuine, novel navigation aid.)*
- The four depth-layer symbols (◯ ◑ ● ✦) appear in a neutral charcoal.
- **This is a full-color premium book** — the path color-coding, tinted verse panels, full-bleed warm-cream
  pages, colored chapter dividers, and the Devanagari-as-hero-art all depend on color. (Exact CMYK/HEX values
  and KDP production engineering in **file 18**.)

## 4. Typography
- **English body:** a refined, highly-readable serif with character — e.g., **GT Sectra**, **Tiempos**, or
  **Source Serif**; comfortable measure (~60–66 characters/line), generous leading (1.5).
- **English display/headings:** a clean modern sans for chapter titles & UI — **GT America**, **Söhne**, or
  **Inter**.
- **Devanagari:** a **bespoke or high-craft display face** (e.g., **Fedra Hindi**, **Kohinoor**, or custom)
  — treated as **heroic art**, large and centered above each verse.
- **IAST transliteration:** a sympathetic serif italic, clearly secondary to the Devanagari.
- **Pull-quotes & the famous verses:** set at display size, possibly in the saffron accent — these are the
  "screenshot" moments.

## 5. Grid & page architecture
- **Wide outer margin** (a hard requirement for Layer 3 margin notes, file 16) — a 3-column feel: body /
  verse-zone / outer-margin-rail.
- **Modular blocks**, never running prose across a full page of nothing else.
- **Generous white space** — the calm of the sthitaprajña made visible.
- **Consistent verse-block chassis** (from file 12 §2): number tab · Devanagari · IAST · literal · meaning ·
  commentary · bridge · careful — locked in a vertical rhythm.
- **Running heads/feet:** chapter name (top), and a mini "you-are-here" depth-path indicator (bottom).

## 6. Iconography & system elements (design once, deploy everywhere)
- **Depth symbols:** ◯ Wayfarer · ◑ Student · ● Seeker · ✦ Adept · ◎ Sādhana.
- **Path color-tags** (karma/bhakti/jñāna/dhyāna) as small glyphs beside verse numbers.
- **Callout glyphs:** 🌉 Bridge · ⚠ Careful · ❓ Hard question · 📜 Sanskrit note · ✦ Takeaway · ◎ Practice.
- **Diagram suite** (commission original, modern line-art — NOT clip-art): the **chariot** (Plato's
  Phaedrus echo), the **guṇa wheel**, the **Aśvattha tree**, the **two paths of death** (devayāna/pitṛyāṇa),
  the **field/knower** diagram, the **karma-ladder** (2.62–63), a **timeline**, the **cast of characters
  map**, the **18-step sādhana foldout**.

## 7. The "Living Book" layer — interactivity (younger generation expects this)
- **QR codes / NFC** discreetly placed linking to:
  - a **professional chanting** of each deep-dive verse (Devanagari + IAST audio) — hearing the *gītā*
    "sung" is essential; no book can convey it (file 13).
  - **guided meditations** for the Sādhana modules.
  - **animated diagrams** (the cosmic form, the guṇa wheel in motion).
- A **companion microsite/app** (lightweight): the 18-step sādhana tracker, a glossary search, a
  verse-of-the-day, an audio library, discussion/reflection journal. *The print book is the anchor; the app
  is the practice companion.*
- **Edition as ecosystem:** Premium hardcover (the object) · Paperback (mass) · Enhanced e-book (audio +
  links embedded) · App (the sādhana path).

## 8. Structural / navigational aids
- **Color-coded ribbon markers** (3 ribbons in the hardcover) — readers juggle orientation + commentary +
  glossary.
- **A "reading-path" selector** in the front matter: *"What kind of reader are you?"* → routes to ◯/◑/●/✦.
- **Tabbed page edges** (printed thumb-tabs) by Part, for fast navigation.
- **Pull-out 18-step sādhana foldout** tipped into the back — the spine of Layer 2, detachable.
- **Two page-edge indices** printed on the fore-edge: verse-number on one side, theme on the other.

## 9. Cover & object design
- **No literal Krishna/Arjuna illustration** on the cover (avoids kitsch + reverence issues). Instead: a
  striking **typographic** or **abstract-geometric** cover — e.g., the **OM glyph / the Aśvattha tree /
  the chariot-wheel** rendered as minimal modern line art, or pure type ("THE BHAGAVAD GITA" in magnificent
  Devanagari + English).
- **Materials:** cloth-bound spine, foil-stamp title, deboss; uncoated tactile stock; colored
  edge-staining; a printed linen-wrapped or soft-touch cover.
- **Endpapers:** the **cast of characters** or the **timeline**, printed as a beautiful infographic.
- The object should feel like a **gift-worthy artifact** — because that is how this book will spread.

## 10. What the design must NEVER do
- No stock-mandala / faux-gold / clip-art deity imagery.
- No dense unbroken text blocks (kills the younger reader).
- No conflation of translation and commentary (the Prabhupada/Pattanaik flaw, file 14).
- No tiny Devanagari relegated to a footnote — the Sanskrit is honored, large, centered.
- No buried Sanskrit without a gloss (the jargon-overload flaw).
- No visual chaos — restraint is the aesthetic; white space is sacred.

## 11. Design deliverables to commission (phase plan)
*(Production engineering, KDP trim/bleed/gutter/color-profile/font-embedding specs are in **file 18**.)*
1. **Type & color system** (moodboard → locked palette/type) — values in file 18 §5.
2. **Chapter-spread master templates** (the verse-block chassis + the 4-layer stack from file 16).
3. **Icon & glyph set** + **path color system**.
4. **Original diagram suite** (10–12 pieces).
5. **One fully designed sample chapter** (recommend Ch 9 — 9.22 is iconic) to validate the system end-to-end
   before full production.
6. **Cover & object design** (3 directions).
7. **App/companion microsite MVP** (sādhana tracker + audio).

## 12. The one-line brief to the designer
> *"Design a calm, layered, tactile book that a 24-year-old would be proud to carry, in which the
> Sanskrit is treated as art, every verse is color-coded by spiritual path, the reader can navigate four
> depths of meaning on a single spread, and a companion app turns the book into a daily practice — so
> beautiful and so usable that the design itself teaches the Gita's own lesson of inner stillness."*

## 13. Format locked (see file 18)
- **Trim: 8" × 10"** — hardcover (flagship) **and** paperback (mass), both KDP **Premium Color, 60# white**.
- Bleed file size **8.25" × 10.25"**; gutter 0.625" at ~460 pp; wide Adept outer margin rail.
- Matte covers; same cover art across both bindings; companion enhanced e-book + app.
