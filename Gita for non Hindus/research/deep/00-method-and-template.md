# Deep Commentary — Method & Per-Verse Template

> *This is the engine of the book's accuracy and depth. Every verse of the Gītā (all 700) will eventually
> receive the treatment below. The output is NOT for the reader verbatim — it is the author's working
> reference from which the Student/Seeker/Adept layers of each chapter are authored truthfully. Source every
> claim; flag every uncertainty; present rival readings fairly.*

## Sources of authority (use, and cite, these)
1. **Sanskrit mūla** — Gita Press Gorakhpur / BORI critical edition (the verse text). Verified against
   `research/20-verse-master-verified-sanskrit.md`.
2. **Padārtha (word-analysis)** — wisdomlib "word-split" + Apte/Monier-Williams roots; Sargeant's
   word-by-word where available.
3. **The classical bhāṣyas** —
   - **Śaṅkara** (Advaita, c. 700–750 CE) — IITK Gita Supersite + translations.
   - **Rāmānuja** (Viśiṣṭādvaita, 11–12th c.) — Gītā Bhāṣya.
   - **Madhva** (Dvaita, 13th c.) — Gītā Tātparya.
   - **Abhinavagupta** (Kashmir Śaiva, 10–11th c.) — *Gītārthasaṅgraha* (aesthetic-devotional).
   - **Jñāneśvar** (13th c.) — *Jñāneśvarī* (Marathi bhakti).
   - **Vallabha** (Śuddhādvaita, 15–16th c.); **Rāmānanda / Nimbārka** where relevant.
4. **Modern scholarship** — Sargeant (word-by-word), Zaehner, van Buitenen, Edgerton, Minor, Fowler, Davis
   (*Gita: A Biography*), Sharpe (*Universal Gita*).

## The per-verse record (the template)
For EACH verse:
- **Header:** number, Devanagari, IAST.
- **Anvaya (sandhi-resolved prose order):** the verse unscrambled into syntactic order.
- **Padārtha:** key words → root (*dhātu*), grammatical form, literal sense.
- **Literal translation:** close, word-faithful.
- **Śaṅkara** (Advaita): the core of his reading + the doctrinal point it serves.
- **Rāmānuja** (Viśiṣṭādvaita): his reading; note *prapatti* where it appears.
- **Madhva** (Dvaita): his reading; note eternal self/God distinction.
- **Abhinavagupta / others:** where notable (symbolic, aesthetic, bhakti, Śaiva).
- **Variant / textual note:** any manuscript/numbering variant (else "none material").
- **Cross-references:** other Gītā verses + Upaniṣads/Rāmāyaṇa that bear on it.
- **Doctrinal significance:** what this verse advances (and which chapter-theme it carries).
- **Common misreadings:** the distortions to guard against.
- **Layered rendering:** one line each for Wayfarer / Student / Seeker / Adept.

## Rules of intellectual honesty
- Never present one school's reading as *the* meaning; the Gītā sustains many — say so.
- Where the commentators disagree, name the disagreement and what is at stake.
- If a story/etymology is popular but unhistorical, label it as tradition, not fact.
- Flag anything not independently verifiable as `[VERIFY]`.
- Sanskrit must be accurate (cross-checked against file 20 / Gita Press).
