# Deep Commentary — Chapter 4: Jñāna-Karma-Sannyāsa Yoga (Knowledge, Action & Renunciation)

> *The avatāra doctrine, the primacy of knowledge, and the inner meaning of action. Verified (IITK).
> Landmarks: 4.7–8, 4.13, 4.34, 4.37–38, 4.42.*

**At a glance.** Kṛṣṇa traces the yoga through the *guru-paramparā* (4.1–3), reveals the **avatāra doctrine**
(4.5–8), teaches the paradox of *action-in-inaction* (4.16–24), catalogues the forms of **sacrifice**
(4.25–33), and climaxes with the supremacy of **knowledge** (4.34–42) — "nothing purifies like knowledge"
(4.38).

---

## 4.1–4.3 — The lineage of the teaching
Kṛṣṇa: "I taught this yoga to Vivasvat (the sun-god) → Manu → Ikṣvāku; the royal sages knew it in
succession; by long lapse it was lost; today I teach **you** the same ancient yoga, because you are My
devotee (*bhakto'si*) and friend (*sakhā*)" (4.1–3).
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: the *paramparā* (lineage) shows the yoga's antiquity and authority; its loss is the
*kali-yuga* forgetfulness. Rāmānuja: the devotee-friend qualification (4.3) shows the Lord's accessibility.
**Layers —** W: "This teaching is ancient; it comes to those ready to receive it." / S: guru-paramparā. /
Sk: value the lineage behind your learning. / A: paramparā vs direct revelation.

## 4.4–4.10 — The avatāra doctrine
**4.5–6:** Kṛṣṇa has taken many births (*bahūni me vyatītāni janmāni*) — He knows them all, Arjuna does not.
Though unborn (*ajo 'pi san*), imperishable, the Lord of all — "by presiding over My own prakṛti I take
birth through My *māyā*" (*ātma-māyayā*). **4.7–8 — LANDMARK:** *yadā yadā hi dharmasya glānir bhavati* —
"whenever dharma declines and adharma rises, I manifest Myself (*ātmānaṃ sṛjāmi*); to protect the good
(*sādhūnāṃ*), destroy the wicked (*duṣkṛtām*), and establish dharma — I take birth age after age (*yuge
yuge*)." **4.9–10:** one who knows His divine birth and action (*divyam*) truly (*tattvataḥ*) is not reborn —
comes to Him.
**Padārtha:** *ajo 'pi san* (4.6, "though unborn"), *ātma-māyayā* (4.6, "through My own māyā/divine power"),
*glāniḥ* (4.7, "decline/decay"), *abhyutthānam* (4.7, "rise/uprising"), *sṛjāmi* (4.7, "I manifest/emit"),
*paritrāṇāya* (4.8, "for the protection of"), *dharmasaṃsthāpanārthāya* (4.8, "for the establishment of
dharma").
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: the avatāra's body is not *karma-born* but *māyā-born* (divine); the birth is *līlā*
(play), not bondage. The *māyā* (4.6) is the Lord's own power of self-limitation, not illusion. Rāmānuja: the
avatāra is real; the *divya-śarīra* (divine body) is not constituted by prakṛti's guṇas. Madhva: each
avatāra is the supreme Lord's *avatāra* — Vāsudeva — appearing in different forms. Abhinavagupta: the
avatāra is consciousness's self-disclosure at critical moments.
**Cross-refs:** 9.11 (fools despise the avatāra in human form), 10.41 (all excellence is a spark of Him).
**Misreading:** that avatāra = Christian incarnation (once-for-all, salvific through blood-sacrifice).
Differences: avatāras are **many and cyclic**, not salvific in the atonement sense — they **restore dharma**
and **reveal**.
**Layers —** W: "God comes whenever the world needs Him, in every age." / S: the avatāra doctrine; ātma-māyā.
/ Sk: where do you see the divine breaking through in human history? / A: Śaṅkara (līlā, not karma-born) vs
Rāmānuja (divya-śarīra); cyclic vs once-for-all.

## 4.11–4.15 — Universalism and the non-agent Lord
**4.11:** *ye yathā māṃ prapadyante tāṃs tathaiva bhajāmy aham* — "as one approaches Me, so I receive them;
all follow My path." **4.13 — THE VARṆA VERSE:** *cāturvarṇyaṃ mayā sṛṣṭaṃ guṇakarma-vibhāgaśaḥ / tasya
kartāram api māṃ viddhy akartāram avyayam* — "the fourfold order was created by Me according to **guṇas and
karma**; though its author, know Me as the **non-agent**, immutable." **4.14:** "actions taint Me not; I have
no desire for their fruits." **4.15:** knowing this, the ancients seeking liberation performed action.
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: 4.13 ties varṇa to *guṇa* (quality) and *karma* (action), NOT birth — the four
*varṇas* are psycho-spiritual types. "Though author, non-agent" = the Lord ordains the **framework** but does
not **compel** individual action. Rāmānuja: varṇa is real (divinely ordained), but based on **guna-karma**
(inherent quality + life's work), which may or may not align with birth. Madhva: varṇa is tied to *svabhāva*
(inborn nature). **See file 11 for the full caste treatment.**
**Misreading of 4.13:** citing it to defend birth-based caste oppression; the verse explicitly says
*guṇa-karma-vibhāga* (by qualities and actions), not *janma* (birth).
**Layers —** W: "God meets each seeker in their own way; the social order is by nature and work, not birth."
/ S: guṇa-karma-vibhāga; akartṛtva. / Sk: read your own "varṇa" as your vocation, not your caste. / A:
Śaṅkara/Rāmānuja/Madhva on guṇa vs janma; file 11.

## 4.16–4.24 — Action in inaction: the paradox of karma
**4.16:** "even the wise are deluded about what is action (*karma*) and inaction (*akarma*)." **4.17:**
"one must understand action, wrongful action (*vikarma*), and inaction." **4.18 — THE KEY PARADOX:** *karmaṇy
akarma yaḥ paśyed akarmaṇi ca karma yaḥ* — "he who sees inaction in action, and action in inaction — he is
wise, a yogī, accomplisher of all." **4.19–24:** the one whose undertakings are free from desire
(*kāma-saṅkalpa-varjitāḥ*), whose actions are burnt by the fire of knowledge (*jñānāgni-dagdha-karmāṇam*) —
he does nothing though acting (4.20). **4.24:** *brahmārpaṇaṃ brahma haviḥ* — "Brahman is the offering,
Brahman the oblation, Brahman the fire, Brahman the offerer; Brahman alone is to be reached by one absorbed
in Brahman-action."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: 4.18 — "inaction in action" = seeing that the Self is **not** the doer even while the
body acts (3.27, 5.8–9); "action in inaction" = seeing that outward abstention while the mind seethes is
still action (3.6). 4.24: the *brahmārpaṇa* verse is the supreme statement of the *advaita-bhāvanā* — all
phases of action (means, act, agent, goal) are Brahman.
**Misreading:** that "inaction in action" means doing nothing (it means *akartṛ-bhāvanā*, the recognition of
non-doership while fully acting).
**Layers —** W: "The wise act fully yet are inwardly still — they see the Doer behind all doing." / S:
karma/akarma/vikarma; the brahmārpaṇa verse. / Sk: offer the act as Brahman (4.24). / A: Śaṅkara's
akartṛ-bhāvanā; the advaita-bhāvanā of 4.24.

## 4.25–4.33 — The catalogue of sacrifices
Twelve kinds of *yajña*: worship of gods (4.25), offering senses into restraint (4.26), sense-objects into
sense-fires (4.26), breath-control (*prāṇāyāma*, 4.29), charity/austerity/study (4.28), knowledge-sacrifice
(*jñāna-yajña*, 4.33 — "better than material sacrifice"). **4.33:** *śreyān dravyamayād yajñāj jñānayajñaḥ* —
"the sacrifice of knowledge is better than the material sacrifice; all action culminates in knowledge."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: all these *yajñas* are stages of *upāsana* (meditation) that purify the mind for
knowledge; *jñāna-yajña* is supreme because knowledge alone liberates.
**Layers —** W: "There are many ways to offer — but knowledge is the highest offering." / S: the 12 yajñas.
/ Sk: choose your form of daily offering. / A: Śaṅkara's upāsana-stages.

## 4.34–4.42 — The supremacy of knowledge
**4.34 — LANDMARK:** *tad viddhi praṇipātena paripraśnena sevayā* — "know that by **humble approach**
(*praṇipāta*), **inquiry** (*paripraśna*), and **service** (*sevā*); the seers of truth will impart
knowledge to you." **4.35–36:** knowing it, you will see all beings in the Self; even the greatest sinner
crosses over by the **raft of knowledge** (*jñāna-plavena*). **4.37 — LANDMARK:** *yathaidhāṃsi samiddho
'gnir bhasmasāt kurute* — "as a blazing fire turns kindling to ash, so the fire of knowledge burns all action
to ash." **4.38:** *na hi jñānena sadṛśaṃ pavitram iha vidyate* — "nothing in this world purifies like
knowledge; one perfected by yoga finds it within in time." **4.39–40:** the faithful, devoted, self-controlled
attain knowledge and supreme peace; the doubting perish — "neither this world nor the next nor happiness
exists for the doubter." **4.42:** "with the sword of knowledge (*jñānāsinā*), sever this doubt born of
ignorance in your heart — take up yoga, **arise**."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: 4.34 gives the **method** of knowledge: *praṇipāta* (surrender of pride to the
teacher), *paripraśna* (reasoned questioning — NOT blind faith), *sevā* (humble service that makes one fit
to receive). 4.37: knowledge burns the *saṃskāras* (karmic seeds) — both merit and demerit. 4.38: knowledge
is the supreme purifier because it alone removes *avidyā* (ignorance), the root of all impurity.
Rāmānuja: *jñāna* here is the knowledge of the Lord's nature and the devotee's dependence; the "sword" (4.42)
cuts the doubt of whether surrender works. Madhva: knowledge of the Lord's *vibhūtis* (excellences) fuels
*bhakti*.
**Cross-refs:** 2.52–53 (when understanding crosses delusion), 5.16 (knowledge as the sun).
**Layers —** W: "Approach a teacher with humility, ask, serve — and they will give you the knowledge that
purifies like fire." / S: praṇipāta/paripraśna/sevā; jñāna-agni. / Sk: find your teacher; ask genuinely. /
A: Śaṅkara (jñāna burns saṃskāras) vs the three schools on what *jñāna* means.
