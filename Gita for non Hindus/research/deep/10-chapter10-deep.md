# Deep Commentary — Chapter 10: Vibhūti Yoga (Divine Manifestations)

> *The catalogue of divine glories — "wherever you see excellence, there am I." Landmarks: 10.8, 10.20,
> 10.41–42.*

**At a glance.** Kṛṣṇa reveals that all excellence, power, beauty, and splendour in the universe are His
*vibhūti* (divine manifestation). He catalogues the greatest of each category — among mountains the Himālaya,
among rivers the Gangā, among weapons the thunderbolt — culminating in the principle: **"whatever is
glorious, prosperous, or mighty — know it as a spark of My splendour"** (*mama tejoṃśa-sambhavam*, 10.41).
The chapter bridges from philosophical knowledge (7–9) to the **revelation** (ch. 11).

## 10.1–10.7 — The power of knowing the vibhūtis
Kṛṣṇa: "neither gods nor sages know My origin — I am the origin of all" (10.2–3); "one who knows Me as the
unborn, beginningless, the great Lord of all worlds — **undeluded among mortals** (*muhyamāneṣu sarvabhūteṣu
avimuḍhaḥ*)" (10.3, [VERIFY]). The seeds of all qualities and dispositions come from Him.
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: knowing the *vibhūtis* (divine manifestations) is itself a means of meditation
(*upāsana*) — by seeing the Lord in every excellence, the mind is purified for the direct vision of ch. 11.

## 10.8–10.11 — The devotee's cycle of remembrance
**10.8:** *ahaṃ sarvasya prabhavo mattaḥ sarvaṃ pravartate / iti matvā bhajante māṃ budhā bhāva-samanvitāḥ*
— "I am the source of all; from Me all proceeds; knowing this, the **wise** (*budhāḥ*) worship Me with
devotion." **10.9:** their minds on Me, their lives surrendered, they enlighten one another and **ever speak
of Me** (*satataṃ kīrtayanto mām*), finding contentment and delight. **10.10–11:** "to the ever-devoted,
worshipping with love, I give the **yoga of understanding** (*buddhi-yoga*) by which they come to Me; out of
compassion, dwelling in their hearts, I destroy the darkness born of ignorance with the **lamp of knowledge**
(*jñāna-dīpena bhāsvatā*)."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: 10.10's *buddhi-yoga* = the Lord's gift of the discriminative understanding that
leads to Self-realisation; it is **grace** — He gives it **from within** (*ātma-bhāva-sthaḥ*, 10.11) by
dwelling in the devotee's heart. 10.8: the sequence is **knowledge → devotion** (*matvā bhajante*) — knowing
the source, one naturally worships. Rāmānuja: 10.10–11 is the supreme verse of **grace** — the Lord Himself
becomes the inner teacher (*guru*), destroying ignorance from within.
**Layers —** W: "To those who love Him, God gives the understanding that leads to Him — and personally
destroys their ignorance from within." / S: buddhi-yoga as grace; the jñāna-dīpa. / Sk: sense the inner lamp.
/ A: Śaṅkara (buddhi-yoga = discriminative grace) vs Rāmānuja (the Lord as inner guru).

## 10.12–10.18 — Arjuna's confirmation
Arjuna affirms: "You are the supreme Brahman, the supreme abode, the purifier, the eternal divine Person,
unborn, all-knowing — all sages declare this" (10.12–13). "You alone know Yourself; tell me more of Your
**vibhūtis** by which You pervade the worlds — how shall I meditate on You?" (10.17).

## 10.19–10.42 — The catalogue and its climax
Kṛṣṇa: "I shall tell you My chief manifestations — there is no end to My extent" (10.19). He lists: among
the **Ādityas**, Viṣṇu; among lights, the **Sun** (*ravi*); among **Maruts**, Śaṅkara [VERIFY]; among
**stars**, the Moon; among **Vedas**, the Sāmaveda; among **gods**, Indra; among **senses**, the mind; among
**living beings**, consciousness (*cetanā*); among **Rudras**, Śaṅkara; among **mountains**, Meru; among
**rivers**, **Bhāratī** (the Gangā/Sarasvatī); among **Vasiṣṭhas**, Bṛhaspati; among **generals**,
Skanda; among **waters**, the ocean; among **sages**, Bhṛgu; among **sounds**, Oṁ; among **sacrifices**,
*japa* (murmured repetition); among **immovables**, the Himālaya; among **trees**, the Aśvattha; among
**horses**, Uccaiḥśravas; among **elephants**, Airāvata; among **men**, the king; among **weapons**, the
thunderbolt (*vajra*); among **cows**, Kāmadhenu; among **causes of reproduction**, Kandarpa; among **serpents**,
Vāsuki; among **water-creatures**, Varuṇa; among **ancestors**, Aryamā; among **law-givers**, Yama; among
**demons**, Prahlāda; among **calculators**, time; among **beasts**, the lion; among **birds**, Garuḍa;
among **purifiers**, the wind; among **warriors**, Rāma; among **fish**, the crocodile; among **rivers**, the
Gaṅgā; among **creations**, the beginning and the end; among **sciences**, the science of the Self; among
**logicians**, the conclusive argument. And: "I am the **gambling** of the fraudulent, the **splendour** of
the splendid, the **victory** of the victorious, the **resolve** of the resolved, the **sattva** of the
good." (10.36.)
**10.41–10.42 — THE CLIMAX:** *yad yad vibhūtimat sattvaṃ śrīmad ūrjitam eva vā / tat tad evāvagaccha tvaṃ
mama tejoṃśa-sambhavam* — "whatever is glorious, prosperous, or mighty — know it as a **spark of My
splendour**." And: *athavā bahunaitena kiṃ jñātena tavārjuna / viṣṭabhyāham idaṃ kṛtsnam ekāṃśena sthito
jagat* — "what need to know all this? I **sustain this entire universe with a single fraction** of Myself."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: the *vibhūti* meditation trains the mind to see **every excellence as a theophany**
— a real manifestation of the divine. This *upāsana* (meditation on the qualified) purifies for the vision
of the unqualified. 10.42: the universe is sustained by a **fraction** (*ekāṃśena*) — the Lord's **total**
nature is infinitely beyond the cosmos. Rāmānuja: each vibhūti is a real *āveśa* (indwelling) of the Lord
in a specific form; the catalogue shows His **immanence**.
**Layers —** W: "Wherever you see greatness — in a mountain, a river, a hero, a star — there is a spark of
God. And all of this is just a fraction of Him." / S: vibhūti-upāsana; ekāṃśena. / Sk: find God in one
excellence daily. / A: Śaṅkara (theophany-meditation) vs Rāmānuja (real āveśa).
