# Deep Commentary — Chapter 11: Viśvarūpa-Darśana Yoga (The Cosmic Vision)

> *The most sublime and terrifying chapter — the revelation of the Cosmic Form. Landmarks: 11.12, 11.32,
> 11.54–55.*

**At a glance.** Arjuna asks to see Kṛṣṇa's divine form (11.1–4). Kṛṣṇa grants him **divine sight**
(*divyam cakṣuḥ*, 11.8) and reveals the **Viśvarūpa** — the cosmic form containing all beings, all times, all
powers (11.9–15). Arjuna is terrified (11.23–30); he hears "I am **Time**, the destroyer of worlds"
(*kālo'smi*, 11.32); he begs Kṛṣṇa to resume his gentle form (11.45–46). Kṛṣṇa withdraws and declares:
**only undivided devotion reveals this form** (11.53–55).

---

## 11.1–11.8 — Arjuna's request and the gift of divine sight
**11.4:** "if You think me able to bear it, show me Your **imperishable Self** (*avyayam… ātmanā*)." **11.5:**
"behold the hundreds and thousands of My forms, of various kinds, divine, of many colours and shapes."
**11.7:** "behold the **whole universe**, moving and unmoving, whatever else you wish to see — all here, in
My body (*gūḍhake*)." **11.8:** "but you cannot see Me with these eyes; I give you a **divine eye**
(*divyaṃ cakṣuḥ*)."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: the *divyam cakṣuḥ* is not a literal organ but the **spiritual capacity** to
perceive the divine — ordinary perception, bound to *prakṛti*, cannot apprehend the Supreme; the Lord must
**bestow** the faculty. Rāmānuja: the divine eye is the Lord's **grace** that enables the finite to behold
the infinite.

## 11.9–11.31 — The revelation
Sañjaya describes the vision: many mouths and eyes, many wonders, divine ornaments, celestial weapons,
garlands of heavenly fragrance; radiant like a thousand suns (*divi sūrya-sahasrasya bhaved yugapad
utthitā*, 11.12); the whole universe divided in many ways but resting in the body of the God of gods
(*deva-dehasya*, 11.13). Arjuna, overwhelmed: "I see all gods in You, all beings, Brahmā on the lotus-seat,
all sages and divine serpents" (11.15). "I see You, infinite on every side, with no beginning, middle or end"
(*anādi-madhya-antam*, 11.19) — with crown, mace, discus, a mass of radiance, **blazing everywhere**
(*sarvato 'pīśitum*, 11.17 [VERIFY]). Arjuna quivers: "my limbs tremble; seeing Your faces terrible with
teeth like fire, I am **dazed** and find no peace" (*dhṛtir vinaśṭā*, 11.24). He sees all warriors entering
Kṛṣṇa's **mouths** "like moths rushing into a blazing fire" (*bhāsvānalān*, 11.29) — the sons of Dhṛtarāṣṭra,
Bhīṣma, Droṇa, Karṇa, all the great warriors — being **devoured**.

## 11.32–11.34 — "I AM TIME"
**11.32 — LANDMARK (triṣṭubh meter):** *kālo'smi loka-kṣaya-kṛt pravṛddho / lokān samāhartum iha pravṛttaḥ /
ṛte'pi tvāṃ na bhaviṣyanti sarve / ye'vasthitāḥ pratyanīkeṣu yodhāḥ* — "I am **mighty Time** (*kālaḥ*), the
**destroyer of the world** (*loka-kṣaya-kṛt*), now engaged in **consuming the worlds** (*lokān samāhartum
iha pravṛttaḥ*); even without you (*ṛte'pi tvām*), **all these warriors** standing in the enemy ranks **will
not be** (*na bhaviṣyanti*)." **11.33:** "therefore **arise and win glory** (*uttiṣṭha yaśo labhasva*); by Me
they are **already slain** (*mayā hataḥ*); be but My instrument (*nimitta-mātraṃ bhava*), O Śavyasācin."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: *kāla* is here the **cosmic destructive aspect** of the Lord — not time-as-measure
but time-as-the-all-devouring power of dissolution (cf. 10.30, 10.33). The point: the warriors are **already**
destined to die; Arjuna's participation is providential, not causative. He is *nimitta-mātra* (a mere
instrument) — this **does not abolish his agency or responsibility** but places it in the context of a larger
cosmic necessity. Rāmānuja: the Lord as Time is the **agent** of the warriors' deaths; Arjuna should fight
without the **ego-burden** of being the killer. Madhva: the Lord's will is sovereign; Arjuna's duty is to
**cooperate** (*anugraha*). Abhinavagupta: Time is the rhythm of consciousness's self-expression — creation
and dissolution are its pulse.
**Cross-refs:** Oppenheimer's quoting of 11.32 at the Trinity test ("Now I am become Death, the destroyer of
worlds" — Ryder's translation of *kāla* as "Death").
**Misreading:** that 11.32–33 negate free will or excuse violence ("God did it"). The Gītā says Arjuna's
**action is real** but **not ultimate** — a middle ground between determinism and absolute autonomy.
**Layers —** W: "God said: I am Time itself, and these warriors are already in my jaws — so do your part
without the weight of thinking you are the ultimate cause." / S: kāla as cosmic destroyer; nimitta-mātra; the
theodicy. / Sk: release the burden of thinking everything depends on you. / A: Śaṅkara (kāla as dissolution
power; nimitta-mātra preserves agency) vs the free-will question; Oppenheimer.

## 11.35–11.50 — Arjuna's hymn and plea
Arjuna hymns the Lord: "You are the **first of gods** (*ādi-deva*), the ancient **Puruṣa**; You are the
**supreme resting-place** (*paraṃ nidhānam*) of this universe; You are the **knower and the known** and the
**supreme abode**; by You the world is pervaded" (11.37–38). "I rejoice in having seen what was never seen
before — yet my **mind trembles with fear** (*dhārayāmi kṣamatebhaṃ rūpaṃ tava*, [VERIFY 11.45])." "Let me
see that form of Yours with crown, mace, disc — **be gracious** (*prasīda*)." (11.46.)

## 11.51–11.55 — Only devotion reveals this form
**11.52–53:** "this form of Mine is **very hard to see** (*su-durdarśam*); even the gods ever yearn to see
it — neither by the **Vedas** (*vedaiḥ*), nor by **austerity**, nor by **charity**, nor by **ritual** can I
be seen in this form." **11.54 — LANDMARK:** *bhaktyā tv ananyayā śakya aham evaṃ-vidho 'rjuna / jñātuṃ
draṣṭuṃ ca tattvena praveṣṭuṃ ca paramtapa* — "only by **undivided devotion** (*ananyayā bhaktyā*) can I, as
I truly am, be **known, seen** (*draṣṭuṃ*), and **entered into**." **11.55:** *mat-karma-kṛn mat-paramo
mad-bhaktaḥ saṅga-varjitaḥ / nirvairaḥ sarva-bhūteṣu yaḥ sa mām eti pāṇḍava* — "who does My work, is devoted
to Me, free from attachment and enmity toward all beings — **that one comes to Me**."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: 11.54 is the Gītā's supreme statement on **bhakti** — not Vedic scholarship, not
austerity, but **undivided love** (*ananyayā bhaktyā*) reveals the Lord. This is *parā-bhakti* — devotion
that has matured into knowledge; the Lord is **seen** (*draṣṭum*) in the sense of direct realisation.
Rāmānuja: 11.54 is the charter-verse for **bhakti-yoga** as the supreme means; the *ananya-bhakti* (exclusive
devotion) surpasses all other paths. Madhva: the vision is the Lord's gift to His supreme devotee. 11.55
gives the **four marks** of the one who attains: (1) does God's work, (2) devoted to God, (3) free from
attachment, (4) free from enmity toward all.
**Cross-refs:** 9.34, 12.8–9, 18.55, 18.65–66.
**Layers —** W: "Only devotion — not scholarship, not austerity — lets you truly see the Divine. Be His,
serve all, hold no enmity — and you come to Him." / S: ananyā bhakti; the four marks (11.55). / Sk: release
one enmity as an act of devotion. / A: Śaṅkara (parā-bhakti = realisation) vs Rāmānuja (bhakti-yoga charter).

# Deep Commentary — Chapter 12: Bhakti Yoga (The Yoga of Devotion)

> *The shortest chapter — the essence of bhakti and the marks of the devotee. Landmarks: 12.2, 12.8–9,
> 12.13–19.*

**At a glance.** Arjuna asks (12.1): which is better — worshipping the **personal** (*saguṇa*) God with form
or the **impersonal** (*nirguṇa*) Absolute without form? Kṛṣṇa answers: the personal is easier for most
(12.2–7), but both reach Him. He then lists the **marks of the devotee** He holds dearest (12.13–19).

## 12.1–12.8 — Saguṇa vs Nirguṇa
**12.1** (Arjuna): those devoted to the personal form (*saguṇa*) and those to the impersonal, imperishable
(*nirguṇa, akṣara*) — which are more **established in yoga** (*yogatarāḥ*)? **12.2:** those who fix their
minds on Me, ever united, worshipping with supreme faith (*śraddhayā parayopetāḥ*) — I hold them **most
established** (*yukta-tamāḥ matāḥ*). **12.3–4:** but those who worship the imperishable, indefinable,
all-pervading, unimaginable, unchanging — **mastering the senses**, **equal to all**, devoted to the welfare
of all — they also attain Me. **12.5:** "for those whose minds are attached to the **unmanifest**
(*avyakta*), the path is **harder** (*klera-vataraḥ [VERIFY]*) — for embodied beings, the path to the
unmanifest is **difficult**." **12.6–7:** "but those who **surrender all actions to Me** (*mām upāśritya*),
with mind fixed on Me — I swiftly become their **deliverer** (*ud-dhartā*) from the ocean of death."
**12.8:** *mayy eva mana ādhatsva mayi buddhiṃ niveśaya / nivasiṣyasi mayy eva ata ūrdhvaṃ na saṃśayaḥ* —
"fix your mind and intellect on Me; you shall dwell in Me — no doubt."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: the *saguṇa* path (devotion to the personal form) is **superior in practice** for
the embodied soul because the mind needs an object; the *nirguṇa* path (the unmanifest) is the ultimate truth
but **harder** to sustain (12.5) because the mind cannot easily fix on the formless. Both reach the same
goal. Rāmānuja: 12.6–7 — *prapatti* (surrender to the personal Lord) is the supreme path; He **personally**
becomes the deliverer. Madhva: the *saguṇa* is easier and therefore commended; both are valid.
**Misreading:** that 12.5 disparages the *nirguṇa* path; it acknowledges its **difficulty**, not its
inferiority.
**Layers —** W: "God can be loved as a Person or contemplated as the formless — love is easier for most." /
S: saguṇa/nirguṇa; the difficulty of avyakta-upāsana. / Sk: choose the form that opens your heart. / A:
Śaṅkara (saguṇa is practice-superior; nirguṇa is truth-superior).

## 12.9–12.12 — Gradations of practice
If you cannot fix your mind, then **practise** (*abhyāsa*, 12.9); if you cannot practise, then **act for Me**
(*mad-arthaṃ karma*, 12.10) — even acting for My sake leads to perfection; if you cannot do that, then
**renounce the fruit of action** (*phala-tyāga*, 12.11) and be self-controlled. **12.12:** knowledge is
better than practice; meditation (*dhyāna*) better than knowledge; **renunciation of fruit** (*phala-tyāga*)
better than meditation — from renunciation, **peace** follows.
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: 12.9–12 are a **gradation of means** from easiest to hardest — the Gītā's pastoral
sensitivity to each person's capacity. Rāmānuja: each level is real; *phala-tyāga* is the universal baseline.

## 12.13–12.20 — The marks of the devotee
**12.13–19 — THE PORTRAIT:** *adroṣṭā sarva-bhūtānāṃ maitraḥ karuṇa eva ca / nirmamo nirahakāraḥ
sama-duḥkha-sukhaḥ kṣamī* — "hating none, friendly, compassionate; free from possessiveness and ego;
equal in pain and pleasure; **forgiving** (*kṣamī*)" (12.13). The marks cascade: **contented** (*santuṣṭaḥ*),
**self-controlled** (*yogī*), **firm in resolve** (*dṛḍhaniścayaḥ*); mind and intellect **surrendered to Me**
(*mayy arpita-mano-buddhiḥ*); one who **does not agitate the world** nor is agitated by it (*na udvijate
lokaḥ, lokān na udvijate*), free from joy/jealousy/fear/anxiety (*harsa-amarṣa-bhay-odvegaiḥ*); **without
desire** (*akiñcana*), **pure** (*śuci*), **indifferent** (*udāsīna*); **equipoised** (*sama*); who has
**renounced all undertakings** (*sarva-ārambha-parityāgī*); who **rejoices not, nor grieves, nor desires**
(*na hṛṣyati, na dveṣṭi, na śocati, na kāṅkṣati*); **without possessiveness** (*nirmamaḥ*), **filled with
devotion** (*bhaktimān*); **the same in friend and foe** (*samaḥ śatrau mitre ca*), in honour and dishonour,
in heat and cold, pleasure and pain; **free from attachment** (*saṅga-varjitaḥ*); **equal in praise and
censure** (*tulya-nindā-stutiḥ*), **silent** (*maunī*), **content with anything** (*santuṣṭo yena kenacit*),
**homeless** (*aniketaḥ*), **firm-minded** (*sthira-matiḥ*). "One who is **filled with faith** (*śraddhā*)
and **devoted to Me** (*bhaktimān*) — that one is **dear to Me** (*me priyaḥ*)."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: this portrait describes the **jñānī** — the one who has realised the Self — whose
character naturally bears these marks; the verse repeats "*me priyaḥ*" (dear to Me) because the Self-realised
sage IS the Lord's beloved. Rāmānuja: this is the **bhakta** — the marks are the fruit of devotion, not its
preconditions; anyone who cultivates devotion grows toward these. Madhva: the marks show the devotee's
excellence, which makes him fit for the Lord's grace.
**Cross-refs:** 2.55–72 (the *sthitaprajña* — parallel portrait), 13.7–11 (the marks of knowledge).
**Layers —** W: "The person God loves most is the one who harms no one, holds nothing, fears nothing — and
loves Him." / S: the 20+ marks; the sthitaprajña parallel. / Sk: cultivate one mark this week. / A: Śaṅkara
(the marks = jñānī) vs Rāmānuja (the marks = bhakta's fruit).
