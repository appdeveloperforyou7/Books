# Deep Commentary — Chapter 7: Jñāna-Vijñāna Yoga (Knowledge & Realisation)

> *The chapter where bhakti begins — God reveals His two natures and the four kinds of seekers. Sanskrit
> landmarks cross-checked against IITK Supersite. Any unverified Devanagari flagged [VERIFY].*

**At a glance.** Kṛṣṇa reveals His **higher** (conscious) and **lower** (material) natures (7.4–5), declares
Himself the source and thread of all (7.6–7), explains how **māyā** deludes all (7.13–14), categorises the
**four types of devotees** (7.16), and names the **wise devotee** as dearest (7.17–19). The chapter turns from
knowledge (*jñāna*) to realisation (*vijñāna*) and from there to devotion.

---

## 7.1–7.3 — The supreme knowledge
**7.1:** "hear how, practising yoga with mind fixed on Me, you will **know Me fully** (*mām asaktaṃ
vijānīyāḥ*)." **7.2:** this knowledge, joined with *vijñāna* (direct realisation), knowing which one attains
the supreme — "among thousands, hardly one strives for perfection; among the perfected, hardly one **knows Me
in truth** (*tattvataḥ*)." **7.3:** the rarity of true knowledge.
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: *jñāna* = the knowledge of the Self from scripture; *vijñāna* = its direct
realisation (*anubhava*); only the latter liberates. 7.2: knowing the Lord *tattvataḥ* (in truth, not
merely notionally) is rarer than perfection itself.
**Layers —** W: "True knowledge of the Divine is rare — but it is offered to you now." / S: jñāna vs
vijñāna. / Sk: is your knowledge conceptual or lived? / A: Śaṅkara's śāstra vs anubhava.

## 7.4–7.7 — The two natures and the source of all
**7.4–5:** My **lower** (*apara*) nature — earth, water, fire, air, ether, mind, intellect, ego (the eight,
7.4); My **higher** (*para*) nature — the *jīva-bhūta* (the life-principle / conscious soul) by which the
whole world is sustained (7.5). **7.6–7:** both originate from Me; "nothing is higher than Me (*mattaḥ
parataraṃ nānyat*); all is strung on Me as gems on a thread (*sūtre maṇi-gaṇā iva*)." **7.7 — LANDMARK.**
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: the *aparā prakṛti* (7.4) = matter; the *parā prakṛti* (7.5) = the *jīvas*
(individual souls as rays of the one consciousness); both are aspects of the Lord. "Gems on a thread" (7.7)
= all beings are sustained by the one Brahman which pervades without being modified.
Rāmānuja: the *parā prakṛti* is the totality of *cit* (conscious souls) which form the Lord's **body**
(*śarīra-śarīri*); the thread-image shows His support of all. Madhva: the two prakṛtis are the Lord's
creations; He is above both as the *sarvottama* (supreme above all).
**Layers —** W: "Everything is made of two things — matter and consciousness — and both come from the One
who holds all like a thread holds gems." / S: aparā/parā prakṛti; sūtre maṇi-gaṇā iva. / Sk: sense the
"thread" behind the gems. / A: Śaṅkara (jīvas as consciousness-rays) vs Rāmānuja (cit as Lord's body).

## 7.8–7.12 — "I am…" the essence of everything
Kṛṣṇa catalogues: the taste in water, the light of sun and moon, the syllable Oṁ in all scriptures, the
sound in ether, the **strength** of the strong (free from desire and fear), the **desire** (*kāma*) that is
not contrary to dharma (7.11) — all states of being (*sattva, rajas, tamas*) are from Him. (Cf. 9.16–19,
10.20–41.)
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: these identifications are not polytheism but the recognition that the Self/Brahman
is the **inner essence** of every phenomenon — every quality points back to its source.
**Layers —** W: "Every good quality you see — strength, light, taste — is a reflection of the Divine." / S:
the adhidaivata identifications. / Sk: trace one quality to its source. / A: Śaṅkara's essence-ontology.

## 7.13–7.15 — Deluded by māyā
**7.13:** "all beings in this world are deluded by the **three guṇas** (*tribhiḥ guṇa-mayaiḥ*); they do not
know Me as the supreme, imperishable — for **māyā** diverts them." **7.14 — LANDMARK:** *daivī hy eṣā
guṇa-mayī mama māyā duratyayā / mām eva ye prapadyante māyām etāṃ taranti te* — "this divine māyā of the guṇas
is **hard to cross** (*duratyayā*); those who take refuge in Me alone **cross over** it." **7.15:** the
deluded (*mūḍhāḥ*) — those of demonic nature (*āsurīṃ bhāvam*) — do not surrender; they are the lowest of
men, deprived of knowledge by māyā.
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: *māyā* is the divine power (*daivī*) by which the one appears as many — not
illusion in the cheap sense but the Lord's self-projecting power. The guṇas veil the Self. **Only surrender
to the Lord (as the Self) crosses māyā** — not mere effort or knowledge-in-the-abstract. Rāmānuja: *prapatti*
(surrender) is the means; the *guṇa-mayī māyā* is prakṛti's power over the soul. Madhva: the Lord's *māyā*
is real; crossing it requires His grace. Abhinavagupta: māyā is the **contraction** (*saṃkoca*) of
consciousness into limitation.
**Misreading:** that *māyā* means "everything is a hallucination" (a common misreading of Advaita). The Gītā
uses *māyā* as the **divine power of projection**, not as universal illusion — the world is *real* as
projection, unreal only as independent of Brahman.
**Cross-refs:** 4.6 (*ātma-māyayā*), 9.10, 11.32, 14.3–4, 18.61.
**Layers —** W: "A divine veil hides the truth from most — but those who turn to God pass through it." / S:
māyā as guṇa-mayī; duratyayā; prapatti as the means. / Sk: what is your "māyā" — what veil hides reality
from you? / A: Śaṅkara (māyā as self-projection, not illusion) vs Rāmānuja (prakṛti's power) vs
Abhinavagupta (saṃkoca).

## 7.16–7.19 — The four kinds of devotees; the wise one is dearest
**7.16:** *catur-vidhā bhajante māṃ janāḥ su-kṛtino 'rjuna / ārto jijñāsur arthārthī jñānī ca bharatarṣabha*
— "four kinds of virtuous people worship Me: the **distressed** (*ārtaḥ*), the **seeker of knowledge**
(*jijñāsuḥ*), the **seeker of wealth** (*arthārthī*), and the **wise** (*jñānī*)." **7.17:** the wise is
**dearest** (*priyaḥ*) — "ever united, devoted to the One, he is supremely dear to Me." **7.18:** all are
noble (*udārāḥ sarva evaite*), but the wise is like My very Self (*jñānī tv ātmaiva me mataḥ*) — "because his
mind is set on Me as the supreme goal." **7.19:** *bahūnāṃ janmanām ante jñānavān māṃ prapadyate* — "at the
**end of many births**, the wise one surrenders to Me, knowing 'Vāsudeva is all (*sarvam iti*)' — such a
great soul (*mahātmā*) is very rare (*su-durlabhaḥ*)."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: the four categories trace a spiritual arc — from distress-driven prayer to
curiosity to worldly aspiration to **pure wisdom**. The *jñānī* (7.17–19) is the one who has realised the
Self; his surrender (*prapadyate*, 7.19) is not emotional but the **knowledge** that "Vāsudeva is all."
"End of many births" = after long spiritual evolution. Rāmānuja: the *jñānī* is the supreme *bhakta* —
his "knowledge" is knowledge **of the Lord**, and his devotion is the highest. Madhva: the four types
reflect the soul's fitness; the *jñānī* knows the Lord's *vaiśiṣṭya* (supreme excellence) and loves Him.
**Cross-refs:** 9.13–15, 9.22, 10.10.
**Layers —** W: "Four kinds of people turn to God — the desperate, the curious, the ambitious, and the wise.
The last is rarest and dearest." / S: the catur-vidhā bhajana; jñānī as ātmaiva. / Sk: which of the four are
you — and which are you becoming? / A: Śaṅkara (jñānī = Self-realised) vs Rāmānuja (jñānī = supreme bhakta);
"Vāsudeva is all" across schools.

## 7.20–7.23 — Those who worship other gods
Those whose desires are **limited** (*hṛta-jñānāḥ*) worship other deities, constrained by their own *svabhāva*
— "I stead-fast the faith of each" (*yo yo yaḿ yaḿ tanuḿ bhaktah śraddhayārcchituṃ icchati, tasya tasyā
calāṃ śraddhāṃ vidadhāmy aham*, [VERIFY exact wording]) — so they attain the fruit they seek, but "finite is
that fruit" (*antavat tu phalaṃ teṣām*). The worshippers of the gods go to the gods; the worshippers of the
ancestors go to the ancestors. "But My worshippers come to Me."
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: the Lord, as the *antar-yāmin* (inner ruler), **strengthens** whatever faith a person
chooses — even misguided faith — so that the individual follows their own nature to its (limited) fruit. This
is not endorsement but **providence**: God does not block anyone's choice. Rāmānuja: finite fruits (*antavat*)
prove the inferiority of indirect worship. (Cf. 9.23–25.)
**Layers —** W: "Even limited faith leads somewhere — but only direct worship reaches the Supreme." / S:
hṛta-jñānāḥ; antavat phalam; the Lord steadying all faith. / Sk: respect others' faith journeys without
envying finite goals. / A: Śaṅkara on God as antar-yāmin of all faiths.

## 7.24–7.30 — "The deluded think I was born"
**7.24–25:** the deluded (*abhā-jānantīḥ* [VERIFY]) think the Lord comes under prakṛti's sway — they do not
know His **unborn** (*aja), **imperishable** (*avyaya) nature, the supreme person (*parama-bhāvam*). **7.26:**
"I know all past, present, and future beings — but **no one knows Me** (*mām tu na vetti kaścana*)."
**7.27–28:** all beings are born into delusion (*icchā-dveṣa-samutthena dvandva-mohena*) — but those whose
sins have ended (*anta-gataṃ pāpaṃ*), free from the delusion of dualities, worship Me with firm vows.
**7.29–30:** those who take refuge in Me, striving for liberation from old age and death — they know
*Brahman*, the *ādhyātma*, the *karma*; they know Me at the time of death in every mode.
**Commentary:** Śaṅkara: 7.26 — the Lord knows all, but the deluded do not know Him because *māyā* veils
them. 7.27: *icchā-dveṣa* (desire-aversion) is the engine of *saṃsāric* delusion; only the purified mind
(*anta-gata-pāpam*) breaks through. Rāmānuja: "no one knows Me" until the Lord **reveals** Himself (which
happens in ch. 11).
**Cross-refs:** 9.11 (fools despise the avatāra), 4.6–8, 11.
**Layers —** W: "God is hidden by our desires and aversions — only the purified mind truly knows Him." / S:
icchā-dveṣa; anta-gata-pāpam. / Sk: what desire or aversion veils the Divine from you? / A: Śaṅkara on māyā
as the veil; Rāmānuja on revelation (ch 11).
