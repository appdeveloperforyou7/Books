# 05 — Chapter-by-Chapter Guide (All 18 Chapters)

Chapter titles, verse counts, and themes are from the standard text. Key-verse citations follow
**chapter.verse** numbering. Translations of famous verses are paraphrased for sense; **exact wording must be
finalized against a chosen primary translation** `[VERIFY against e.g. Sargeant / Gambhirananda / Easwaran]`.

The three traditional thematic blocks (commentarial convention):
- **Ch 1–6 — Karma / the self (action & discipline)**
- **Ch 7–12 — Bhakti / the Divine (devotion & grace)**
- **Ch 13–18 — Jñāna / knowledge (discrimination & synthesis)**

---

## Chapter 1 — *Arjuna Viṣāda Yoga* (अर्जुनविषादयोग) — "The Yoga of Arjuna's Dejection" — 47 verses
- **What happens:** The armies assemble; conches sound; Arjuna asks Krishna to place the chariot between the
  armies. Seeing his kinsmen and teachers, Arjuna collapses — grief, trembling, dropped bow. He declares he
  would rather beg than kill them, and throws down his weapons.
- **Function:** Sets the **human problem** the whole Gita answers. Arjuna's *viṣāda* (sorrow/despair) is the
  universal spiritual crisis.
- **Key verses:** 1.28–29 (Arjuna's grief), 1.47 ("I shall not fight").
- **For readers:** Arjuna is not a coward — he is a good man frozen by conscience. Frame this with empathy.

## Chapter 2 — *Sāṃkhya Yoga* (सांख्ययोग) — "The Yoga of Knowledge" — 72 verses
- **What happens:** The Gita's intellectual heart. Krishna rebukes Arjuna's faintness, then delivers the
  **immortal-soul teaching** (ātman cannot be killed, 2.19–30). He introduces **karma yoga** (2.47–53),
  describes the qualities of the **sthitaprajña** (the person of steady wisdom, 2.54–72), and the mark of
  the liberated: serene, desire-free, "like the ocean unmoved by rivers."
- **Key verses:** **2.47** (the signature verse), 2.13, 2.20, 2.22, 2.48, 2.62–63 (the ladder of fall:
  thought→desire→anger→delusion→ruin), 2.70.
- **For readers:** If they read only one chapter, it should be this. It contains the Gita in microcosm.

## Chapter 3 — *Karma Yoga* (कर्मयोग) — "The Yoga of Action" — 43 verses
- **What happens:** Arjuna asks: if knowledge is superior, why push me to terrible action? Krishna explains
  the **two paths** (knowledge vs. action) and insists one **cannot avoid action** — even survival needs it.
  He teaches **yajña** (sacrifice) as the spirit of action, and the **yaśasvī** duty-model. He diagnoses
  **kāma** (lust/desire) and **krodha** (anger) as the true enemies, residing in the senses and mind.
- **Key verses:** 3.5, 3.19, 3.21 (leaders set the standard), 3.35, 3.37, 3.42–43.

## Chapter 4 — *Jñāna-Karma-Sannyāsa Yoga* — "The Yoga of Knowledge, Action & Renunciation" — 42 verses
- **What happens:** Krishna reveals the **avatāra doctrine** (4.5–8: he is born age after age to protect
  dharma). He teaches the secret of **actionless action** (one who sees inaction in action is wise), the
  **sacrifices** of knowledge, and ends: "the sword of knowledge, cut asunder this doubt born of ignorance"
  (4.34, 4.42).
- **Key verses:** 4.7–8 (avatāra verse), 4.34 (approach a teacher), 4.37–38 (knowledge's fire), 4.42.

## Chapter 5 — *Karma-Sannyāsa Yoga* — "The Yoga of Renunciation" — 29 verses
- **What happens:** Resolves karma yoga vs. sannyāsa: both lead to the same goal, but **karma yoga is easier
  / superior in practice** (5.2). Describes the liberated sage acting without "I-am-the-doer," seeing the
  same Self in all beings (5.18).
- **Key verses:** 5.10, 5.18, 5.22–23 (sense-joys are wombs of suffering).

## Chapter 6 — *Ātma-Saṃyama Yoga / Dhyāna Yoga* — "The Yoga of Meditation" — 47 verses
- **What happens:** Practical **meditation instructions** (seat, posture, focus). The mind as friend/enemy
  (6.5–6). Arjuna protests: "the mind is restless as the wind!" Krishna: "by practice (*abhyāsa*) and
  detachment (*vairāgya*) it is controlled" (6.35).
- **Key verses:** 6.5–6, 6.16–17 (moderation), 6.35, 6.47.

## Chapter 7 — *Jñāna-Vijñāna Yoga* — "The Yoga of Knowledge & Realization" — 30 verses
- **What happens:** Krishna begins the **bhakti** revelation: his nature as the source of all (7.6–7), the
  **four kinds of devotees** (the distressed, the seeker of wealth, the curious, the wise — the last best,
  7.16), and the difficulty of knowing God because **māyā** (divine illusion) deludes all (7.13–14, 7.25).
- **Key verses:** 7.7, 7.13–14, 7.16–19.

## Chapter 8 — *Akṣara-Brahma Yoga* — "The Yoga of the Imperishable Brahman" — 28 verses
- **What happens:** Definitions of Brahman, adhibhūta, adhidaiva, adhiyajña, and the **path of death**:
  dying with the mind on God at the right cosmic moment leads to liberation; the **two paths** of departure
  — *devayāna* (to liberation) and *pitṛyāṇa* (to return). The power of the last thought (8.5–6) and the
  **taṛaka** mantra (8.13 — "OM").
- **Key verses:** 8.5–7, 8.13 (OM), 8.16, 8.28.

## Chapter 9 — *Rāja-Vidya-Rāja-Guhya Yoga* — "The Yoga of Royal Knowledge & Royal Secret" — 34 verses
- **What happens:** The most intimate bhakti chapter. God pervades and sustains all yet is transcendently
  distinct (9.4–10). All worship reaches him (9.23–25). **The supreme promise (9.22, 9.26–34):** even a
  leaf, flower, fruit, or water offered with devotion is accepted; even the sinful, if they worship with
  wholehearted devotion, become righteous and soon attain peace.
- **Key verses:** 9.22, 9.26, 9.30–34.

## Chapter 10 — *Vibhūti Yoga* — "The Yoga of Divine Manifestations" — 42 verses
- **What happens:** Krishna catalogs his **vibhūtis** — divine glories/manifestations: among the great, he is
  the greatest of each (the sun among lights, the Ganges among rivers, the Himalaya among mountains, etc.).
  Teaches seeing God in all excellence (10.20, 10.41).
- **Key verses:** 10.20, 10.41, 10.32.

## Chapter 11 — *Viśvarūpa-Darśana Yoga* — "The Yoga of the Cosmic Vision" — 55 verses
- **What happens:** Arjuna asks to see Krishna's true cosmic form. Granted **divine sight**, he beholds the
  **Viśvarūpa** — the terrifying, all-containing, all-devouring cosmic manifestation: countless faces, eyes,
  weapons; all beings entering "like moths into a blazing fire" (11.29). Arjuna trembles, begs forgiveness
  for earlier familiarity, and asks Krishna to resume his gentle human form. Krishna does, and affirms the
  **unfathomable form is seen only by devotion** (11.53–55).
- **Key verses:** 11.12, 11.32 ("I am Time, the destroyer of worlds"), 11.54–55.
- **For readers:** This is the most sublime and the most terrifying chapter — read it carefully; the "I am
  Time" verse was quoted by Oppenheimer at the Trinity nuclear test.

## Chapter 12 — *Bhakti Yoga* (भक्तियोग) — "The Yoga of Devotion" — 20 verses (shortest)
- **What happens:** Arjuna: which is better — worshipping the personal God (*saguṇa*) or the impersonal
  Absolute (*nirguṇa*)? Krishna: the personal is easier for embodied beings, but both attain him. Lists the
  **qualities of the true devotee** (12.13–19): free from hatred and ego, compassionate, equanimous,
  contented, devoted — "very dear to me."
- **Key verses:** 12.8–11, 12.13–19.

## Chapter 13 — *Kṣetra-Kṣetrajña-Vibhāga Yoga* — "The Yoga of the Field & the Knower of the Field" — 34 verses
- **What happens:** The **Sāṃkhya** analysis: the body/world = the *field* (kṣetra); the conscious Self = the
  *knower of the field* (kṣetrajña). Distinguishes **prakṛti** (matter) from **puruṣa** (spirit). Describes
  the Supreme as the indwelling witness in all hearts. **Knowledge = knowing field, knower, and freedom.**
- **Key verses:** 13.1–2, 13.8–12 (true knowledge = humility, non-violence, etc.), 13.15–17, 13.22.

## Chapter 14 — *Guṇatraya-Vibhāga Yoga* — "The Yoga of the Three Gunas" — 27 verses
- **What happens:** How **prakṛti** binds the soul through the three **guṇas** — sattva (binds via
  attachment to happiness/knowledge), rajas (binds via action/passion), tamas (binds via delusion/laziness).
  The goal: become **guṇātīta** — transcending the three, unaffected by pain/pleasure, the same to friend
  and foe (14.22–26).
- **Key verses:** 14.5, 14.14–15, 14.19, 14.26.

## Chapter 15 — *Puruṣottama Yoga* — "The Yoga of the Supreme Person" — 20 verses
- **What happens:** The cosmic **Aśvattha** (banyan/peepul) tree — roots above, branches below — the saṃsāra
  tree to be cut by the axe of detachment. Then the Gita's boldest self-revelation: **two puruṣas** — the
  perishable (all beings) and the imperishable (the soul), and beyond both the **Puruṣottama** (Supreme
  Person), which Krishna himself is (15.16–19).
- **Key verses:** 15.1–3, 15.7, 15.15, 15.16–19.

## Chapter 16 — *Daivāsura-Sampad-Vibhāga Yoga* — "The Yoga of Divine & Demoniac Natures" — 24 verses
- **What happens:** Two fundamental types of human character: the **divine** (daivī) endowments — fearlessness,
  purity, non-violence, truth, compassion (16.1–3) — and the **demoniac** (āsurī) traits — hypocrisy,
  arrogance, greed, cruelty (16.4, 16.7–21). Warns against **lust, anger, greed** as "the threefold gate of
  hell" (16.21).
- **Key verses:** 16.1–3, 16.21, 16.24.

## Chapter 17 — *Śraddhātraya-Vibhāga Yoga* — "The Yoga of the Threefold Faith" — 28 verses
- **What happens:** Faith (*śraddhā*) and the nature of one's faith colors everything. Classifies **food,
  sacrifice, austerity, and gift-giving** according to the three gunas (sāttvic, rājasic, tāmasic). The
  meaning of the sacred syllable **"OM TAT SAT"** (17.23–27).
- **Key verses:** 17.3, 17.15 (right speech), 17.20 (right giving), 17.23.

## Chapter 18 — *Mokṣa-Sannyāsa Yoga* — "The Yoga of Liberation & Renunciation" — 78 verses (longest)
- **What happens:** The grand **synthesis and farewell**. Distinguishes true **tyāga** (renouncing the fruit
  of action) from mere **sannyāsa** (giving up action). Repeats the five factors of action (18.13–15).
  Explains how **knowledge, action, and doer** are each of three kinds (by guna). Krishna summarizes the
  whole teaching, then delivers the **final promise (18.65–66)**: "Fix your mind on me, be devoted to me,
  you will come to me... Abandon all dharmas and surrender to me; I will free you from all sins; grieve not."
  Arjuna's illusion is dispelled: **"I shall act according to your word"** (18.73). Sanjaya closes, blessing
  the Gita as supremely auspicious.
- **Key verses:** 18.61–62, 18.65, **18.66** (the carama-śloka, climactic verse), 18.73, 18.78.
