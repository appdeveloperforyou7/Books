# 07 — Glossary of Sanskrit/Hindi Terms (for the final book's back-matter)

Sorted alphabetically. Each entry: **IAST term (Devanagari)** — plain meaning, with a short expansion. This
is the **working glossary**; it will be edited, pruned, and re-sorted for the book. Entries marked **(core)**
are essential; **(see X)** cross-references.

---

**Abhyāsa (अभ्यास)** — repeated practice; one of the two means (with vairāgya) of controlling the mind (6.35).

**Adharma (अधर्म)** — the opposite of dharma; unrighteousness, disorder, violation of right order.

**Adhi**- (अधि) prefix — "pertaining to": *adhibhūta* (the material plane), *adhidaiva* (the divine/cosmic
principle), *adhiyajña* (the principle of sacrifice), *adhyātma* (the spiritual/self principle). See ch. 8.

**Ahaṃkāra (अहंकार)** — egoism, the "I-maker," the false sense of separate doership; a product of prakṛti.

**Āsana (आसन)** — a seated posture for meditation (in the Gita, a steady, comfortable seat — not the acrobatic
postures of modern studio yoga).

**Aśvattha (अश्वत्थ)** — the sacred fig/peepul tree; the cosmic "upside-down tree" of saṃsāra (15.1).

**Asura / āsurī (असुर / आसुरी)** — a demon; demoniac nature (greed, cruelty, hypocrisy) — opposite of daivī
(divine) (ch. 16).

**Ātman (आत्नन्)** — the Self; the immortal conscious principle within (core). See 03.

**Avatāra (अवतार)** — a "descent"/incarnation of the Divine (esp. Vishnu) to restore dharma (4.7–8).

**Bhagavān (भगवान्)** — "the Lord," the Divine Possessor of all auspicious attributes; the personal Supreme
(used throughout as a title for Krishna).

**Bhakti (भक्ति)** — loving devotion, surrender of the heart to God (core). See 04.

**Brahman (ब्रह्मन्)** — the impersonal Absolute, ultimate reality (core). (Distinct from **Brāhmaṇa** = a
priestly person; and **brahmacharya** = celibacy/student discipline.)

**Brahmacharya (ब्रह्मचर्य)** — continence, disciplined sense-control; the first of the four āśramas
(student stage of life).

**Caraṇa-śloka (चरमश्लोक)** — "the final verse" = 18.66, the Gita's climactic call to surrender.

**Dāna (दान)** — giving, charity/gift.

**Daivī / daivī-sampad (दैवी सम्पद्)** — divine endowment / divine wealth (virtues) (16.1–3).

**Dama (दम)** — control of the senses.

**Darśana (दर्शन)** — a "viewpoint," i.e. a philosophical system; the six classical Hindu darśanas (Nyāya,
Vaiśeṣika, Sāṃkhya, Yoga, Mīmāṃsā, Vedānta).

**Devayāna / Pitṛyāṇa (देवयान / पितृयान)** — the "path of the gods" (leading to liberation) and "path of the
ancestors" (leading back to rebirth) (8.24–26).

**Dharma (धर्म)** — duty, righteousness, cosmic/social order, the law of one's being (core). See 03.

**Dhyāna (ध्यान)** — meditation, sustained contemplation.

**Guṇa (गुण)** — a "strand/quality" of prakṛti: sattva, rajas, tamas (core). See 03.

**Guṇātīta (गुणातीत)** — one who has transcended the three gunas (14.22–26).

**Hṛī (ह्री)** — modesty, a sense of shame at wrongdoing.

**Īśvara (ईश्वर)** — the Lord, the Supreme personal God as ruler of the cosmos.

**Japa (जप)** — murmured repetition of a sacred name or mantra.

**Jīva / Jīvātman (जीव / जीवात्मन्)** — the individual embodied soul.

**Jñāna (ज्ञान)** — spiritual knowledge, liberating wisdom (core). See 04.

**Kāla (काल)** — time; in 11.32, the all-consuming cosmic Time ("I am Time").

**Kāma (काम)** — desire/lust, the craving for sense-pleasure; a root cause of bondage (3.37).

**Karma (कर्म)** — (a) action; (b) the moral law of cause-and-effect across lives (core). See 03.

**Karmayoga (कर्मयोग)** — the path of selfless, desireless action (core). See 04.

**Kṣatriya (क्षत्रिय)** — the warrior/ruler class (one of the four varṇas). See 08.

**Kṣetra / Kṣetrajña (क्षेत्र / क्षेत्रज्ञ)** — the field (body/world) / the knower of the field (the Self)
(ch. 13).

**Kuṇḍalinī (कुण्डलिनी)** — (not in the Gita directly) the latent spiritual energy of later tantric/yogic
systems — mention only if bridging.

**Māyā (माया)** — divine power of illusion/projection by which the One appears as many (7.14, 4.6).

**Mokṣa (मोक्ष)** — liberation from saṃsāra (core). See 03.

**Niṣkāma karma (निष्काम कर्म)** — desireless / disinterested action; action without craving for results.

**Oṃ / Praṇava (ओम् / प्रणव)** — the sacred syllable "OM," symbol of Brahman (8.13, 17.23).

**Para-prakṛti / Apara-prakṛti (परा/अपरा प्रकृति)** — Krishna's higher (conscious) and lower (material)
nature (7.4–5).

**Phala (फल)** — fruit, result/outcome of action; *phala-tyāga* = relinquishing the fruit.

**Prakṛti (प्रकृति)** — primordial matter/Nature, comprising the gunas (core). See 03.

**Prajāpati (प्रजापति)** — "Lord of creatures," a Vedic creator deity.

**Prāṇa (प्राण)** — vital breath/life-force.

**Puruṣa (पुरुष)** — spirit, pure consciousness, the witness (Sāṃkhya). See 03.

**Puruṣottama (पुरुषोत्तम)** — the Supreme Person, beyond the perishable and imperishable (15.17–19) = Krishna.

**Rāga (राग)** — attachment/attraction; with *dveṣa* (aversion), the twin impulses binding the soul.

**Rājas (रजस्)** — the guna of passion, activity, restlessness (core). See 03.

**Sāttvika (सात्त्विक)** — pertaining to sattva; pure, harmonious.

**Saguṇa / Nirguṇa (सगुण / निर्गुण)** — with-attributes (personal God) / without-attributes (impersonal
Absolute) (ch. 12).

**Saṃsāra (संसार)** — the cycle of birth, death, and rebirth (core). See 03.

**Sañjaya (संजय)** — the narrator who reports the Gita to king Dhṛtarāṣṭra.

**Sannyāsa (संन्यास)** — renunciation; formally, the fourth āśrama (life-stage) of total withdrawal.

**Sattva (सत्त्व)** — the guna of purity, light, harmony, goodness (core). See 03.

**Śāstra (शास्त्र)** — scripture, authoritative sacred teaching.

**Śraddhā (श्रद्धा)** — faith, reverent trust (ch. 17).

**Sthitaprajña (स्थितप्रज्ञ)** — "one of steady wisdom," the serene sage of 2.54–72.

**Svapna / Suṣupti** — dream / deep-sleep states (used to contrast with waking and the fourth, turīya).

**Svadharma (स्वधर्म)** — one's own personal duty, suited to one's nature and role (3.35). See 08/11.

**Svarga (स्वर्ग)** — heaven, a temporary celestial reward (not the same as mokṣa).

**Tamas (तमस्)** — the guna of inertia, dullness, ignorance (core). See 03.

**Tapas (तपस्)** — austerity, self-discipline, "heat" of spiritual effort (ch. 17).

**Tat tvam asi (तत् त्वम् असि)** — "That thou art" — the great Upanishad mahāvākya of identity of Self and
Brahman.

**Tyāga (त्याग)** — relinquishment (esp. of the fruit of action) (ch. 18).

**Vairāgya (वैराग्य)** — dispassion, detachment (with abhyāsa, controls the mind, 6.35).

**Varṇa (वर्ण)** — the fourfold social classification (brāhmaṇa, kṣatriya, vaiśya, śūdra). See 08/11.

**Vāsudeva (वासुdeva)** — a name of Krishna; "the indwelling divine in all."

**Vedānta (वेदान्त)** — "end of the Vedas" = the Upanishads and their philosophical tradition. See 08.

**Vibhūti (विभूति)** — a divine manifestation/glory of the Lord (ch. 10).

**Vijñāna (विज्ञान)** — realized/experiential knowledge (distinct from book knowledge, jñāna).

**Viśvarūpa (विश्वरूप)** — the "cosmic form" of the Lord (ch. 11).

**Yajña (यज्ञ)** — sacrifice, offering; in the Gita, all action offered selflessly becomes sacrifice.

**Yoga (योग)** — union; a disciplined path (core). See 04.

**Yogi (योगी)** — one who practices yoga; a spiritual practitioner.

**Yuga (युग)** — a cosmic age/world-cycle (kṛta, tretā, dvāpara, kali). The avatar verse (4.7) is "age after
age."
