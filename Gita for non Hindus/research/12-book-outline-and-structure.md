# 12 — Book Structure & Detailed Table of Contents

> *Final blueprint for **Bhagavad Gita for Non-Hindus**.*
> Built to occupy the market gap (file 14): **Easwaran-level accessibility + Patton/Schweig-level verse
> fidelity + an explicit non-Hindu cultural primer + honest treatment of the hard issues.**

---

## 1. The structural logic (why it is built this way)

Three reading journeys in one volume, so different readers get what they need:

- **The browser** reads the front-matter primer + chapter "Intro Cards" + Part III essays → high-level
  understanding in an afternoon.
- **The student** reads Part II chapter-by-chapter → faithful verse-level understanding.
- **The seeker** reads straight through → a guided, devotional-plus-intellectual journey.

This **dual-depth design** is the single biggest differentiator vs. Easwaran (too soft on rigor), Prabhupada
(too sectarian), and Hawley (no fidelity).

### Four structural commitments (from the reviews, file 14)
1. **Front-load a non-Hindu primer** so no verse stalls on background.
2. **Layer every chapter** — a high-level frame *then* verse deep-dives.
3. **Keep translation and commentary visually distinct** (Verse / Meaning / Commentary blocks).
4. **Plant hard-issue essays at the natural moments** they arise, never hidden.

---

## 2. Format conventions used throughout

- **Sanskrit handling:** every term on first use = **Devanagari (देवनागरी) + IAST (*iast*) + plain meaning**.
- **Verse block template** (Part II):
  ```
  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ 2.47  [verse number + chapter title]        │
  │ Devanagari verse                            │
  │ IAST transliteration                        │
  │ ── Literal translation ──                    │
  │ ── Plain-English meaning ──                  │
  │ ── Commentary (philosophical) ──             │
  │ ── Bridge for the non-Hindu reader ──        │
  │ ── What this is NOT (where relevant) ──      │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
  ```
- **Margin glossary:** recurring terms (dharma, karma, etc.) carry a one-line meaning in the page margin.
- **Callout boxes:** 🌉 *Bridge* (world parallels), ⚠ *Careful* (common misreadings), ❓ *Hard question*,
  📜 *Sanskrit note*, ✦ *Takeaway*.
- **Running visual aids:** the chariot diagram, the guṇa wheel, the two paths of death, the Aśvattha tree.

### PART I — BEFORE YOU BEGIN: The Orientation  *(~100 pp, ~26% of book)*
*Purpose: first EARN the reader (the manifesto), then give the non-Hindu reader the mental map before any
verse.*

**1. Why You — A Non-Hindu — Should Read the Gita** *(14 pp)* **← the manifesto, the book's hook.**
*(Source: file 19.)* The crisis of modern work/life the Gita solves; it's for householders, not monks; it
meets the mental-health epidemic; it integrates faith/reason/action; tools the West under-develops; cultural
literacy for a global century; it never asks you to convert; the proof — Emerson, Thoreau, Eliot, Huxley,
Jung, Oppenheimer, Gandhi→MLK. Honest caveats. The promise. **Free of Sanskrit until the end.**

**2. The Gita in Five Minutes** *(6 pp)* — what it is, where it sits, the dialogue, why it changed the world.
*(Source: file 01.)*

**3. The Story Around the Song** *(12 pp)* — the Mahābhārata, Kurukshetra, the dynastic feud, Arjuna's
collapse. Written as a short vivid narrative. *(Source: file 02.)*

**4. A Map of Hindu Thought** *(12 pp)* — Vedas/Upanishads/epics/Purāṇas; śruti vs smṛti; the four aims of
life (puruṣārthas); the four life-stages (āśramas); the Trimūrti and avatars; ritual and yajña. *(file 08.)*

**5. The Big Ideas in Plain English** *(20 pp)* — one entry each for **Dharma, Karma, Ātman, Brahman, Mokṣa,
Saṃsāra, the three Guṇas, Prakṛti/Puruṣa, Avatāra, Bhakti, Māyā.** Each: definition → where it appears → a
world parallel → "what it is not." *(file 03 + 07.)*

**6. The Four Paths (Yogas)** *(14 pp)* — Karma, Jñāna, Bhakti, Dhyāna; how they interrelate; "yoga is NOT
postures." *(file 04.)*

**7. How to Read a Battlefield** *(10 pp)* — literal vs allegorical; the Gita as inner drama; Gandhi's
*ahiṃsā* reading vs Tilak/Aurobindo's activism; sets up honest war treatment. *(files 09, 11.)*

**8. A First Taste: Six Verses to Live By** *(8 pp)* — a teaser of the most famous verses (2.47, 2.22, 4.7–8,
9.22, 11.32, 18.66) fully explained, so the reader feels the method before Part II. *(file 06.)*

### PART II — THE SONG, CHAPTER BY CHAPTER  *(~230 pp, ~60% of book)*
*The core. One book-chapter per Gita chapter (18). Consistent template per chapter:*

> **a. Intro Card** (chapter title in Devanagari + IAST + English; verse count; "What happens" in 3 lines).
> **b. The Big Picture** (~1–2 pp): the chapter's central idea, plain and memorable.
> **c. The Verses** — all verses *summarized* in flowing groups; **~8–12 key verses per chapter deep-dived**
> in the four-part Verse block. *(Total deep-dives ≈ 150–180 of the 700 — see §5.)*
> **d. Bridge & Application** — a modern, non-Indian parallel + a "how this lives today" paragraph.
> **e. Chapter Takeaway** (✦) — 2–3 sentences.
> **f. Hard-Question Callouts** planted where the text raises them (see §4).

## 5. The verse-scope decision (recommended)
**Summarize all 700; deep-dive ~150–180 (~22%).** Rationale: full verse-by-verse (Prabhupada/Yogananda scale)
overwhelms newcomers; deep-dive-only (Hawley) loses fidelity. Summarizing all 700 + deep-diving the load-bearing
ones keeps the book **~400 pp / ~130,000 words**, humane but complete. *(Confirm before writing starts.)*

## 6. Tone & voice (recommended)
**Warm-but-rigorous:** Easwaran's accessibility married to Schweig's/Johnson's discipline. Never preachy,
never dry. The author is a learned companion, not a guru. *(Confirm before writing starts.)*

## 7. Suggested production specs
- ~400 pages; hardcover + paperback + ebook.
- Sanskrit in a distinct serif; transliteration in italics; pull-quotes of famous verses as chapter epigraphs.
- 8–10 diagrams/illustrations (chariot, guṇa wheel, Aśvattha tree, two paths of death, varṇa/āśrama grid,
  timeline).
- Two-color interior for callout boxes; running margin glossary.
