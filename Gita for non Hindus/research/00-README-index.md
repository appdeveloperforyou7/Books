## How to use this folder

- All facts here are anchored to reputable scholarship (Wikipedia/Britannica as entry points; IEP;
- classical commentaries by Shankara, Ramanuja, Madhva; modern scholars such as van Buitenen, Fowler,
  Zaehner, Edgerton, Minor; Gandhi's and Tilak's readings).
- Anything that needs **verse-level verification before publishing** is flagged with `[VERIFY]`.
- Numerical facts (chapter count = 18, total verses = 700, chapter verse-counts) are confirmed against
  the standard critical benchmark (Adi Shankara's 700-verse standard).

## Key verified facts (quick reference)

- **Title:** *Bhagavad Gītā* (भगवद्गीता) = "Song of the Divine/God" (*bhagavat* = divine/lord; *gītā* = song).
- **Location in epic:** Chapters 23–40 of Book VI (*Bhīṣma Parvan*) of the *Mahābhārata*.
- **Form:** A dialogue, mostly between Prince **Arjuna** and his charioteer **Krishna** (avatar of Vishnu).
- **Size:** **18 chapters**, **700 verses** (ślokas). Standard fixed by Adi Shankara (c. 8th c. CE).
- **Date:** Generally **2nd–1st century BCE** (range 400 BCE – 2nd c. CE across scholars).
- **Language:** Epic-Puranic Sanskrit (post-Vedic, pre-classical).
- **Attribution:** Traditionally **Vyasa**; scholarship treats it as a composite work, integral to the epic.

## Editorial principles for the book (draft)

1. **Translate Sanskrit before using it.** Every Sanskrit term is given in (a) Devanagari, (b) IAST
   transliteration, (c) a plain-English meaning on first use.
2. **No assumed background.** Explain Hindu cosmology, varna, scripture, and ritual as if fresh.
3. **Respect plural readings.** Where Vedanta schools disagree (e.g., identity vs. difference of jīvātman
   and Brahman), present the spectrum, not one verdict.
4. **Verse + meaning + application.** Each explained verse gives literal meaning, philosophical meaning,
   and a real-life parallel a non-Indian reader can relate to.
5. **Glossary-first.** Terms used repeatedly are collected in a glossary; cross-referenced inline.
