## A. The signature verses

### 2.47 (★) — the most quoted verse in the Gita
- **karmaṇy-evādhikāras te mā phaleṣu kadācana |**
  **mā karma-phala-hetur bhūr mā te saṅgo 'stv akarmaṇi ||**
- *You have a right to action alone — never to its fruits. Let not the fruit of action be your motive, nor
  let your attachment be to inaction.*

### 2.48 (★) — equanimity defined
- **yoga-sthaḥ kuru karmāṇi ... samaṁ yoga ucyate**
- *Abide in yoga and perform action, abandoning attachment, the same in success and failure; this evenness
  of mind is called yoga.*

### 2.22 (★) — reincarnation image
- **vāsāṁsi jīrṇāni yathā vihāya ...**
- *As a person casts off worn-out clothes and puts on new ones, so the embodied soul casts off worn-out
  bodies and enters new ones.*

### 2.20 (★) — immortality of the Self
- **na jāyate mriyate vā kadācin ...**
- *It (the Self) is never born and never dies; unborn, eternal, ever-existing, primeval — it is not slain
  when the body is slain.*

### 2.62–63 (⚠) — the ladder of spiritual ruin
- *Contemplating sense-objects → attachment → desire → anger → delusion → loss of memory → loss of
  intellect → destruction.* (The psychology of how a good person falls.)

## B. Action & duty

### 3.35 / 18.47 (⚠ — often misread about caste)
- **śreyān sva-dharmo viguṇaḥ para-dharmāt sv-anuṣṭhitāt**
- *Better one's own dharma though imperfectly performed than the dharma of another well performed; better to
  die in one's own dharma — another's is fraught with danger.* → **See file 11 for the sensitive caste
  discussion.**

### 3.19 — act without attachment
- *Therefore, without attachment, always perform the action that must be done; by acting without attachment,
  one attains the Supreme.*

## C. The Self & knowledge

### 6.5–6 (★) — the mind as friend/enemy
- *Let one lift the self by the Self, and not let the self sink; for the Self alone is the friend of the
  self, and the Self alone is the enemy of the self.*

### 5.18 (★) — the sage's equal vision
- *The wise see the same Self in a learned and gentle brahmin, a cow, an elephant, a dog, and an outcast
  (caṇḍāla).*

### 13.8–12 — what knowledge really is
- Humility, non-violence, patience, uprightness, service, purity, steadfastness, self-control, dispassion
  toward sense-objects, absence of ego — *this is knowledge; what is other than this is ignorance.*

## D. The avatar & the Divine

### 4.7–8 (★🙏)
- **yadā yadā hi dharmasya glānir bhavati bhārata ...**
- *Whenever there is a decline of dharma and a rise of adharma, I manifest myself. To protect the good and
  destroy evil-doers, and to re-establish dharma, I take birth, age after age.*

### 10.20 — God as the inmost Self
- *I am the Self, O Arjuna, seated in the hearts of all beings; I am the beginning, the middle, and the end
  of all beings.*

### 11.32 (⚠ — Oppenheimer's verse)
- **kālo 'smi loka-kṣaya-kṛt pravṛddhaḥ ...**
- *I am Time, the destroyer of worlds, grown mighty, now engaged in consuming the worlds.*

## E. Devotion & grace (climactic)

### 9.22 — the promise to the devotee
- *To those who worship me, thinking of me alone, ever united, I bring full security and supply what they
  lack and preserve what they have.*

### 9.26 (🙏)
- *If one offers me with devotion a leaf, a flower, a fruit, or water, I accept that devoted offering.*

### 9.30–34 (🙏) — grace for the fallen
- *Even if a person of very evil conduct worships me with single-minded devotion, that one is to be
  considered righteous... soon such a one becomes righteous and attains eternal peace; know for certain that
  my devotee is never lost.*

### 18.66 (★🙏) — the carama-śloka, final word of the Gita's teaching
- **sarva-dharmān parityajya mām ekaṁ śaraṇaṁ vraja |**
  **ahaṁ tvā sarva-pāpebhyo mokṣayiṣyāmi mā śucaḥ ||**
- *Abandoning all dharmas, take refuge in me alone; I will liberate you from all sins; do not grieve.*

### 18.78 — Sanjaya's closing benediction
- **yatra yogeśvaraḥ kṛṣṇo ... tatra śrīr vijayo bhūtir dhruvā nītiḥ matir mama**
- *Wherever there is Krishna the Lord of yoga, and Arjuna the archer, there are fortune, victory, prosperity,
  and unfailing justice — such is my conviction.*

## F. Mantric / technical verses

### 8.13 — OM (Oṃkāra)
- *Whoever, departing the body, remembers me alone at the time of death, attains my state... and the one
  syllable "OM" (praṇava) is my symbol.*

### 17.23 — OM TAT SAT
- *Brahman is "OM"; "TAT" ("That") and "SAT" ("Truth/Being") — by this threefold designation Brahman, the
  Vedas, and sacrifice were made of old.*

## Editorial note
Each of these verses deserves, in the final book, a **four-part treatment**:
1. Sanskrit (Devanagari) + IAST transliteration + word-meanings;
2. a clear, literal translation;
3. a plain-English philosophical explanation (what it actually says, stripped of jargon);
4. a bridge/analogy for the non-Hindu reader, and a "what it is NOT" caution against common misreadings.
