9.2
राजविद्या राजगुह्यं पवित्रमिदमुत्तमम् ।
प्रत्यक्षावगमं धर्म्यं सुसुखं कर्तुमव्ययम् ॥
Prāja-vidyā rāja-guhyam pavitram idam uttamam / pratyakṣāvagamaṃ dharmyaṃ su-sukhaṃ kartum avyayam
LThe king of sciences, the king of secrets — purest, supreme, directly realised, righteous, joyful to practise, imperishable.
MThis knowledge is the highest of all: it purifies, is directly experienced, easy to live, and once grasped, never fades.
9.3
अश्रद्दधानाः पुरुषा धर्मस्यास्य परन्तप ।
अप्राप्य मां निवर्तन्ते मृत्युसंसारवर्त्मनि ॥
Paśraddadhānāḥ puruṣā dharmasyāsya parantapa / aprāpya māṃ nivartante mṛtyu-saṃsāra-vartmani
LThose without faith in this dharma, failing to reach Me, return to the path of death and rebirth.
MWithout trust in the teaching, no progress is possible; the sceptic stays bound to the round of birth and death.
9.4
मया ततमिदं सर्वं जगदव्यक्तमूर्तिना ।
मत्स्थानि सर्वभूतानि न चाहं तेष्ववस्थितः ॥
Pmayā tatam idaṃ sarvaṃ jagad avyakta-mūrtinā / mat-sthāni sarva-bhūtāni na cāhaṃ teṣv avasthitaḥ
LBy My unmanifest form all this universe is pervaded; all beings dwell in Me — yet I dwell not in them.
MGod holds all things within himself, yet is not contained by them — present everywhere, trapped nowhere.
9.5
न च मत्स्थानि भूतानि पश्य मे योगमैश्वरम् ।
भूतभृन्न च भूतस्थो ममात्मा भूतभावनः ॥
Pna ca mat-sthāni bhūtāni paśya me yogam aiśvaram / bhūta-bhṛn na ca bhūta-stho mamātmā bhūta-bhāvanaḥ
LYet beings are not really in Me — behold My divine yoga: I sustain all without dwelling in them; My Self originates all beings.
MA paradox of intimacy: he sustains every being without being lodged inside any of them.
9.6
यथाकाशस्थितो नित्यं वायुः सर्वत्रगो महान् ।
तथा सर्वाणि भूतानि मत्स्थानीत्युपधारय ॥
Pyathā ākāśa-sthito nityaṃ vāyuḥ sarvatrago mahān / tathā sarvāṇi bhūtāni mat-sthānīty upadhāraya
LAs the great wind, ever moving, rests in space — understand that all beings rest in Me.
MLike wind moving within space — everything moves within him, dependent on him, while he remains free.
9.7
सर्वभूतानि कौन्तेय प्रकृतिं यान्ति मामिकाम् ।
कल्पक्षये पुनस्तानि कल्पादौ विसृजाम्यहम् ॥
Psarva-bhūtāni kaunteya prakṛtiṃ yānti māmikām / kalpa-kṣaye punas tāni kalpādau visṛjāmy aham
LAt a cosmic age's end all beings merge into My nature; at the new age's dawn I emit them again.
MAt each cosmic cycle's end all beings dissolve into nature; at the next dawn he sends them forth again.
9.8
प्रकृतिं स्वामवष्टभ्य विसृजामि पुनः पुनः ।
भूतग्राममिमं कृत्स्नमवशं प्रकृतेर्वशात् ॥
Pprakṛtiṃ svām avaṣṭabhya visṛjāmi punaḥ punaḥ / bhūta-grāmam imaṃ kṛtsnam avaśaṃ prakṛter vaśāt
LTaking hold of My own nature, again and again I project this whole multitude of beings, helpless under nature's sway.
MHe is the quiet power behind creation's rhythm, projecting all beings — yet he himself remains unaffected.
9.9
न च मां तानि कर्माणि निबध्नन्ति धनञ्जय ।
उदासीनवदासीनमसक्तं तेषु कर्मसु ॥
Pna ca māṃ tāni karmāṇi nibadhnanti dhanañjaya / udāsīna-vad āsīnam asaktaṃ teṣu karmasu
LThese actions bind Me not, O Arjuna — I sit as one indifferent, unattached to those acts.
MThough all action flows from him, he is never bound by it: indifferent, unattached, free.
9.10How the world runs — God as the silent overseer
मयाध्यक्षेण प्रकृतिः सूयते सचराचरम् ।
हेतुनानेन कौन्तेय जगद्विपरिवर्तते ॥
mayādhyakṣeṇa prakṛtiḥ sūyate sacarācaram / hetunānena kaunteya jagad viparyavartate
GĪTĀ 9.10
LLiteral

Under Me as overseer, Nature (prakṛti) brings forth the moving and the unmoving; by this cause, O Arjuna, the universe revolves.

MIn plain English

The whole world is not running itself, and it is not running you. There is an intelligence behind it — not pushing from outside like a mechanic, but pervading from within like the stillness at the centre of a wheel. Everything that grows, moves, and changes is nature doing its work; but nature itself is watched over by something that does not change.

CCommentary

This single verse quietly dissolves two errors at once. It rejects the idea of a remote, absentee God (he is adhyakṣa, “the overseer,” right here) and the idea that the material world is itself divine (prakṛti does the producing; he supervises it).

For the new reader

Think of a great conductor. The musicians make every sound; the conductor makes none. Yet without the conductor’s silent presence, the music falls apart. Krishna is not a note in the symphony — he is what holds the symphony together.

Try this — watch for the “overseer”

Today, pick one ordinary process — a plant growing, your breath, the turning of the day — and instead of seeing only the surface, sense the still intelligence within it. The practice of Chapter 9 begins here: in recognizing that you are watched over, not in earning it.

Going deeper — roots & commentaries

prakṛti (प्रकृति) = primordial nature; adhyakṣa (अध्यक्ष) = “over-seer,” from adhi + akṣa (eye). The Sāṃkhya schools read this as pure dualism (matter vs. spirit); the bhakti schools read Krishna himself as the adhyakṣa — the hinge where impersonal philosophy turns personal.
