9.11
अवजानन्ति मां मूढा मानुषीं तनुमाश्रितम् ।
परं भावमजानन्तो मम भूतमहेश्वरम् ॥
Pavajānanti māṃ mūḍhā mānuṣīṃ tanum āśritam / paraṃ bhāvam ajānanto mama bhūta-maheśvaram
LFools despise Me in My human body, not knowing My supreme nature as the great Lord of all beings.
MThose who see only a human body miss the divinity standing right before them.
9.12
मोघाशा मोघकर्माणो मोघज्ञाना विचेतसः ।
राक्षसीमासुरीं चैव प्रकृतिं मोहिनीं श्रिताः ॥
Pmoghāśā mogha-karmāṇo mogha-jñānā vicetasaḥ / rākṣasīm āsurīṃ caiva prakṛtiṃ mohinīṃ śritāḥ
LTheir hopes, acts and knowledge are vain; bewildered, they cling to the delusive demoniac nature.
MDeluded minds chase empty hopes and end in darkness, deceived by their own restless nature.
9.13
महात्मानस्तु मां पार्थ दैवीं प्रकृतिमाश्रिताः ।
भजन्त्यनन्यमनसो ज्ञात्वा भूतादिमव्ययम् ॥
Pmahātmānas tu māṃ pārtha daivīṃ prakṛtim āśritāḥ / bhajanty ananya-manaso jñātvā bhūta-ādim avyayam
LBut the great souls, grounded in the divine nature, worship Me with undivided mind, knowing Me as the imperishable source.
MThe truly great turn wholly toward the Divine, knowing him as the deathless source of all that exists.
9.14
सततं कीर्तयन्तो मां यतन्तश्च दृढव्रताः ।
नमस्यन्तश्च मां भक्त्या नित्ययुक्ता उपासते ॥
Psatataṃ kīrtayanto māṃ yatantaś ca dṛḍha-vratāḥ / namasyantaś ca māṃ bhaktyā nitya-yuktā upāsate
LEver chanting My glories, striving with firm vows, bowing in devotion, they worship Me, ever united.
MTheir lives become one continuous act of remembrance — chanting, striving, bowing, devoted.
9.15
ज्ञानयज्ञेन चाप्यन्ये यजन्तो मामुपासते ।
एकत्वेन पृथक्त्वेन बहुधा विश्वतोमुखम् ॥
Pjñāna-yajñena cāpy anye yajanto mām upāsate / ekatvena pṛthaktvena bahudhā viśvato-mukham
LOthers worship Me by the sacrifice of knowledge — as the One, as the many, in diverse ways, the all-faced universal Being.
MSeekers approach the One in many ways — as the single Reality, as the many, as the all-pervading whole.
9.16
अहं क्रतुरहं यज्ञः स्वधाहमहमौषधम् ।
मन्त्रोऽहमहमेवाज्यमहमग्निरहं हुतम् ॥
Pahaṃ kratur ahaṃ yajñaḥ svadhāham aham auṣadham / mantro'ham aham evājyam aham agnir ahaṃ hutam
LI am the rite, the sacrifice, the offering to the ancestors, the healing herb, the mantra, the clarified butter, the fire and the oblation.
MEvery element of worship — the rite, the fire, the offering, the very words — is himself.
9.17
पिताहमस्य जगतो माता धाता पितामहः ।
वेद्यं पवित्रमोंकार ऋक् साम यजुरेव च ॥
Ppitāham asya jagato mātā dhātā pitāmahaḥ / vedyaṃ pavitram oṁkāra ṛk sāma yajur eva ca
LFather, mother, sustainer, grandsire of this world; the knowable, the purifier, the syllable Oṁ, the Ṛg, Sāma and Yajur Veda.
MHe is the parent, support and meaning of the whole world, and the sound Oṁ that names it.
9.18
गतिर्भर्ता प्रभुः साक्षी निवासः शरणं सुहृत् ।
प्रभवः प्रलयः स्थानं निधानं बीजमव्ययम् ॥
Pgatir bhartā prabhuḥ sākṣī nivāsaḥ śaraṇaṃ suhṛt / prabhavaḥ pralayaḥ sthānaṃ nidhānaṃ bījam avyayam
LGoal, supporter, Lord, witness, abode, refuge, friend; origin, dissolution, ground, resting-place and the imperishable seed.
MWhatever you seek — a refuge, a friend, a foundation, the seed of all things — he is that.
9.19
तपाम्यहमहं वर्षं निगृह्णाम्युत्सृजामि च ।
अमृतं चैव मृत्युश्च सदसच्चाहमर्जुन ॥
Ptapāmy aham ahaṃ varṣaṃ nigṛhṇāmy utsṛjāmi ca / amṛtaṃ caiva mṛtyuś ca sad asac cāham arjuna
LI send forth heat; I withhold and release the rain; I am immortality and death, O Arjuna — both being and non-being.
MHe is the weather of existence itself: the rain and heat, life and death, being and not-being.
9.20
त्रैविद्या मां सोमपाः पूतपापा
यज्ञैरिष्ट्वा स्वर्गतिं प्रार्थयन्ते ।
ते पुण्यमासाद्य सुरेन्द्रलोकमश्नन्ति दिव्यान्दिवि देवभोगान् ॥
Ptraividya māṃ somapāḥ pūta-pāpā yajñair iṣṭvā svarga-gatiṃ prārthayante / te puṇyam āsādya surendra-lokam aśnanti divyān divi deva-bhogān
LKnowers of the three Vedas, cleansed of sin, worship Me by sacrifice and seek heaven; attaining merit, they enjoy the delights of Indra's world.
MRitual-seekers reach a temporary heaven as a reward and enjoy its pleasures while they last.
9.21
ते तं भुक्त्वा स्वर्गलोकं विशालं
क्षीणे पुण्ये मर्त्यलोकं विशन्ति ।
एवं त्रयीधर्ममनुप्रपन्ना गतागतं कामकामा लभन्ते ॥
Pte taṃ bhuktvā svarga-lokaṃ viśālaṃ kṣīṇe puṇye martya-lokaṃ viśanti / evaṃ trayī-dharma anuprapannā gatāgataṃ kāma-kāmā labhante
LHaving enjoyed that vast heaven, when merit is exhausted they fall back to the mortal world — so desire-driven Vedic worshippers gain only round-trip coming and going.
MBut when the merit runs out they fall back to earth — desire-driven worship earns only round-trips, never freedom.
