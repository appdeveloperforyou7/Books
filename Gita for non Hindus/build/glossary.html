GlossarySanskrit & key terms — with chapter references
Abhyāsa अभ्यास
Repeated practice; with vairāgya, the means of steadying the mind (6.35).
Ahaṃkāra अहंकार
The "I-maker," the false sense of separate doership; a product of prakṛti.
Āsana आसन
A steady seated posture for meditation — not the acrobatic postures of modern yoga.
Ātman आत्मन्
The Self: the unchanging, immortal conscious witness within all beings.
Avatāra अवतार
A "descent" of the Divine (esp. Viṣṇu) to restore dharma (4.7–8).
Bhagavān भगवान्
"The Lord," the personal Supreme; a title for Kṛṣṇa throughout the Gītā.
Bhakti भक्ति
Loving devotion; surrender of the heart to God — the path of chapters 7–12.
Brahman ब्रह्मन्
The impersonal Absolute, the infinite conscious ground of all reality.
Caraṇa-śloka चरमश्लोक
"The final verse" — 18.66, the Gītā's climactic call to surrender.
Dharma धर्म
Duty, righteousness, the right order of things and the law of one's own being.
Dhyāna ध्यान
Meditation, sustained contemplation that stills the mind (ch. 6).
Guṇa गुण
A "strand" of nature: sattva, rajas, or tamas (ch. 14).
Guṇātīta गुणातीत
One who has transcended the three guṇas (14.22–26).
Jñāna ज्ञान
Spiritual knowledge; the path of discerning the real from the unreal.
Karma कर्म
Action; and the moral law of cause and effect that binds the soul across lives.
Karmayoga कर्मयोग
The path of selfless, desireless action — the Gītā's signature teaching.
Kṣetra / Kṣetrajña क्षेत्र/क्षेत्रज्ञ
The field (body/world) and the knower of the field (the Self) — ch. 13.
Māyā माया
The divine power by which the One appears as many; the source of illusion (7.14).
Mokṣa मोक्ष
Liberation: freedom from saṃsāra, the cycle of rebirth.
Niṣkāma karma निष्काम कर्म
"Desireless action" — acting without craving for the result (2.47).
Oṃ (Praṇava) ॐ
The sacred syllable that is the sound-symbol of Brahman (8.13).
Prakṛti प्रकृति
Primordial Nature/Matter, comprising the guṇas; all that changes.
Puruṣa पुरुष
Spirit, pure consciousness, the unchanging witness (Sāṃkhya).
Puruṣottama पुरुषोत्तम
The Supreme Person, beyond the perishable and imperishable (15.17–19).
Rajas रजस्
The guṇa of passion, activity and restlessness.
Saguṇa / Nirguṇa सगुण/निर्गुण
God "with attributes" (personal) / "without attributes" (impersonal) — ch. 12.
Saṃsāra संसार
The cycle of birth, death and rebirth driven by karma and desire.
Sannyāsa संन्यास
Renunciation; formally, the life-stage of total withdrawal.
Sattva सत्त्व
The guṇa of purity, clarity, harmony and goodness.
Śraddhā श्रद्धा
Faith, reverent trust — the substance of one's inner nature (ch. 17).
Sthitaprajña स्थितप्रज्ञ
"One of steady wisdom," the serene sage described in 2.54–72.
Svadharma स्वधर्म
One's own duty, suited to one's nature and role (3.35).
Tamas तमस्
The guṇa of inertia, dullness and ignorance.
Tat tvam asi तत् त्वम् असि
"That thou art" — the Upaniṣad maxim of the identity of Self and Brahman.
Tyāga त्याग
Relinquishment, especially of the fruit of action (ch. 18).
Vairāgya वैराग्य
Dispassion, detachment; with abhyāsa, steadies the mind (6.35).
Varṇa वर्ण
The fourfold social classification (4.13); treated critically in this book.
Vedānta वेदान्त
"The end of the Vedas" — the Upaniṣads and their philosophical tradition.
Yajña यज्ञ
Sacrifice/offer; in the Gītā, any selfless act offered to the Divine.
Yoga योग
Union with the Divine; a disciplined path; also, equanimity and skill in action.
Yuga युग
A cosmic age/world-cycle; the avatāra appears "age after age" (4.7).
