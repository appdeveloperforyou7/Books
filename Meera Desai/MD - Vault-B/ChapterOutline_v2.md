### THE AFTERMATH (Chapters 31–34)

---

### Chapter 31 — The Photographs
**POV:** Meera | **Day:** 9 (pre-dawn) | **Location:** Meera's hotel room

Meera processes what she saw in the repository — fourteen minutes that confirmed 2,300 years of guardianship. She reviews the photographs she took: the Ashoka Chronicle, the birch-bark manuscripts, the copper cylinders, the Indus Valley documents that made Priya weep. The images are grainy, shot in haste, but they're proof. She sends encrypted copies to her personal cloud — a dead-man's switch she hasn't told anyone about.

**Cliffhanger:** Her phone rings. Rathore. "Mehra made it back to Rothwell. And Rothwell just made a phone call to his legal team in London. Whatever he's planning, it's starting now."

---

### Chapter 32 — The Report
**POV:** Rathore | **Day:** 9 (morning) | **Location:** Liaison office

Rathore files his official report to RAW — carefully edited to acknowledge the mechanism activation without revealing the full scope of what was found. He describes "a sealed subterranean chamber of archaeological significance" and recommends a full government assessment. Within two hours, Delhi responds: a delegation is en route. The RAW director, the ASI director-general, and a representative from the Prime Minister's office. Rathore reads between the lines: they're coming to take control.

**Cliffhanger:** His deputy hands him an intercept: Rothwell's Geneva dealer, Yuki Tanaka, has filed a preliminary inquiry with the International Court of Justice regarding "cultural heritage of universal significance in the custody of the Indian state." Legal maneuvering has begun.

---

### Chapter 33 — The Delegation
**POV:** Meera / Rathore | **Day:** 9 (afternoon) | **Location:** Temple complex

The government delegation arrives with the efficiency of a military operation. The RAW director is cold and professional; the ASI director-general is barely containing scholarly excitement; the PM's representative — a woman identified only as Mrs. Kapoor — has the particular authority of someone who speaks for the Prime Minister. They descend into the repository with Meera's photographs as their guide, and their reactions range from awe (ASI) to greed (RAW) to political calculation (Kapoor).

**Cliffhanger:** The RAW director emerges from the repository and says, with the calm of a man who has already decided: "This is a matter of national security. Everything — the mechanism, the chamber, the manuscripts — is classified effective immediately. Agent Desai, your consultancy is concluded."

---

### Chapter 34 — Sidelined
**POV:** Meera | **Day:** 9 (evening) | **Location:** Meera's hotel room

Meera is formally thanked and dismissed. Her access to the temple is revoked. Her security credentials are confiscated. She's told her flight back to New York has been booked for the following evening. She sits in her hotel room, stripped of authority, and realizes she has something the government doesn't: the photographs, the complete inventory, and the Reveal equation. She's not powerless — she just has no official power.

**Cliffhanger:** Her phone buzzes. The encrypted cloud upload she set up at dawn — someone accessed it. Not her. The dead-man's switch has been probed. Someone knows she has the files.

---

### ROTHWELL'S WAR (Chapters 35–43)

---

### Chapter 35 — The Philanthropist
**POV:** Rothwell | **Day:** 10 (morning) | **Location:** Taj Vivanta, Thiruvananthapuram

Rothwell processes Mehra's report: the mechanism was activated, a passage opened, sounds of gunfire, the brief glimpse of a descending staircase before the wall sealed again. He doesn't have the contents — but he now has proof that the repository exists and can be accessed. He shifts strategy from theft to legal/media warfare, activating Yuki Tanaka's court petition and preparing a media campaign that will frame India as "hoarding knowledge that belongs to humanity."

**Cliffhanger:** His phone rings. London. His legal team has found a precedent: a 2003 UNESCO convention clause that allows international petitions for cultural property of "outstanding universal value." They can file within forty-eight hours.

---

### Chapter 36 — The Priest's Decision
**POV:** Kesavan | **Day:** 10 (morning) | **Location:** Kesavan's quarters

Kesavan learns that the government has classified the repository and dismissed Meera. He faces a choice: accept the government's control, trigger the failsafe and seal the repository forever, or find another way. He opens the priest's log — the 2,300-year record — and reads the entries about previous crises, looking for guidance from the guardians who came before him. The log offers no easy answers, only the accumulated wisdom of people who chose to protect knowledge in impossible circumstances.

**Cliffhanger:** He closes the log and makes a decision. He will not trigger the failsafe. He will not accept government control. He will find Meera Desai and give her the one thing the government cannot take: the priest's log, and with it, the moral authority of 2,300 years of guardianship.

---

### Chapter 37 — The Meeting
**POV:** Meera / Kesavan | **Day:** 10 (afternoon) | **Location:** Kesavan's quarters

Kesavan contacts Meera through a private channel — a young priest who serves as intermediary. They meet in his quarters, away from government surveillance. He gives her the priest's log: a handwritten record spanning eighty generations, documenting every interaction between the guardians and the outside world. "This is yours now," he says. "Not because the government can't take it — because you've earned it."

**Cliffhanger:** Meera opens the log and reads the most recent entry, in Kesavan's hand: "The repository has been opened. The American woman used the Reveal configuration. Ashoka's intention fulfilled. I am too old to fight what comes next. She is not." Below it: a note about the astronomical alignment — the court-ordered opening date. "The stones will yield again on that day. The mechanism will activate twice: once for Reveal, once for Open. Whoever is present will choose."

---

### Chapter 38 — The Rogue
**POV:** Rathore | **Day:** 10 (evening) | **Location:** Liaison office

Rathore is reassigned to "repository security" — a polite way of saying he's been sidelined too, tasked with guarding a chamber he's not allowed to enter. He chafes against the orders but follows them, until Gupta intercepts a piece of intelligence that changes everything: Rothwell's court petition includes a specific description of the mechanism, the passage, and the repository contents — details that could only come from someone with direct knowledge.

**Cliffhanger:** Rathore stares at the intercept. Someone on the delegation — someone who descended into the repository — is feeding information to Rothwell. The leak isn't Gupta anymore. It's someone with higher clearance.

---

### Chapter 39 — The Second Leak
**POV:** Rathore / Meera | **Day:** 11 (morning) | **Location:** Secure meeting point

Rathore contacts Meera despite orders — the intelligence about the second leak is too sensitive for official channels. They meet in a park near the temple, pretending to be two professionals taking a morning walk. Rathore lays out the evidence: the delegation's movements, the timing of Rothwell's petition, the specific details that only someone inside the repository could know. The circle of suspects is small and uncomfortable — it's someone who was in that chamber.

**Cliffhanger:** Meera says quietly: "There's one person on the delegation who has a connection to Rothwell that nobody checked." She pulls up a file on her phone: the ASI director-general served on an advisory board with a Rothwell Foundation trustee for three years. Not proof — but a thread worth pulling.

---

### Chapter 40 — The ASI Director
**POV:** Meera / Rathore | **Day:** 11 (afternoon) | **Location:** Liaison office

Rathore quietly investigates the ASI director-general's connections and finds a pattern: consulting fees from a Rothwell-affiliated cultural heritage NGO, attendance at private functions at Rothwell House, and — most damning — a email from two months ago referencing "the Kerala matter" sent to a personal account. The director-general has been compromised, not for ideology but for access: Rothwell promised him first access to the repository's manuscripts in exchange for information.

**Cliffhanger:** Rathore shows Meera the evidence. "If we expose him, the delegation collapses, the government is embarrassed, and the repository becomes a political football. If we don't expose him, Rothwell has eyes inside the system. Either way, we lose."

---

### Chapter 41 — The Filing — PINCH POINT
**POV:** Rothwell / Meera | **Day:** 11 (evening) | **Location:** Rothwell's hotel / Meera's hotel

Rothwell's legal team files an emergency petition with the International Court of Justice: *Rothwell Foundation v. Republic of India*. The petition claims the repository contains "cultural heritage of outstanding universal value" and argues that India's classification of the contents violates international cultural property norms. Simultaneously, Rothwell's media contact — a senior editor at a major international newspaper — publishes a carefully sourced story: SECRET CHAMBER BENEATH INDIAN TEMPLE HOLDS ANCIENT KNOWLEDGE GOVERNMENT WON'T SHARE.

**Cliffhanger:** The story breaks at midnight. Within an hour, it's the top trending topic worldwide. Meera watches it unfold on her phone, feeling the ground shift beneath her. Rothwell has done something she couldn't: he made the repository public. And he's framed the narrative on his terms.

---

### Chapter 42 — The Firestorm
**POV:** Meera / various | **Day:** 12 (morning) | **Location:** Various — media coverage

Global media erupts. Every news outlet picks up the story. The Indian government is blindsided and furious — forced to confirm the repository's existence while denying Rothwell's framing. The PM holds a press conference claiming the discovery as a triumph of Indian heritage. Social media divides along predictable lines: Western voices calling for "international access," Indian voices calling it "neocolonial interference." Meera watches both sides distort the truth and feels disgust at the colonial undertones of both.

**Cliffhanger:** The PM's press conference ends with an announcement: the repository will remain classified, but a "limited international committee" will be formed to advise on access. Meera's name is not mentioned. She's been cut out entirely.

---

### Chapter 43 — The Counter
**POV:** Meera | **Day:** 12 (afternoon) | **Location:** Meera's hotel room

Meera realizes she's been outmaneuvered at every turn — by the government, by Rothwell, by forces larger than herself. She holds the photographs, the equation, the priest's log, and the dead-man's switch. She has leverage but no platform. Then she remembers Rothwell's message — *"We should talk"* — and makes a decision that feels like surrender but might be strategy: she calls Rothwell and agrees to meet.

**Cliffhanger:** Rothwell answers on the first ring. "Ms. Desai. I've been waiting for this call. Shall I send a car?"

---

### THE POLITICAL CRUCIBLE (Chapters 44–58)

---

### Chapter 44 — The Offer
**POV:** Meera / Rothwell | **Day:** 12 (evening) | **Location:** Rothwell's hotel suite

Meera and Rothwell meet face-to-face for the first time — a tense, intellectually charged conversation between two people who represent genuinely different philosophies about cultural heritage. Rothwell is charming, knowledgeable, and makes a compelling case for international access. He offers Meera a position on his advisory board — legitimate, public, well-compensated. She declines and makes a counter-offer: she'll publicly credit his family's role in preserving the mathematical notation if he publicly supports international scholarly access through a proper committee. Rothwell, recognizing that Meera holds the stronger hand (she has the knowledge; he has only money), accepts — not because he's reformed, but because respectability serves his long game.

**Cliffhanger:** As she leaves, Rothwell says: "You know I'm going to be on that committee, Ms. Desai. One way or another." Meera doesn't respond. She knows he's right.

---

### Chapter 45 — The Proposal
**POV:** Meera / Rathore / Kesavan | **Day:** 12 (night) | **Location:** Temple complex

Meera presents her committee proposal to Rathore and Kesavan: three-way custodianship — temple trust (cultural), ASI (scientific), international scholars (academic). No single entity controls access. Rothwell gets a seat — because his presence ensures Western engagement and his self-interest keeps the process honest. Kesavan agrees reluctantly; Rathore agrees to advocate within RAW; the proposal is sent to the PM's office.

**Cliffhanger:** The response comes at 3 AM: "Proposal received. Under review. No action until legal proceedings resolved." Translation: the government is using Rothwell's court petition as cover to maintain control. Meera stares at the message and feels the familiar weight of institutional inertia pressing down.

---

### Chapter 46 — The Committee
**POV:** Meera / various | **Day:** 13 (morning) | **Location:** Liaison office

International pressure — from Rothwell's media play, from the court petition, from academic institutions worldwide demanding access — forces the government's hand. The PM's office announces the formation of the Padmanabhaswamy International Knowledge Committee: twelve members representing India, Britain, the US, Japan, Germany, and the Vatican. Meera is appointed founding secretary. Rothwell — as agreed — is given a board seat.

**Cliffhanger:** Meera reads the committee charter and finds a clause buried in paragraph seven: "The Government of India reserves the right to classify specific findings as matters of national security, removing them from committee jurisdiction." The government has given with one hand and taken with the other.

---

### Chapter 47 — Inside the Tent
**POV:** Rothwell | **Day:** 13 (afternoon) | **Location:** Committee meeting room

Rothwell takes his seat on the committee with the grace of a man who has achieved exactly what he planned. He's polite, constructive, engaged — and quietly establishing relationships with every member, identifying which ones can be influenced, which ones are threats, and which ones have access to the repository's most sensitive contents. In his POV, we see his calculation: the committee is a stepping stone, not a destination. He'll use it to get close to the knowledge, then find a way to control its dissemination.

**Cliffhanger:** After the meeting, he calls Yuki Tanaka. "The committee has access to the repository beginning tomorrow. I need the scanning equipment in place by then. High-resolution. Every manuscript, every page. If we can't take the knowledge out, we'll copy it."

---

### Chapter 48 — The Second Descent
**POV:** Meera / Committee members | **Day:** 13 (evening) | **Location:** The Repository

The first official committee access to the repository. Scholars from six countries descend into the 2,300-year-old chamber and begin the systematic cataloguing of its contents. Priya finds the Brahmi-Indus bilingual document and begins preliminary translation. The ASI director-general (the compromised one) is present, his eyes moving between the manuscripts and Rothwell with a frequency Meera notices.

**Cliffhanger:** Rathore pulls Meera aside. "I've confirmed the director-general is the leak. But if we expose him now, the committee collapses and the government takes full control. We need to contain him, not remove him."

---

### Chapter 49 — The Inventory
**POV:** Meera / Priya | **Days:** 13–15 | **Location:** Repository / committee offices

Compressed-time chapter covering the first days of cataloguing. The discoveries pile up: mathematical theorems predating Brahmagupta, metallurgical formulae for wootz steel, astronomical tables, medical texts, and the Indus Valley documents. Priya's preliminary analysis of the Brahmi-Indus key suggests the Indus script records a proto-Dravidian language with mathematical notation. The knowledge is moving into the world — slowly, carefully, under committee oversight.

**Cliffhanger:** A secure message from Rathore: "The RAW director has requested a private meeting. He wants to discuss 'specific items of national security interest' in the repository. He's coming for the mathematical manuscripts."

---

### Chapter 50 — The Weaponization
**POV:** Meera / Rathore | **Day:** 16 | **Location:** Committee office

The RAW director formally requests that the mathematical manuscripts — specifically, the prime factorization framework Priya identified — be removed from committee jurisdiction and placed under military research classification. The committee charter's national security clause gives him the legal basis. Meera objects; the vote splits along national lines; the Indian members support classification; the international members oppose it. The deadlock is unresolved.

**Cliffhanger:** Priya approaches Meera after the vote with a manuscript she hasn't shown anyone yet. "I held this back from the main inventory. It wasn't in the Reveal documentation — I found it in a side chamber. It describes a prime factorization algorithm that is... Meera, if this works, it breaks RSA encryption. Every bank, every government, every secure communication in the world. This is why Ashoka sealed it."

---

### Chapter 51 — The Dangerous Knowledge
**POV:** Meera | **Day:** 16 (night) | **Location:** Meera's hotel room

Meera stares at the manuscript Priya gave her and feels the full weight of what it represents. The knowledge IS dangerous — exactly as Ashoka feared, exactly as Kesavan warned. If the government classifies it for military research, they'll weaponize it. If the committee publishes it openly, anyone can weaponize it. If Rothwell gets a copy, he'll sell access to the highest bidder. There is no good option — only less bad ones.

**Cliffhanger:** Her phone rings. Unknown number. She answers. "Ms. Desai. This is Mrs. Kapoor, from the Prime Minister's office. We need to discuss the algorithm. The Prime Minister is taking a personal interest. You will bring the manuscript to Delhi tomorrow." It is not a request.

---

### Chapter 52 — The Stand
**POV:** Meera | **Day:** 17 (morning) | **Location:** Committee office

Meera refuses to hand over the algorithm to the PM's office without committee approval. She invokes her authority as founding secretary and the committee's charter — which requires a full vote for any item to be reclassified. Mrs. Kapoor is unimpressed. The PM's office applies pressure through multiple channels: Rathore is reassigned (again), the committee's budget is questioned, and Meera's diplomatic status is "reviewed."

**Cliffhanger:** Rathore, informed of his reassignment, calls Meera from the airport. "They're sending me to Delhi for 'consultation.' That's code for 'stay out of the way until we've taken what we want.' Meera — they're going to classify the algorithm with or without the committee vote. The national security clause gives them cover."

---

### Chapter 53 — Rothwell's Move
**POV:** Rothwell | **Day:** 17 (afternoon) | **Location:** Rothwell's hotel suite

Rothwell learns about the algorithm through the compromised ASI director-general. He immediately grasps its implications — not for cryptography (that doesn't interest him) but for leverage. If he can obtain a copy, he can use the threat of public disclosure to force the Indian government's hand on repository access. He instructs Yuki Tanaka to prepare a second, more aggressive legal filing: an emergency motion arguing that the algorithm's classification violates international norms on scientific knowledge sharing.

**Cliffhanger:** He also sends a message to Meera: "I know about the algorithm. I know the government wants to classify it. I know you have the only copy not in the repository. I can help you keep it out of military hands — but only if you trust me. The enemy of your enemy, Ms. Desai."

---

### Chapter 54 — The Confrontation
**POV:** Meera / Rothwell | **Day:** 17 (evening) | **Location:** Temple exterior

Meera confronts Rothwell outside the temple — the most intellectually charged scene in the novel. Not a fight but an argument. Rothwell argues that no single nation should control knowledge with global security implications. Meera argues that "international access" in Rothwell's framework means Western control, that the knowledge came from India and belongs to India's heritage, and that his "philanthropy" is colonialism in a tailored suit. Both make valid points. Neither fully wins.

**Cliffhanger:** Rothwell's parting shot: "Your committee is a fantasy. It has no enforcement power, no independent funding, and a charter that lets the government classify anything it wants. The only thing standing between that algorithm and a military research lab is you — one woman with a manuscript she can't publish, can't destroy, and can't give away. That's not a plan, Ms. Desai. That's a prayer."

---

### Chapter 55 — The Dead Man's Switch
**POV:** Meera / Priya | **Day:** 17 (night) | **Location:** Meera's hotel room

Meera prepares her data package — everything: photographs, translations, the mathematical key, the Ashoka Chronicle text, and the algorithm manuscript — and sends it to Priya with dead-man's switch instructions. If Meera is forced out, if the committee is corrupted, if the knowledge is classified for military use, Priya releases everything to fifty universities simultaneously. It's Mutually Assured Destruction — the nuclear option for knowledge warfare.

**Cliffhanger:** Priya asks: "What if you're wrong? What if releasing everything is worse than letting the government classify it?" Meera doesn't have an answer. She sets the switch anyway.

---

### Chapter 56 — Kesavan's Choice
**POV:** Kesavan | **Day:** 18 (morning) | **Location:** Kesavan's quarters

Kesavan, watching the political battle from outside, faces his own crisis. His health is failing — the tremor in his hand has spread, his breathing is labored, and the young priests are concerned. He knows he is dying. The guardianship must pass. He formally designates Meera as the next guardian — not through ritual (there's no time) but through the priest's log, adding an entry that records his decision and his reasoning.

**Cliffhanger:** He writes: "I pass the guardianship to Meera Desai, not because she is a priest or a scholar of our tradition, but because she solved the puzzle we could not, opened the door we were forbidden to open, and chose the path Ashoka intended. The knowledge is in motion. I trust her to steer it."

---

### Chapter 57 — The Ultimatum
**POV:** Meera | **Day:** 18 (afternoon) | **Location:** Meera's hotel room

Meera sends her ultimatum to both the PM's office and Rothwell: if the repository is not opened to full international scholarly access within six months, with the algorithm under independent peer review rather than military classification, everything in her possession goes to fifty universities. She can't be discredited (the photographs are verified), she can't be silenced (the dead-man's switch is automated), and she can't be ignored (the international media is watching).

**Cliffhanger:** Two responses arrive within the hour. The PM's office: "We will discuss terms." Rothwell: "Clever. I'll withdraw the court petition in exchange for a guaranteed seat on the oversight board and full scanning access. You need me, Ms. Desai. I'm the only one both sides trust enough to hate equally."

---

### Chapter 58 — The Terms
**POV:** Meera / Rathore / Kesavan | **Day:** 18 (evening) | **Location:** Temple complex

Meera negotiates the final terms with both sides simultaneously: the committee stays, the government drops the classification of all non-algorithm materials, the algorithm goes to an independent review panel of cryptographers and mathematicians from five nations, and Rothwell gets his board seat plus scanning access. It's imperfect, it's fragile, and it depends on the good faith of parties who have demonstrated very little good faith. But it's the best deal anyone is going to get.

**Cliffhanger:** Kesavan, hearing the terms, nods slowly. "Ashoka designed the Reveal configuration for this moment. Not for priests. Not for governments. For a committee of people who disagree but choose to work together anyway." He pauses. "I think he would approve."

### THE DARK KNOWLEDGE (Chapters 59–65) — BLACK MOMENT SEQUENCE

---

### Chapter 59 — The Opening Prep
**POV:** Meera / various | **Days:** 18–20 | **Location:** Temple complex

Compressed time as the vault opening approaches. The committee completes initial cataloguing. The security arrangements are finalized. Meera reviews everything one last time: the photographs, the priest's log, her father's notebook, the Reveal equation. The algorithm manuscript stays with her — the one piece of knowledge that hasn't been digitized, copied, or shared with anyone except Priya.

**Cliffhanger:** Rathore returns from Delhi, his face carved from stone. "The PM's office made a back-channel deal. After the public opening, the algorithm goes to DRDO — the Defence Research and Development Organisation. Military classification. The committee doesn't get a vote."

---

### Chapter 60 — The Betrayal
**POV:** Meera / Rathore | **Day:** 20 (night) | **Location:** Temple corridor

The black moment begins. The government has bypassed the committee entirely. The algorithm — knowledge that could break encryption worldwide — is going to India's military research apparatus. Rothwell, learning of the deal through his compromised source, accelerates his own plans: he's going to use the public vault opening as cover to scan every manuscript in the repository, including the algorithm, before the military takes control. And the compromised ASI director-general is going to help him.

**Cliffhanger:** Meera realizes she's been outmaneuvered at every level. The government has the legal authority. Rothwell has the resources. She has... the dead-man's switch. Which would destroy everything she's spent the entire novel trying to protect.

---

### Chapter 61 — The Conscience
**POV:** Rathore | **Day:** 20 (late night) | **Location:** Rathore's hotel room

Rathore sits alone with his orders: facilitate the military transfer, ensure the algorithm reaches DRDO, keep Meera away from the repository during the public opening. He's spent eighteen years following orders. His father died following orders. He opens his wallet and looks at the photograph he carries — his father in uniform, the one taken six months before Kargil. For the first time in his career, Vikram Rathore wonders if following orders is the same as doing the right thing.

**Cliffhanger:** He picks up his phone and calls Meera. "I have something to tell you. And when I tell you, I'll be committing a crime. Are you ready?"

---

### Chapter 62 — The Warning
**POV:** Meera / Rathore | **Day:** 21 (pre-dawn) | **Location:** Temple complex

Rathore tells Meera everything: the DRDO deal, the timeline, the compromised ASI director, Rothwell's scanning operation planned for the public opening. He's betraying his government, his service, and his oath — because the algorithm in military hands is worse than the algorithm in no one's hands. "I've watched my country lose heritage to smugglers for eighteen years," he says. "I'm not going to watch it lose its soul to a weapons program."

**Cliffhanger:** Meera now knows everything. The government's plan. Rothwell's plan. And she has hours to stop both.

---

### Chapter 63 — The No-Win — BLACK MOMENT
**POV:** Meera | **Day:** 21 (dawn) | **Location:** Temple complex

The black moment crystallizes. Every path forward leads to disaster:
- Let the government proceed: algorithm goes to military, weaponized
- Activate the dead-man's switch: all knowledge released indiscriminately, including the dangerous algorithm
- Let Rothwell scan everything: Western control of Indian heritage, colonialism 2.0
- Trigger the failsafe: 2,300 years of preserved knowledge sealed forever

There is no winning move. Meera stands in the temple corridor as dawn breaks over the gopuram and feels the full weight of Ashoka's dilemma pressing down on her.

**Cliffhanger:** Her phone rings. Her father. "Meera, I've been watching the news. I know what's happening. And I have something to tell you about the Reveal configuration that I didn't put in the notebook."

---

### Chapter 64 — The Third Way
**POV:** Meera | **Day:** 21 (morning) | **Location:** Temple complex (phone call)

Her father tells her something she didn't know: the Reveal configuration has a secondary function. When activated during the astronomical alignment (which occurs on the day of the public opening), it produces not just copper-plate impressions but a complete, sealed, authenticated record of the repository's contents — a snapshot that can be independently verified by any scholar. This record, once created, cannot be classified, controlled, or destroyed. It exists in the mechanism itself, reproducible by anyone who knows the sequence.

**Cliffhanger:** Meera understands: she doesn't need to stop the government or stop Rothwell. She needs to create an irrevocable public record that makes classification impossible and control irrelevant. The mechanism is the dead-man's switch — built 2,300 years ago for exactly this moment.

---

### Chapter 65 — The Decision
**POV:** Meera / Rathore / Priya | **Day:** 21 (day) | **Location:** Temple underground

Meera, Rathore, and Priya descend to the mechanism one last time, during the final pre-opening security sweep. Meera reconfigures the blocks for the enhanced Reveal — the secondary function her father described. The mechanism activates. Deep beneath the temple, the copper-plate impression system produces a complete authenticated record of every item in the repository, stamped with the astronomical alignment date and the mechanism's own verification mark. The record is physical, permanent, and impossible to classify. Meera removes the plates and secures them.

**Cliffhanger:** She emerges from the underground to find Kesavan waiting. His eyes are clear, his voice steady. "Tonight is the opening. The alignment begins at sunset. The mechanism will activate one final time, and when it does, the choice passes from the guardians to the world." He pauses. "I am ready. Are you?"

# ACT 3: THE OPENING (Chapters 66–85)

### Chapter 66 — The Day
**POV:** Meera | **Day:** 21 (afternoon) | **Location:** Temple complex

The day of the public opening. The world is watching. Every major news network, dignitaries, Supreme Court justices, the PM. Meera takes her position as art-crime consultant, carrying the authenticated copper-plate record in a sealed case. Rothwell arrives as a VIP guest. The compromised ASI director lurks at the edges. Rathore manages security with a team he now trusts only partially.

---

### Chapter 67 — The Door Opens
**POV:** Meera | **Day:** 21 (evening) | **Location:** Vault B entrance

The Vault B door opens. Mountains of gold. The world sees Indian wealth. The media goes wild. Meera watches the officials' eyes — who knows about the repository, who doesn't. The gold is the spectacle; the knowledge is the secret.

---

### Chapter 68 — Rothwell's Scan
**POV:** Rothwell / Meera | **Day:** 21 (night) | **Location:** Repository

During the evening, while media focuses on the gold, Rothwell's team attempts the scan — but Meera and Rathore intercept. A tense confrontation in the repository: Rothwell's operatives caught with scanning equipment, the compromised ASI director exposed.
