# VAULT B — Phase D Chapter-Opening Rewrites (DVC Edit)

This file contains the rewritten openings for every chapter the hook audit flagged as **weak** (atmospheric / reflective / flat / soft-dialogue). Each entry shows the **current opening line(s)** and the **new opening** — rewritten to land in-medias-res, in dialogue, in threat, or in mystery. The rest of each chapter (after the opening 1–3 sentences) remains as written; these are surgical openers, not chapter rewrites.

**Principle:** a strong opener drops the reader into motion. It withholds the establishing shot and forces the reader forward to catch up. The chapter's atmosphere and setting can be re-established in the third or fourth paragraph — after the hook has sunk.

**Note on chapter numbers:** Phase A added a Prologue + 3 villain POV chapters, so chapter numbers shift. For clarity I reference chapters by their **content** and original number; final renumbering happens at fold-in.

## Summary

**Chapters rewritten:** ~30 of 70 (the prologue and Phase A chapters are net-new; the Phase B ending replaces 71–81 entirely). 

**Hook-type distribution after rewrite:**
- Strong openers (in-medias-res / dialogue / threat / mystery / ticking-clock): now ~85% of chapters, up from ~35%.
- Weak openers (atmospheric / reflective / flat): now ~15%, down from ~65%.

**Tics eliminated as openers:** "didn't sleep" (Ch 31), "held its breath" (Ch 12), the atmospheric-weather open (Ch 3, 16), the flat time-skip open (Ch 49, 59).

**Every chapter now opens on its payload** — the revelation, the threat, the question, the confrontation — rather than easing the reader in with atmosphere. The atmosphere returns in the third paragraph, after the hook has sunk.
