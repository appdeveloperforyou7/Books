## 1. THE CORTE-REAL BROTHERS (Gaspar and Miguel)

### ESTABLISHED FACTS

**Gaspar Corte-Real (c. 1450 – disappeared 1501)**
- Born into the noble Corte-Real family on Terceira in the Azores, youngest of three sons of João Vaz Corte-Real (c. 1420–1496)
- In 1498, King Manuel I of Portugal took interest in western exploration, likely believing lands discovered by John Cabot were within Portugal's control under the Treaty of Tordesillas
- **1500 voyage**: Gaspar reached Greenland, believing it to be East Asia, but was unable to land due to ice
- **1501 voyage**: Took three caravels. Prevented from landing at Greenland again by frozen seas. Changed course and landed in a country of large rivers, pine trees, and berries — believed to be **Labrador**. They captured **57 Indigenous people** who were taken back to Portugal and sold into slavery to help finance the voyage. Two of the three ships returned; the ship carrying Gaspar was **lost at sea**
- Nothing more was heard of Gaspar after 1501

**Miguel Corte-Real (c. 1448 – disappeared 1502)**
- Charted approximately **600 miles of the coast of Labrador**
- Had invested significant sums in Gaspar's expeditions; Gaspar had promised him a share of any new lands claimed
- **May 1502**: Miguel set out from Lisbon with three ships to search for his brother. The ships apparently reached the location where Gaspar had landed, then split up to search. The ship carrying Miguel failed to appear at the designated rendezvous on **August 20, 1502**. The other two ships returned to Portugal; Miguel was never seen again
- **1503**: The last surviving brother, Vasco Añes Corte-Real, planned another rescue expedition. King Manuel I furnished two ships but **would not permit Vasco himself to sail**. The expedition returned without finding any trace of either brother

**Important context**: Memorial University historian Jeff Webb stated in 2017 that Gaspar is "a minor figure about whom very, very little is known with confidence." The 20th-century myth-building around Corte-Real was largely the work of historian-diplomat Eduardo Brazão, former National Secretary of Portugal's propaganda ministry under Salazar.

### CONTESTED / SPECULATIVE
- Whether Gaspar and Miguel reached areas further south than Labrador (i.e., Newfoundland proper, or even New England)
- Whether their father João Vaz Corte-Real may have sailed to North America even earlier (no solid evidence)
- The exact location of their landfall — Labrador vs. Newfoundland

### NOVEL FODDER
- **Two brothers disappear nine years apart in the same waters.** The Portuguese Crown refuses to let the third brother sail. What did they find? What was the Crown protecting?
- The 57 captured Indigenous people brought to Portugal — your novel could trace what happened to them or their descendants
- The possibility (never proven) that Miguel survived and lived among Indigenous people for years
- The Salazar-era propaganda angle: why was 20th-century Portugal so invested in amplifying this story?

## 2. THE DIGHTON ROCK INSCRIPTIONS

### ESTABLISHED FACTS
- A 40-ton sandstone boulder originally located in the riverbed of the **Taunton River** at **Berkley, Massachusetts** (formerly part of Dighton)
- Noted for petroglyphs: "primarily lines, geometric shapes, and schematic drawings of people, along with writing, both verified and not"
- First recorded description by Rev. Cotton Mather in 1690: described as having "very deeply Engraved" characters (though later evidence suggests the incisions were never very deep)
- **1680**: Rev. John Danforth made a drawing of the petroglyphs, preserved in the British Museum — but his drawing **conflicts** with the reports of others and current markings
- The inscriptions have been weathering for centuries and were described as already largely effaced by 1768

### THEORIES (IN ORDER OF CREDIBILITY)
1. **American Indigenous origin** — Most likely. A schematic face on the rock is similar to an Indigenous petroglyph in Eastern Vermont. This is the mainstream archaeological view.
2. **Portuguese explorer origin (1912)** — Proposed by Edmund B. Delabarre of Brown University, who claimed the markings were abbreviated Latin reading: *"I, Miguel Cortereal, 1511. In this place, by the will of God, I became a chief of the Indians."* This was **thoroughly debunked** by Douglas Hunter in *The Place of Stone* (2017) and dismissed by Samuel Eliot Morison in 1971.
3. **Norse origin (1837)** — Proposed by Carl Christian Rafn. Rejected by archaeologists T.D. Kendrick and Kenneth Feder.
4. **Phoenician origin (1783)** — Proposed by Ezra Stiles. No credible support.
5. **Chinese origin** — Proposed by Gavin Menzies in *1421* (2002). Pseudohistory, thoroughly debunked.

### NOVEL FODDER
- The rock is real. The inscriptions are genuinely mysterious. Multiple conflicting drawings exist from different centuries. Danforth's 1680 drawing doesn't match what's on the rock now — **what was added or removed, and when?**
- The Taunton River connects to Narragansett Bay, which connects to **Newport, Rhode Island** — the site of the Newport Tower. Geographic proximity is real.
- Delabarre's reading is debunked, but your fictional scholar could discover something new — perhaps using modern imaging technology that reveals earlier, deliberately erased markings
- The rock is now preserved in a museum at Dighton Rock State Park — a thriller-worthy location

## 3. PORTUGUESE FISHING FLEETS IN NEWFOUNDLAND/CANADA BEFORE COLUMBUS

### ESTABLISHED FACTS
- **The Portuguese were fishing the Grand Banks of Newfoundland by at least the early 1500s**, and possibly earlier
- There is a long-standing tradition that Basque, Breton, and Portuguese fishermen knew about the cod-rich waters of the North Atlantic before any official "discovery"
- The Portuguese had a well-established **cod fishing industry** and were known to fish off Newfoundland; a Portuguese fishing fleet operated there regularly by the 1500s
- **No documentary evidence** proves Portuguese fishermen reached Newfoundland before Columbus (1492) or Cabot (1497)
- The Azores, settled by the Portuguese from the 1430s, served as a mid-Atlantic staging point that made North Atlantic voyages more feasible

### CONTESTED
- Whether unrecorded fishing voyages preceded official explorations — this is **plausible but unproven**
- Some historians argue the Portuguese may have known about Brazil's coastline before Cabral's official "discovery" in 1500, based on how far south his landfall was (suggesting deliberate exploration rather than accident)
- The **secrecy policy** of the Portuguese Crown (see Section 8) means absence of documentation does not prove absence of voyages

### NOVEL FODDER
- A secret Portuguese fishing settlement on the New England coast, unknown to history, is **extremely plausible fiction** because:
  - The Portuguese were demonstrably in these waters
  - They had every reason to keep discoveries secret
  - Fishing communities leave minimal archaeological trace
  - The Portuguese had the nautical technology to make these voyages

## 4. THE PORTUGUESE CONNECTION TO THE NEWPORT TOWER

### ESTABLISHED FACTS ABOUT THE TOWER
- Round stone tower in **Touro Park, Newport, Rhode Island**
- Not exactly circular: ~22 feet diameter one way, ~23 feet the other
- Eight cylindrical columns forming stone arches (two slightly broader than the other six)
- Interior was once coated with smooth white plaster
- Has a fireplace backed with grey stone, flanked by nooks
- Four windows on the main floor, three very small ones at upper level
- Height: 28 feet, walls ~3 feet thick

### SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE (STRONGLY FAVORS 17TH-CENTURY COLONIAL ORIGIN)
- **1677 will of Benedict Arnold** (first colonial governor of Rhode Island, NOT the Revolutionary War traitor) mentions "my stone built Wind Mill" — the site is between this mill and his mansion
- **1848 mortar comparison**: Mortar from the tower proved "very similar composition" to mortar from the oldest known Newport structures (c. 1640), including Arnold's tomb — all "composed of shell lime, sand, and gravel"
- **1948 archaeological excavation** by the Society for American Archaeology (Harvard's Hugh Henken, field work by William S. Godfrey): All discovered artifacts were from the 17th century. Godfrey identified Benedict Arnold as the builder
- **1993 radiocarbon dating** of mortar by Danish/Finnish team: Results suggest mortar was made between **1635 and 1698** (approximately 1680)
- The tower closely resembles the **Chesterton Windmill** in Warwickshire, England (built 1632-33 as a windmill — original building accounts survive, including payments for sailcloths)

### THE PORTUGUESE HYPOTHESIS (FRINGE)
- **Edmund B. Delabarre** (early 20th century): Associated Dighton Rock with Miguel Corte-Real and linked this to the Newport Tower
- **Manuel Luciano DaSilva**: Suggested one of the Corte-Real brothers built the tower as a watchtower
- **Herbert Pell** (former U.S. Ambassador, 1948): Argued the tower resembles elements of the **Convent of Christ at Tomar** in Portugal
- **ALL Portuguese-origin claims are rejected by mainstream archaeologists and historians**

### THE OBSERVATORY HYPOTHESIS (INTRIGUING BUT UNPROVEN)
- Professor William Penhallow (URI physicist and astronomer) found astronomical alignments in the tower's windows:
  - Summer solstice sunset shines through west window onto a niche in the inner wall
  - Angle from east to west window tracks the **lunar minor standstill** (18° south of west)
  - Smaller windows align with significant stars
- These alignments **could be accidental**, but if deliberate, would explain the "odd" window pattern
- This does NOT prove Portuguese origin — astronomical knowledge existed in 17th-century England too

### NOVEL FODDER — THIS IS YOUR KEY LOCATION
The Newport Tower is **perfect** for a Da Vinci Code-style thriller because:
1. **It is genuinely mysterious** — even mainstream scholars disagree about whether it was a windmill, a lookout, or something else
2. **The astronomical alignments are REAL** — someone designed them deliberately, and the question of WHO is unresolved
3. **The Portuguese connection is not crazy** — it's wrong, but it's not crazy. The Portuguese were in these waters. The Convent of Christ at Tomar has a round church. The connection was proposed by a U.S. Ambassador.
4. **The mortar dating has a loophole** — carbon dating of mortar dates the mortar, not the structure. Mortar could have been applied during repointing long after initial construction.
5. **The Chesterton Windmill connection** — Benedict Arnold grew up near Chesterton. But what if the resemblance isn't because Arnold copied it, but because both structures share a common ancestor in design?
6. **The fireplace** — windmills don't need fireplaces. Some English windmills do have them, but it's unusual. Your novel could use this as a clue that the building had a different original purpose.
7. **The eight arches** — two are broader than the other six. Why? This irregularity has never been fully explained.

## 5. THE KNIGHTS TEMPLAR → ORDER OF CHRIST → PORTUGUESE EXPLORATION PIPELINE

### ESTABLISHED FACTS (THIS IS REAL HISTORY, NOT CONSPIRACY THEORY)

**The Knights Templar (c. 1118–1312)**
- Founded c. 1118 as a military-religious order during the Crusades
- Became enormously wealthy and powerful across Europe
- Their round churches were modeled after the **Dome of the Rock** in Jerusalem (which crusaders believed was the Temple of Solomon) and the **Church of the Holy Sepulchre**
- **Friday, October 13, 1307**: Philip IV of France ordered mass arrests of Templars. Over the next several years, the order was suppressed and dissolved by Pope Clement V (papal bull *Vox in excelso*, 1312)

**The Portuguese Survival**
- **King Denis of Portugal REFUSED to persecute the Templars** — he needed them for the Reconquista and for rebuilding Portugal after wars
- **March 14, 1319**: Pope John XXII issued the bull *Ad ea ex quibus*, creating the **Order of Christ** — essentially the Knights Templar reconstituted under a new name
- The Templar assets, members, and properties in Portugal were **transferred wholesale** to the Order of Christ
- The Templar headquarters at **Tomar** became the headquarters of the Order of Christ
- Initially based at Castro Marim, the Order moved its headquarters to **Tomar in 1356/1357**

**Prince Henry the Navigator (1394–1460)**
- Became Governor and Administrator of the Order of Christ in **1417** (note: not Grand Master initially, because as a wealthy prince he could not take a vow of poverty)
- Used the **Order's considerable resources** to fund Portuguese maritime exploration
- The **Cross of the Order of Christ** (the distinctive red cross with a white inner cross) was flown on the sails of every Portuguese caravel — it became one of the most recognized symbols of the Age of Discovery
- Under Henry, the Order built cloisters at Tomar and funded the exploration of the African coast

**King Manuel I (1469–1521)**
- Became Governor of the Order in **1484**, then King of Portugal in **1495**
- Through the bull *Constante fide*, became the first king to also be Grand Master of the Order of Christ
- The Order thus came under direct Crown control
- Manuel sponsored the construction of the magnificent **Manueline nave** at Tomar (1510 onwards), with its famous Chapter House Window covered in maritime ropes, coral, and the Cross of the Order of Christ — a visual fusion of Templar heritage and oceanic exploration

**King John III (1502–1557)**
- In 1551, Pope Julius III granted the masterships of the military orders to the Portuguese Crown *in perpetuum* (bull *Praeclara Clarissimi*)
- John III **demilitarized** the Order of Christ, transforming it from a fighting order to a religious one
- Built the magnificent Renaissance Cloister at Tomar

### THE REAL PIPELINE
```
Knights Templar (1118-1312)
    ↓ [Dissolved everywhere except Portugal]
Order of Christ (1319-present)
    ↓ [Prince Henry becomes Governor, 1417]
    ↓ [Uses Order's wealth to fund exploration]
Portuguese Maritime Empire
    ↓ [Cross of Christ on every ship's sails]
    ↓ [Order administered both spiritual and temporal governance of discovered territories]
Age of Discovery
```

This is NOT speculation. This is documented, mainstream history. The Knights Templar literally became the Order of Christ, which literally funded and governed Portuguese exploration.

### NOVEL FODDER
- The Templar → Order of Christ pipeline is **100% real history** that reads like conspiracy fiction
- The Templars were rumored to possess secret knowledge from Jerusalem. Your novel can ask: what if some of that knowledge was navigational?
- The round churches of the Templars (like Tomar) are architecturally similar to the Newport Tower (which is also round with arches). This resemblance has been noted by serious people (Ambassador Pell) and cranks alike. Your novel can exploit this.
- The **Cross of the Order of Christ** appearing on stone buildings in New England (if your novel creates such evidence) would be the equivalent of the fleur-de-lis in The Da Vinci Code
- The fact that King Denis **protected** the Templars when everyone else was destroying them — what did Portugal get in return? What knowledge did the surviving Templars bring?

## 6. EVIDENCE OF PORTUGUESE PRESENCE IN NEW ENGLAND / RHODE ISLAND

### WHAT'S REAL
- **No archaeological evidence** of Portuguese presence in New England has been found
- The Corte-Real brothers are documented as reaching Labrador/Newfoundland, but **not New England**
- Portuguese fishing fleets operated on the Grand Banks (off Newfoundland), but there is **no documented evidence** they established settlements further south
- The name **"Narragansett"** has been claimed by some to have Portuguese etymological roots, but this is **linguistic speculation** with no scholarly support
- Portuguese explorer **Estêvão Gomes** (Esteban Gómez), sailing for Spain, explored the coast of Nova Scotia and Maine in **1524-1525** and may have entered Narragansett Bay and New York Harbor — this is the closest documented Portuguese-associated exploration to Rhode Island

### WHAT'S CONTESTED
- Various claims of Portuguese artifacts found in New England have been made but **none have been verified** by mainstream archaeologists
- The **Dighton Rock** inscriptions (see Section 2) are the primary "evidence" cited for Portuguese presence in Massachusetts, but this has been debunked
- Some researchers have claimed Portuguese origin for various stone structures in New England, but these claims are **not accepted** by the archaeological community

### NOVEL FODDER
- The **absence of evidence** is your friend. The Portuguese were famous for secrecy (see Section 8). A secret Portuguese presence that left no trace is plausible fiction.
- **Estêvão Gomes (1524-1525)** is a real historical figure who actually did explore the New England coast. He's underused in fiction.
- The Portuguese had both the **capability** and the **motive** (Treaty of Tordesillas) to explore New England secretly

## 7. THE CONVENT OF CHRIST AT TOMAR

### ESTABLISHED FACTS
- **UNESCO World Heritage Site** since 1983
- Located in **Tomar, Portugal**, about 140 km northeast of Lisbon
- Founded by the Knights Templar c. 1160; the original castle built by Grand Master **Gualdim Pais**
- The famous **round church (rotunda/charola)** was built in the second half of the 12th century, modeled after the **Dome of the Rock** and **Church of the Holy Sepulchre** in Jerusalem
- 16-sided polygonal exterior, octagonal interior with ambulatory
- **1190**: The castle resisted a siege by Almohad Caliph Abu Yusuf al-Mansur (a plaque commemorates this)
- After 1319, became headquarters of the Order of Christ
- Under **Prince Henry the Navigator** (1417-1450): Built the Cemetery Cloister and Washing Cloister
- Under **King Manuel I** (from 1510): Built the famous **Manueline nave** with the spectacular **Chapter House Window** — covered in ropes, coral, vegetal motifs, the Cross of the Order of Christ, and the armillary sphere (Manuel's personal emblem). Architect: **Diogo de Arruda**
- Under **King John III** (1557): Built the magnificent Renaissance Cloister, designed by Diogo de Torralva and completed by Filippo Terzi
- **1581**: The Portuguese nobility gathered at Tomar to recognize Philip II of Spain as King, beginning the **Iberian Union** (1581-1640)
- The site contains examples of **Romanesque, Gothic, Manueline, and Renaissance** architecture

### THE ARCHITECTURAL CONNECTION TO THE NEWPORT TOWER
- The **round church at Tomar** is round, has arches, and uses specific construction techniques
- Herbert Pell (U.S. Ambassador) noted similarities between Tomar's architecture and the Newport Tower in 1948
- The similarities are **superficial** — Tomar is a massive, elaborately decorated complex; the Newport Tower is a small, simple structure
- However, the architectural vocabulary (round design, arches, thick stone walls) does share a common ancestor in Templar building traditions

### NOVEL FODDER
- Tomar is an **incredibly atmospheric location** for thriller set pieces. It's a real place with:
  - A 12th-century Templar castle
  - A round church modeled on Jerusalem
  - Manueline architecture covered in maritime symbols (ropes, coral, sea life)
  - Secret passages and eight centuries of construction
  - The actual headquarters of the organization that funded the Age of Discovery
- The **Chapter House Window** could contain a coded message — it's already covered in symbolic imagery
- The transition from Templar to Order of Christ happened right here. What was hidden during that transition?
- Tomar's round church was designed to evoke Jerusalem. Your novel could connect this to the Newport Tower's possible use as a navigational observatory — both structures oriented toward significant celestial/geographic points

## 8. PORTUGUESE NAVIGATIONAL SECRECY

### ESTABLISHED FACTS
- Portugal maintained an official policy of **cartographic secrecy** (*política de sigilo*)
- The **Padrão Real** ("Royal Standard Pattern") was the official, secret master map of the world maintained by the Portuguese Crown, updated with every new discovery
- Portuguese pilots and navigators were **forbidden** from sharing navigational knowledge, maps, or sailing directions with foreigners
- The **Casa da Índia** (House of India) in Lisbon administered the royal monopoly on navigation and trade and controlled all cartographic information
- The famous **Cantino Planisphere** (1502) was obtained by an Italian diplomat through **espionage** — it was essentially a covert copy of the Portuguese Padrão Real
- Portugal's knowledge of the South Atlantic and the route around Africa was considered a **state secret** of the highest order
- navigational instruments, sailing directions (*roteiros*), and maps were classified state documents
- After Dias rounded the Cape of Good Hope in 1488, it took **nine years** before Vasco da Gama's voyage to India (1497-1498). Some historians speculate that **secret voyages** occurred during this gap
- The Portuguese developed advanced navigational techniques including:
  - The **caravel** ship type (shallow draft, lateen rig, excellent windward ability)
  - **Celestial navigation** using the sun and stars
  - The **astrrolabe** and **quadrant** for determining latitude
  - **Volta do mar** — the oceanic navigation technique of sailing far out to sea to catch favorable winds and currents, rather than hugging coastlines

### CONTESTED
- Whether the Portuguese had secret knowledge of the Americas before Columbus
- Whether Cabral's discovery of Brazil in 1500 was accidental or deliberate (the fact that he landed much further south than expected suggests he may have already known the coast was there)
- What exactly was on the Padrão Real that we don't know about — most of the secret maps were lost in the **1755 Lisbon earthquake** and subsequent tsunami/fire

### NOVEL FODDER
- The **1755 Lisbon earthquake** destroyed most of the Portuguese royal archives, including the secret maps. What was lost? Your novel can invent a document that survived.
- The Portuguese secrecy policy means that **absence of evidence genuinely does not equal evidence of absence** for this topic
- A secret map showing Portuguese knowledge of the New England coast, hidden for centuries, is perfectly plausible fiction
- The Cantino Planisphere was obtained through espionage — your novel could feature modern espionage over a rediscovered Portuguese map
- The **nine-year gap** between Dias (1488) and da Gama (1497) is historically real and unexplained. Secret voyages are plausible.

## 9. THE TREATY OF TORDESILLAS AND SECRET EXPLORATION

### ESTABLISHED FACTS
- Signed **June 7, 1494** in Tordesillas, Spain
- Divided the newly discovered lands outside Europe between Portugal and Spain along a meridian **370 leagues west of the Cape Verde islands**
- Lands east of the line: Portugal. Lands west: Spain
- The treaty was negotiated **without consulting the Pope** and effectively countered the papal bulls of Alexander VI
- The exact position of the line was **never precisely determined** — the treaty specified 370 leagues but didn't define the length of a league, specify which Cape Verde island was the reference point, or require a joint survey (a joint voyage was specified but never occurred)
- Depending on measurement, the line fell anywhere from approximately **42° to 50° west longitude** — a difference of hundreds of miles
- This imprecision meant the eastern part of what is now Brazil was ambiguously placed — and Portugal got it
- **Key fact**: King John II of Portugal was a brilliant negotiator. He moved the line 270 leagues west of the Pope's original line, protecting the Portuguese route around Africa AND giving Portugal rights to the eastern quarter of Brazil. One scholar called it a "diplomatic triumph for Portugal"
- **The antimeridian problem**: The original treaty didn't specify what happened on the other side of the world. After Magellan's voyage (1519-1522) reached the Moluccas (Spice Islands), Spain and Portugal argued over who owned them. This was resolved by the **Treaty of Zaragoza** (1529), where Spain relinquished claims to the Moluccas for 350,000 gold ducats
- France, England, and the Netherlands **never recognized** the treaty. Francis I of France famously declared: "The sun shines for me as it does for others. I would very much like to see the clause of Adam's will by which I should be denied my share of the world."
- Some historians argue Portugal already knew about Brazil's coastline before Cabral's 1500 "discovery" — his landfall was 12 degrees further south than expected, suggesting deliberate exploration rather than accident

### HOW IT AFFECTED SECRET EXPLORATION
- Under Tordesillas, **Newport, Rhode Island** (approximately 71°W) was solidly in the **Spanish zone**, not the Portuguese zone
- This means that if the Portuguese explored New England, they were doing so in territory legally belonging to Spain — providing a powerful motive for **secrecy**
- The Portuguese had every reason to explore secretly, map secretly, and potentially establish covert outposts in the Spanish zone
- The ambiguous line also meant that territories near the boundary were genuinely contestable

### NOVEL FODDER
- A secret Portuguese outpost in what is now Rhode Island would be a **violation of Tordesillas** — giving both Spain and Portugal reason to suppress knowledge of it
- The treaty's imprecision is a plot device: characters can argue over where exactly the line falls, with implications for who owns what
- The Portuguese knew the exact location of the line better than the Spanish (because they had better maps). This asymmetry of knowledge is a thriller goldmine
- The 350,000 gold ducats Spain received for giving up the Moluccas in 1529 could be paralleled in your novel with a secret payment for something in North America

## NOVEL ARCHITECTURE SUGGESTION

Based on this research, here's where fact and fiction can most convincingly blur:

1. **The Templar → Order of Christ → Portuguese exploration pipeline is 100% real**. This is your "Priory of Sion" — except it's documented history.

2. **The Newport Tower's astronomical alignments are real and unexplained**. Someone designed them. Who and why is genuinely debated.

3. **Portuguese secrecy was real**. The loss of the royal archives in 1755 is real. What was lost is unknown.

4. **The Corte-Real brothers' disappearances are real and mysterious**. Two brothers lost in the same waters nine years apart, and the Crown preventing the third from sailing — this is thriller-ready history.

5. **The fiction enters when you connect these elements**: the Tower as a Portuguese construction, hidden documents surviving the 1755 earthquake, a secret purpose for the Tower connected to Templar knowledge, etc.

6. **The Dighton Rock and Narragansett Bay geography** provides a physical connection between the Tower and potential Portuguese exploration sites.
