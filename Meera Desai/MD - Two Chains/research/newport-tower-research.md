# Newport Tower — Research for THE COLLECTOR

## Physical Description
- Location: Touro Park, Newport, Rhode Island (41°29′09″N 71°18′36″W)
- Not exactly circular: SE-NW diameter 22'2" (6.76m), E-W diameter 23'3" (7.09m)
- Height: 28 feet (8.5m), exterior width: 24 feet (7.3m)
- Interior: roughly 18' (5.5m) diameter
- 8 cylindrical columns forming stone arches (2 slightly broader than the other 6)
- Walls ~3 feet thick
- 4 windows on main floor, 3 small windows at upper level
- Fireplace on west wall, backed with grey stone, flanked by nooks
- Interior once coated with smooth white plaster (remnants visible)
- Evidence of a floor that once supported an interior chamber above the arches

## Documented History
- 1677: Benedict Arnold (colonial governor) mentions "my stone built Wind Mill" in his will
- 1741: Described as "the old stone mill"
- 1760: Used as haymow
- 1767: Used as powder store
- 1776: De Barres' plan marks it as "Stone Wind Mill"
- American Revolution: Used as lookout (American) and munitions storage (British)

## Scientific Investigations
- 1848: Mortar comparison — similar composition to oldest Newport structures (shell lime, sand, gravel)
- 1948: Harvard archaeological dig (William Godfrey) — all artifacts from 17th century; concluded Arnold built it
- 1993: Carbon-14 dating of mortar — results suggest mortar made between 1635-1698; 2003 report dates to ~1680

## Alternative Hypotheses (CONTESTED — Key for Novel)

### Norse Origin
- 1837: Carl Christian Rafn proposed Viking origin
- Longfellow incorporated in poem "The Skeleton in Armor"
- Philip Ainsworth Means (1942): claimed it was a church from 11th-14th century
- Problem: Nothing in Norse architecture is similar

### Portuguese Origin
- Edmund B. Delabarre: associated with Dighton Rock and Corte-Real brothers
- Manuel Luciano DaSilva: suggests Corte-Real built it as watchtower
- Herbert Pell (US Ambassador, 1948): tower resembles elements of Convent of Tomar, Portugal

### Templar Origin
- Andrew Sinclair: Scottish Templars led by Henry Sinclair built it ~100 years before Columbus
- The Sinclair voyage is vigorously disputed

### Observatory Hypothesis
- William Penhallow (URI physicist/astronomer): found astronomical alignments
- 4 of 8 pillars face compass points
- Summer solstice: setting sun shines through west window onto niche
- Angle from east to west window: 18° south of west = lunar minor standstill
- Smaller windows align with significant stars
- Penhallow: "These alignments could be accidental, but if deliberate, it explains why the windows seem so odd"

### Chinese Origin
- Gavin Menzies (1421: The Year China Discovered America) — debunked

## What's Confirmed vs. Contested
- CONFIRMED: Structure exists, is old, mortar dates to mid-17th century
- CONFIRMED: Astronomical alignments are real and measurable
- CONFIRMED: Resembles Chesterton Windmill in England (1632-33)
- CONTESTED: Who built it and when exactly
- CONTESTED: Whether it was a windmill, church, watchtower, or observatory
- DEBUNKED: Norse, Chinese origins
- POSSIBLE BUT UNPROVEN: Portuguese/Templar connection

## Novel's Use
The novel will use the Portuguese/Templar hypothesis as its fiction layer. The FACT page will state the tower's contested origins are real, the astronomical alignments are real, the Portuguese/Templar theories exist but are debated — then the novel will treat them as true within the fiction.
