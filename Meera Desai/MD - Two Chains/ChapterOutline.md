## ACT 1: THE DISCOVERY (Days 1-3, Chapters 1-27)

Ch 1 — NYC. Meera at FBI. Nightmares. Six months post-Vault B. New partner Claire Whitfield. Routine case. [Meera POV]
Ch 2 — Cambridge, MA. Dr. Raj Anand found dead in Widener Library. Professional kill. Research notes stolen. Single page remains. [Meera POV]
Ch 3 — NYC. Meera and Claire assigned. The page has Brahmi numerals — same variant as Padmanabhaswamy. Meera recognizes it. [Meera POV]
Ch 4 — Cambridge. Harvard campus. Meet Professor Nathan Cross — evasive, grieving. Anand's laptop also missing. [Meera POV]
Ch 5 — Cambridge. Cross's office. Rare Indian manuscripts on shelves. Cross admits Anand tracked six artifacts with Brahmi inscriptions in American collections. Gives partial list. [Meera POV]
Ch 6 — Cambridge. Meera photographs the Brahmi page. Sends to Priya in India. Can't tell Claire why the script matters. The first lie. [Meera POV]
Ch 7 — Cambridge. Priya calls back. "Western storehouse." Six "markers." Connected to Ashokan network. The guardianship has followed Meera home. [Meera POV]
Ch 8 — NYC. Meera pulls INTERPOL records. Pattern: London (3 months ago), Berlin (6 months ago), now Harvard. Same method — professional, targeted. [Meera POV]
Ch 9 — NYC. Artifact #1: copper plate at Harvard Peabody Museum. Stolen same night as murder. Security footage shows professional thief. [Claire POV]
Ch 10 — NYC. Meera calls Rothwell in London. Does he know about Brahmi artifacts in American collections? He knows she's hiding something. [Meera POV]
Ch 11 — Cambridge/NYC. Cross reveals: all six artifacts acquired by Charles Wrentham, British East India Company surveyor, Kerala 1838-1852. Sold at London auctions. Purchased by American collectors. [Meera POV]
Ch 12 — NYC. Meera researches Wrentham. His journal described underground passages and "ancient knowledge moved westward." Journal now stolen. [Meera POV]
Ch 13 — NYC. Claire identifies suspect: Victor Kessler. Swiss-American provenance consultant. INTERPOL flagged twice. No charges. [Claire POV]
Ch 14 — NYC. Kessler background: disgraced archaeologist, ETH Zurich. Now the most sought-after provenance researcher in private art world. Grandfather was Nazi art recovery officer. [Meera POV]
Ch 15 — NYC. Webb escalates. Case becomes multi-jurisdictional. Meera lead agent, Claire partner. Political pressure building. [Meera POV]
Ch 16 — DC. Acela to Washington. Smithsonian. Meet Dr. Sarah Lindhurst, Asian Art curator. Nervous. [Meera POV]
Ch 17 — DC. Lindhurst reveals: she's been studying the brass vessel for three years. Hidden compartment with Brahmi inscriptions. She told no one. [Meera POV]
Ch 18 — DC. Meera examines the vessel. Brahmi coordinates — partial, needing other artifacts. Secures as evidence. Lindhurst gives research notes. [Meera POV]
Ch 19 — DC. FBIHQ briefing. Webb's boss asks questions. A senator is making inquiries. The case is attracting attention. [Claire POV]
Ch 20 — NYC. News: Santa Fe art dealer Miguel Herrera found dead. Indian artifact missing. Meera knows it's artifact #5. [Meera POV]
Ch 21 — NYC. Meera visits Eleanor Voss's Chelsea gallery. Voss sold artifact #3 to Hollis and brokered #4 to the Aldridges. "Old money. Very old secrets." [Meera POV]
Ch 22 — NYC. Voss reveals more: Wrentham's artifacts were well-known in the trade a century ago, then forgotten. "Everyone assumed they were decorative." [Meera POV]
Ch 23 — Santa Fe. Desert landscape. Sheriff Ray Aguilar. Herrera's shop — crime scene. Same professional method as Harvard. [Meera POV]
Ch 24 — Santa Fe. Herrera's records: sold a copper cylinder three days before death. Cash buyer. Aguilar traces to Miami shipping company. [Claire POV]
Ch 25 — Santa Fe. Meera calls Priya with new inscriptions from Smithsonian. Priya making progress. Meera's phone log shows calls to India, London. Claire watches. [Meera POV]
Ch 26 — Santa Fe/NYC. Meera calls Rothwell. Needs Wrentham information. Rothwell demands future access. Meera agrees. [Meera POV]
Ch 27 — NYC. Rothwell sends scanned Wrentham letter: "sealed chamber beneath a Portuguese-era structure." Dispersed artifacts for safety. Kept one — the silver bowl — sent it to his sister in Virginia. Meera realizes: six artifacts, a treasure map, someone is killing to complete it. [Meera POV]

## ACT 2: THE HUNT (Days 4-7, Chapters 28-55)

Ch 28 — Miami. Port of Miami. Customs traces container that received Herrera's cylinder. [Claire POV]
Ch 29 — Miami. Container routed through Geneva. Mediterranean Logistics. Registered to a mail drop. Connected to Tanaka. [Meera POV]

Ch 31 — Miami/NYC. Meera calls Tanaka. Tanaka will meet — but only in Geneva. Webb approves 48-hour trip. [Meera POV]
Ch 32 — NYC. Claire insists on coming. Meera blocks her. First real fight. "We're partners." "Not on this." [Claire POV]
Ch 33 — Geneva. The Freeport. Climate-controlled vaults. Tanaka — ice-cold, precise. [Meera POV]
Ch 34 — Geneva. Tanaka shows three artifacts Kessler stored: Harvard plate, Santa Fe cylinder, jade seal. Meera photographs all inscriptions. Kessler is looking for a sixth piece. He's becoming unstable. [Meera POV]
Ch 35 — In transit. Meera flies back. Sends photos to Priya. Five of six inscriptions now available. [Meera POV]
Ch 36 — NYC. Claire is cold but professional. She traced shipping to Aldridge Family Trust. "The Virginia family with the silver bowl paid for Kessler's shipping." [Claire POV]
Ch 37 — NYC. Meera shaken. Priya calls: five inscriptions form partial coordinates. Northeastern US. Sixth artifact provides precise location. [Meera POV]
Ch 38 — NYC. Meera researches coordinates. Newport, Rhode Island. The Newport Tower. Contested origins. Portuguese/Templar theories. Astronomical alignments. [Meera POV]
Ch 39 — Palo Alto. Hollis estate — modern, glass, tech money. Private museum room. Hollis charming but obstructive. [Meera POV]
Ch 40 — Palo Alto. Hollis shows the stele. Brahmi inscriptions. Kessler visited two months ago. Cross calls: dead scholar funded through Aldridge Family Trust. [Meera POV]
Ch 41 — Virginia. Aldridge Estate. Horse country. Autumn leaves. Colonial architecture, manicured grounds. [Meera POV]
Ch 42 — Virginia. William Aldridge. Patrician, silver-haired, dying. Evaluating Meera. "What would you do if you found something extraordinary?" [Meera POV]
Ch 43 — Virginia. Aldridge shows the silver bowl. Too well-preserved for "inherited." He knows what it is. Won't hand it over yet. Photo on desk: young man, 1940s uniform. [Meera POV]
Ch 44 — Virginia. Meera presses. Aldridge deflects. She notices: his knowledge of the artifacts is too specific for a casual collector. Something else is going on. [Meera POV]
Ch 45 — London. Meera flies to see Rothwell. His club — leather, wood, old power. [Meera POV]
Ch 46 — London. Rothwell has the 1847 Wrentham letter. Sixth artifact hidden "in the tower itself, placed by the last keeper of the western chain." [Meera POV]
Ch 47 — London. Rothwell's price: access to whatever Meera finds. Negotiation: he sees, doesn't touch. Committee retains control. [Meera POV]
Ch 48 — London. Rothwell reveals: the Western guardianship passed through Portuguese to British to American hands. The last guardian before the chain broke was Jonathan Aldridge. Family still in Virginia. [Meera POV]
Ch 49 — NYC. Meera returns. Tells Claire part of the truth: Aldridge connected through family legacy. Still no guardianship mention. [Meera POV]
Ch 50 — NYC. Claire shows financial records: Aldridge paid Kessler for three years. Not for theft — for research. "He hired Kessler to find the artifacts." [Claire POV]
Ch 51 — NYC. The realization: Kessler went rogue. When he understood what the artifacts pointed to, he stopped following Aldridge's orders. Harvard and Santa Fe murders were Kessler's operative, not Aldridge's. [Meera POV]
Ch 52 — NYC. Meera pieces it together: Aldridge is the Western guardian. He hired Kessler to locate the artifacts, but Kessler became the threat. Now Kessler has three artifacts and is hunting the rest. [Meera POV]
Ch 53 — NYC. Claire's discovery: Meera's encrypted communications. "Priya Sharma. Rathore. Rothwell. You've been running a parallel investigation." [Claire POV]
Ch 54 — NYC. The confrontation. "I trusted you. I thought we were partners." Meera can't explain without revealing the guardianship. Claire walks away. [Meera POV]
Ch 55 — NYC. Meera alone. Night. The weight of 2,300 years and no one to share it with. She calls her father. [Meera POV]

## ACT 3: THE REPOSITORY (Days 8-10, Chapters 56-83)

Ch 57 — NYC. Priya calls: Ravi hospitalized. Parkinson's final stage. Non-responsive. Meera is 8,000 miles away. [Meera POV]
Ch 58 — NYC. Meera at JFK. Can't reach Pune in time. For the first time since Padmanabhaswamy, she cries. [Meera POV]
Ch 59 — NYC. Claire finds her at JFK. "I'm still angry. But you shouldn't be alone." Partnership not healed, but not broken beyond repair. [Claire POV]
Ch 60 — NYC/Pune. Priya reads Ravi's handwritten letter: "The knowledge protects itself. Be that person." Meera chooses the mission. The hardest choice. [Meera POV]
Ch 61 — Newport, RI. The Tower in morning light. Eight stone pillars. Contested origins. Meera walks around it alone. [Meera POV]
Ch 62 — Newport. Claire joins. Professional. Meera shares what she knows about the Tower's history. "Something beneath this structure. Hidden for five hundred years." [Meera POV]
Ch 63 — Newport. Five artifact inscriptions identify the north pillar. Hairline seam — invisible unless you're looking. [Meera POV]
Ch 64 — Newport. Hidden compartment. Inside: the copper key. Green with patina. Brahmi on the handle. The weight of five hundred years in her hand. [Meera POV]
Ch 65 — Newport. Meera calls Aldridge. "I found it." Silence. "I know. I've known for forty years." He's coming. [Meera POV]
Ch 66 — Newport. Aldridge arrives with his physician and granddaughter Catherine. He's dying — cancer. "The western chain ends with me. I needed someone worthy." [Meera POV]
Ch 67 — Newport. Aldridge's story: Western guardianship from Portuguese to British to American. Five generations of Aldridges. His father barely understood. He spent his life trying to piece it together. [Meera POV]
Ch 68 — Newport. Meera confronts: "You hired Kessler. People died." "I hired him to locate, not to kill. When he understood what the artifacts pointed to, he stopped following my instructions." [Meera POV]
Ch 69 — Newport. Kessler is coming. Aldridge's people track him — Providence, thirty miles away. Danny Vo and two others with him. [Meera POV]
Ch 70 — Newport. FBI tactical team arrives, coordinated by Claire. Armed threat approaching. Meera briefs them without revealing what's beneath. [Claire POV]
Ch 71 — Newport. Meera descends. Copper key fits mechanism in pillar base. Hidden stairway. Aldridge can't go — too frail. "The knowledge is yours now, Meera. Both chains." [Meera POV]
Ch 72 — Newport. The repository. Smaller than Padmanabhaswamy — 3x3 meters. Stone shelves. Manuscripts in Sanskrit, Brahmi, Indus script. A single copper plate in Ashokan Brahmi. [Meera POV]
Ch 73 — Newport. The copper plate: this is the backup. Ashoka sent the most critical knowledge west — medical texts. Surgical techniques. Pharmaceutical formulas. Nerve regeneration. Meera's hands tremble. Parkinson's. Her father. This could have helped him. [Meera POV]
Ch 74 — Newport. Kessler arrives through drainage tunnel. Gunfire above. Claire coordinates tactical response. Meera trapped underground. [Kessler POV]
Ch 75 — Newport. Kessler enters the chamber. "Do you know what this is worth? Not money — influence." Danny Vo sees the manuscripts. Hesitates. [Kessler POV]
Ch 76 — Newport. Danny's breaking point. He didn't sign up to destroy heritage. Kessler turns on him. The standoff turns violent. [Meera POV]
Ch 77 — Newport. Kessler triggers the failsafe. Chamber begins collapsing. Ancient engineering — designed to protect from unauthorized access. Meera must choose. [Meera POV]
Ch 78 — Newport. Meera grabs the copper plate, the key medical manuscript, and the copper key. Runs. Danny Vo is trapped. She hears him scream. The chamber collapses. [Meera POV]
Ch 79 — Newport. Kessler escapes through drainage tunnel. FBI captures him at the exit. Meera emerges, covered in dust, clutching manuscripts. [Claire POV]
Ch 80 — Newport. Hospital. Meera tells Claire the full truth — not everything, but enough. "There's a network of ancient knowledge. I'm one of the protectors." Claire: "That's the craziest thing I've ever heard. And I was in the chamber." [Meera POV]
Ch 81 — NYC. Meera's decision: publish the medical knowledge through Priya's academic channels. Nature. The Lancet. Controlled openness. Priya agrees. [Meera POV]
Ch 82 — NYC. The cost. OPR investigation. Meera resigns. Webb: "You were the best agent I ever had." Meera: "I was a guardian pretending to be an agent." [Meera POV]
Ch 83 — Newport. Winter. Snow on the ground. The Tower sealed — "structural investigation." Meera stands before it. Claire beside her — no badge either. "Webb said if you ever need help, the Art Crime team is available." Two women, no institutional backing. Meera touches her scar. Not anxiety. Purpose. She walks toward whatever comes next. [Meera POV]
