# Market Research — Senior Large-Print Activity Books (KDP)

**Purpose:** evidence base for the 10-book series strategy. Source: marketplace search (Amazon listings via search results + niche publisher sites), June 2026.

**Companion documents:** `PLAN-Senior-Activity-Book-Series.md` (build plan) · `CONTENTS-Senior-Activity-Book-Series.md` (canonical activity counts, difficulty tiers, grid specs, page budget) · `LAYOUT-and-Tooling-Senior-Activity-Book-Series.md` (typography/layout spec + PDF-tool decision) · `ratings.md` (competitive scoring vs top 3).

## 1. Demand & competition (sizing)

| Search query (Amazon) | Results | Read |
|---|---|---|
| "large print activity books for seniors" | 9,000+ | Massive demand, **high competition** |
| "large print word search for seniors" | 6,000+ | Core segment, saturated |
| "large word search books for seniors" | 6,000+ | Saturated |

**Implication:** demand is proven and large, but generic single-type books are saturated. Winning requires **differentiation (themes), price edge, and keyword targeting** — not a head-on collision with the dominant generic incumbents.

## 2. Dominant incumbents to avoid fighting directly
- **Martin Littlewolf** — owns the "Big/Huge Book of Large Print Word Search" slots: "9000 Words – 224 Puzzles", "5000 Words – 224 Themed Puzzles". Both flagged **Best Seller**.
- Bulk multi-packs (12-pack variety, 8×10) compete on volume-per-dollar.
- Niche publishers: PuzzleKingPublishing (Extra Large Print), PuzzlePlayBooks, BookShortlist bundles.
- **Takeaway:** differentiate via themed/nostalgia + variety + the Home Remedies "wow" — not pure word-search volume.
- **Our variety spec:** the exact activity counts and Easy/Medium/Challenger difficulty distribution behind the "180+ Brain Games & Puzzles" hook are defined in `CONTENTS-Senior-Activity-Book-Series.md`. The Easy-skewed, multi-domain mix is what supports the "brain exercise" positioning buyers respond to.

## 3. Bestseller content patterns (what winners actually contain)

| Attribute | Observed pattern |
|---|---|
| **Page count** | 120–224 pp; "huge" titles market 200+ pp |
| **Trim size** | 8.5×11 and 8×10 both standard; 8.5×11 = more writing room + premium feel |
| **Anchor activity** | Word search dominates |
| **Mix** | Variety books (word search + sudoku + mazes + scramble + trivia + coloring) sell strongly as "brain exercises" |
| **Volume hooks** | "224 puzzles", "5000 words", "501 brain exercises", "over 100 puzzles" |
| **Title keywords that convert** | "Large Print", "Extra Large / Jumbo / XXL", puzzle count, "themed", "for Seniors", "perfect gift" |
| **Layout** | Full solutions grouped at back; high-contrast black on white; large grids |

Concrete bestseller anatomy examples:
- *Large Print Activity Book for Seniors* — 200pp: 80 word searches + 40 sudoku + 30 mazes + 4 coloring pages.
- *Memory Games for Seniors* — "Large Print XXL … 501 Brain Exercises".
- *40 JUMBO-PRINT Puzzles* — each word search spreads over **two pages** (extra-large grid).

## 4. Two distinct sub-markets

### A. Active seniors / brain health (TARGET — largest volume)
Variety puzzles, nostalgia themes, gift positioning. Larger, more competitive. This is where our series competes.

### B. Dementia / Alzheimer's cognitive care (different product, not our target)
Simpler, photo/reminiscence + very easy puzzles, low page count (~35–80pp), strong **emotional gift** market, far less puzzle-skill competition.
- Examples: *Memories: Memory Lane 1950s/1960s for Seniors with Dementia* (Mighty Oak Books — also 1940s, Movie Stars, Classic TV, Psalms); *1950s Memory Lane* (35pp, short paragraphs, illustrations); *Car Picture Book for Seniors* (40 photos, iconic cars 1950s–70s).
- Etsy has an active handmade segment here.
- **Note:** we are NOT building picture/reminiscence books; flagged as a possible future line only.

## 5. Nostalgia is a proven differentiator
Nostalgia (1950s/60s/70s) recurs across both sub-markets:
- *1960s Nostalgia Activity Book for Seniors* — retro puzzles + trivia + illustrations.
- *Searching for Nostalgia: Word Search … (1950s–1980s)* — active-adults positioning.
- Decade-themed books (1950s, 1960s) and topic nostalgia (Classic Cars, Classic TV) both appear repeatedly.
**Takeaway:** decade + topic nostalgia themes are validated demand; basis for our 10-theme lineup.

## 6. Series & repeat-purchase mechanics
- "Volume" numbering, year editions ("2026 edition"), and multi-packs all appear and drive repeat buys.
- A branded series where each book is a different theme creates collector behavior + widens keyword surface area.

## 7. Implications for our strategy
1. Compete on **differentiation (themed nostalgia) + variety + a unique Home Remedies page**, not raw word-search volume.
2. Match premium **content** quality (variety mix, large print, full solutions) but **undercut on price**.
3. Use **themes** to each own a keyword cluster; use a **branded series** for repeat purchases.
4. Stay in the **active-seniors / brain-health** segment (largest volume); defer dementia-care picture books.
5. Cover must win the **thumbnail** (where buyers actually decide) — large "LARGE PRINT" + puzzle-count badges.

## 8. Pricing economics benchmark (KDP paperback, 8.5×11 B&W)
- Print cost = KDP **Large Trim** (8.5×11) B&W: **$1.00 fixed + $0.017/page** → ~185pp ≈ **$4.15**. (8.5×11 is large trim because >9″ tall.)
- Royalty = (List × 0.60) − Print. Floor ≈ $6.91.
- Competitor band: generic single-type ~$5.99–$7.99; premium variety ~$9.99–$12.99.
- Our move: **list $10.99** (royalty ~$2.45), **launch $8.99** (~$1.25). KDP does **not** sell paperbacks on Amazon.in — low ₹ books there are offset-printed, not POD.
- See full royalty table in the plan file.

## 9. Risks surfaced by research
- Generic-incumbent saturation → mitigate via themes/price/wow.
- Single broken puzzle → 1-star risk → mitigate via solver verification.
- Medical-advice exposure (remedies page) → mitigate via folk-wisdom framing + disclaimer (no "Ayurveda" wording).
