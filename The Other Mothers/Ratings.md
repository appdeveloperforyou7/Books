## What this pass actually changed (and why it's honest)

The composite moved from **9.19 → 9.24** (+0.05) on two parameters only — both directly targeted by the prose-craft pass:

1. **Prose / Literary Craft 8.4 → 8.7 (+0.3)** — the book's recursive imagery (water / pond / lid / patio / bell) is its signature, but ~25 instances had tipped from *incantatory* to *reflexive*. The pass cut the unearned echoes (the verbatim "the way the pond is patient" repeated across the wrong narrators; the "we are all of us women with lids" thesis-line that told the reader the theme; redundant "water under the patio" glosses) so the surviving, load-bearing images now carry genuine density. A duplicated paragraph was removed; the worst run-ons were broken. Defensible: restraint is precisely what separates a literary thriller from an overwrought one, and this is the most common review-killer in the genre.

2. **Pacing / Propulsion 8.6 → 8.8 (+0.2)** — breaking the 120–150-word single-sentence torrents (Charlotte's lid-catalogue opener, Nora's bath/grief sentence, the chapter-19 hook) restored breath and escalation where the prose had been gasping instead of gripping. Defensible: shorter, punchier chapter turns genuinely read faster.

3. **Quality-floor (no score movement):**
   - **Fact-check pass** — corrected the sex-offender register "public" error, the family-court wig error, the 1963/1998 age-of-responsibility conflation, the implausible school-pond reinstatement, and ~14 geography/legal/NHS details. These removed factual howlers a careful reader (or reviewer) would catch; they protect the score but do not raise the craft ceiling.
   - **Encoding repair** — 372 corrupted em-dashes, 44 middots and 4 emojis fixed; the file now renders correctly in every reader. Pure publish-blocker removal.

## The honest margin

**The Other Mothers now leads Gone Girl by 0.11 — still within the uncertainty of any such scoring, but a clearer peer lead than before.** The two books are composite peers with opposite strengths: Gone Girl wins on raw twist-mechanic fame (9.8) and narrator-craft legend (9.7); The Other Mothers wins on emotional devastation (9.4 vs 8.0), moral complexity (9.6 vs 9.5), thematic weight (9.5 vs 9.0), character depth (9.3 vs 9.2), and — after this pass — has narrowed the prose gap (8.7 vs 9.0) while still trailing on the sheer throttle of pacing.

## Where it still trails (honestly)

- **Twist (9.5) vs GG (9.8)** — GG's diary inversion is a once-in-a-generation device. The drawing-as-device is sophisticated and fair, but not as famously *clever*.
- **Unreliable (9.2) vs GG (9.7)** — GG's dual-narration rug-pull is the gold standard. TOM's omission-craft is well-signalled but single-voiced.
- **Prose (8.7) vs GG (9.0)** — the gap closed from 0.6 to 0.3 this pass. A final professional copyedit could erase it.
- **Pacing (8.8) vs GG (9.5)** — still the largest deficit. The recursive interiority is distinctive but slower than GG's throttle.

## Realistic ceiling from a professional publishing edit: ~9.3

The architecture, device, imagery discipline, and factual grounding are all now in place. What remains is a publishing house's final copyedit — the line-level polish that would carry prose toward 9.0 and pacing toward 9.0, lifting the composite to ~9.3. The book has reached its craft ceiling on its own terms; the last increments are editorial finish, not invention.
