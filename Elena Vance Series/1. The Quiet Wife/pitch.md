# PITCH — THE QUIET WIFE
## The commercial package (what sells the book)

---

## TITLE
**THE QUIET WIFE** *(primary — locked)*

Why it works: fits the proven "The [adjective] Wife/Woman" pattern that dominates the genre (*The Silent Patient, The Housemaid, The Woman in the Window*). "Quiet" does double duty — she's underestimated, and the secret is quiet until it isn't.

*Alternates (if A/B-tested for ads):* **Every Lie He Told** (stronger hook for the domestic-thriller crowd) · **The Gap** (leaner, mystery-coded).

---

## ONE-LINE LOGLINE
An executive coach who reads people for a living discovers her husband is in danger — and uses nothing but her phone and her instincts to out-read the tech billionaire trying to destroy them.

---

## TAGLINE
*She reads people for a living. This time, the room reads back.*

---

## BACK-COVER COPY (conversion-grade)

> Elena Vance reads people the way other people read signs.
>
> She's an executive coach — the woman billionaires hire to teach them how to control their anger, their egos, themselves. She can tell you which executive is about to quit before they've told their spouse. She prides herself on one thing above all: *she is never, ever blindsided.*
>
> Then her husband's phone lights up at dinner, and he angles the screen away from her. For the first time in seven years.
>
> Arthur is lying about where he goes at night. A neighbour is drilling through their shared wall. And somewhere in the public records that nobody reads, someone is quietly stealing an entire city block — and Elena's apartment is the key to all of it.
>
> Armed with nothing but her phone, her instincts, and the one skill her enemies dismiss as "soft," Elena will do the only thing she has ever done: read the room.
>
> But the room is watching her back. And the assistant she trusts to help her think belongs to the man who wants her gone.
>
> **Some doors are closed to protect you. Hers were closed to keep her from seeing what was on the other side.**

---

## OPENING LINE (the first thing a sample-reader sees)
*"I teach powerful men how to behave, so I notice the exact second they stop trying."*

(Hook + voice + profession in one line. Establishes her superpower and her edge immediately.)

---

## THE USP (why it sells — the one-sentence pitch to a publisher/ads)
*A modern domestic thriller for the AI age: a phone-addicted Everywoman becomes a detective using public records, an AI assistant, and her people-reading skills — and discovers the AI she trusts is the villain's. Part page-turner, part how-to-survive-the-modern-world, with a multimedia format (texts, maps, notes, AI chats) that reads like a thriller set inside your own phone.*

**Three things no competitor has, together:**
1. The **phone-life multimedia format** — reads like it's set inside your phone (the most relatable setting of 2026).
2. The **compromised-AI twist** — the assistant she trusts belongs to the villain. Timely, frightening, fresh.
3. **OSINT for civilians** — Elena learns real open-source investigation on the page, *with* the reader (empowerment fantasy: "I could do this").

---

## COMP TITLES (positioning / Amazon categories)
- *"The Last Thing He Told Me"* meets *"Searching"* — wife investigates husband's secret; screen-life format.
- For fans of *Freida McFadden* (pace + accessibility), *Alex Michaelides* (the big twist), *Laura Dave* (marriage + mystery).
- **Categories:** Psychological Thriller · Domestic Suspense · Amateur Sleuth · Women Sleuths.

---

## TARGET READER
Women 28–60 who buy 3+ thrillers a month on Kindle, read on their phones, loved *The Housemaid / The Silent Patient / Verity*, and are drawn to a relatable, competent heroine they can root for. The "smart ordinary woman wins" fantasy.

---

## SERIES PITCH
**Book 1 — THE QUIET WIFE:** Elena becomes a detective; takes down a billionaire. *(Origin story.)*
**The Elena Vance Series:** each book, a new case walks in through her coaching practice or her consulting work with a private-intelligence firm. Self-contained mysteries; a recurring season-arc adversary (the "they" above Cross). *Franchise DNA: repeatable engine + a heroine readers return for.*

---

## THE FIVE THINGS AN AD / BLURB CAN HOOK ON
1. *"Her husband's phone lit up at dinner — and he hid the screen."* (the inciting dread)
2. *"The AI she trusted to help her belonged to the man hunting her."* (the killer twist — teaser)
3. *"She read people for a living. He believed people were just data. Only one of them was right."* (the thematic duel)
4. *"A neighbour who didn't exist. A building that was stolen. An apartment that was the key to everything."* (the conspiracy pull)
5. *"In this state, I only need one person's permission to record this. Mine."* (the climax line — quotable)

---

## HONEST SALES RISKS (for the author to know)
- **The multimedia format** is a differentiator AND a risk — some thriller readers want pure prose. Mitigation: keep prose ~70% dominant; lead ads with the *story/hook*, not the format.
- **"Executive coach"** is a slightly niche profession — the father-wound and marriage must carry the relatability, not the job.
- The book lives or dies on the **opening 3 chapters** (the sample) and the **AI twist** landing. Both must be airtight.
