# CHAPTER FIVE
## r/TrueCrimeSleuths

I find the forum at 2 a.m. on Sunday, because of course I do, because the woman who controls things has lost control and the woman who has replaced her does all her thinking in the small hours with the phone glowing on her chest like a nightlight.

I wasn't looking for a forum. I was looking, desperately and a little pathetically, for *how to follow someone without them noticing*, and the internet, which has an answer for everything, had led me past a dozen grifting "private investigator" websites and into a corner of a message board where ordinary people — amateurs, hobbyists, the kind of people who solve things for the love of it — talked to each other like human beings.

The board was called r/TrueCrimeSleuths. It had 400,000 members and a pinned post that read, in part: *"This is not a movie. Nobody here is Jason Bourne. If you want to feel like a spy, go roleplay somewhere else. If you want to actually find things out, sit down."*

I sat down.

I made an account. I called myself *throwaway_v2781* because I am forty-one years old and I do not know how to be anonymous convincingly. And at 2:14 a.m., lying in the dark beside my sleeping husband, I wrote the truest thing I'd written all week.

```
💬 r/TrueCrimeSleuths — u/throwaway_v2781 — 2:14 AM
[HELP] I tried to follow someone today and it was a disaster.

I'm not a PI or a cop or anything. I'm a normal person.
Someone I love is lying to me about where they go. I
thought if I just... followed them, I'd see something.
I got made in about four blocks. Spilled coffee on a
stranger. They definitely saw me. It was bad.

How do people actually do this? Please don't say "hire
someone." I can't. I need to do it myself. What am I
missing?
```

I posted it. Then I lay there in the dark and felt the particular loneliness of a person who has just confessed to the internet.

Nothing happened for eleven minutes. Then a notification.

The first reply was from someone called *soccermom_83* and it said *lol same, following my ex was how I learned I have asthma*, which was not, strictly, useful, but which made me feel briefly less alone.

The second reply was from an account with no avatar and a username that was just letters and numbers, and it was the reply that would, eventually, rearrange my entire life. I didn't know that then. I just knew it was the first answer that sounded like it came from someone who actually knew something.

```
💬 REPLY — u/GreyHat_4 — 2:33 AM
Stop following him.
```

That was it. Two lines. No sympathy, no encouragement, no *oh no honey*. Just: stop.

I should have been offended. Instead I felt, absurdly, the way I feel when a client finally says the true thing in a session — that snap of relief when someone stops being polite and starts being useful. I sat up in bed. I typed back.

```
💬 u/throwaway_v2781 — 2:35 AM
Ok... then what DO I do? I need to know where he's going.
```

```
💬 u/GreyHat_4 — 2:37 AM
You don't need to know where he's going.
You need to know WHY he's lying about where he's going.
Those are different problems. The first one gets you
caught on a subway grate. The second one you can do from
your couch.
```

I stared at the screen. Beside me, Arthur breathed in and out, slow and even, and I was talking to a stranger about him in the dark, and the stranger was, impossibly, the only person in a week who'd said anything that made the knot in my chest loosen.

```
💬 u/throwaway_v2781 — 2:39 AM
How do I do that from my couch?
```

There was a pause. When the reply came, it was longer, and it had the careful tone of someone who'd decided, against their better judgement, to help.

```
💬 u/GreyHat_4 — 2:44 AM
Look. I don't know you. I don't know your situation.
But you wrote "someone I love is lying to me," and you
tried to fix it by putting on a costume and walking
behind them, and that tells me you're smart and scared
and you watch too much TV.

Real finding-out is boring. It's paperwork. It's public
records and timestamps and receipts. It's the stuff
nobody glances at because it looks like nothing. That's
the whole point — it's hiding in plain sight because
plain sight is where nobody looks.

You don't tail a person. You read a person. You read
their patterns. Where they say they are vs where the
data says they are. The gap between those two things is
the answer you want.

What do you actually know right now? List it. No
feelings. Just facts.
```

I lay back on the pillow. The phone lit the ceiling. Arthur slept.

And I did something I hadn't done since my father left — I let a stranger tell me what to do, because I had run out of ways to tell myself.

```
NOTE — 2:51 AM — bed, A asleep
GreyHat. Stranger on a forum. First person to make sense.
RULE: don't chase. Read the gap between what he says
and what the data says.
Tomorrow: list the facts. No feelings. Just facts.
```

I didn't sleep. But for the first time in a week, the alarm in my chest wasn't screaming. It was listening.
