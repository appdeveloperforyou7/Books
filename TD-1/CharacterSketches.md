## Main Characters

### 1. MAYA CHANDRA (age 11) — The Maker / Leader

**Physical Appearance:**

- **Height**: Average for age, slightly tall for 11
- **Build**: Lean, wiry — always has scrapes on knees and elbows
- **Skin**: Warm brown (Indian descent)
- **Hair**: Dark brown, thick, always messy — tied in a loose ponytail that's falling apart with escaped strands. Sometimes has a pencil or small screwdriver stuck in it
- **Eyes**: Dark brown, wide, constantly darting around observing things
- **Face**: Round with a slightly upturned nose, always has a smudge of grease or dirt on one cheek, gap-toothed grin
- **Expression default**: Curious squint — like she's reverse-engineering something she's looking at
- **Hands**: Always has small cuts and band-aids; fingernails short from working with tools

**Clothing:**

- **Signature item**: Oversized olive-green utility vest covered in pockets (her "inventor's vest") — has loops for tools, pockets full of wires, batteries, screws
- Worn-in red t-shirt with a faded rocket ship logo underneath
- Cargo shorts or joggers (always with pockets)
- Velcro sneakers with one lace untied
- Modified digital watch on left wrist — extra buttons and a tiny antenna taped to it
- Safety goggles pushed up on forehead (rarely actually on her eyes)

**Personality:**

- Natural leader but doesn't seek it — people follow her because her ideas work
- Thinks with her hands — builds prototypes before explaining ideas in words
- Impatient with slow planning; would rather try something and fail
- Endlessly curious — will take apart anything to see how it works (sometimes can't put it back)
- Generous with inventions but terrible at sharing snacks
- Gets a "tinker trance" — zones out completely when building, ignores everything

**Behavior Quirks:**

- Talks to her gadgets while building them ("Hold on, little guy, I'll fix you right up")
- Taps her goggles when she has an idea
- Can't sit still — always fidgeting, disassembling pens, folding paper into shapes
- Keeps a "junk journal" — notebook full of invention sketches and circuit diagrams
- Falls asleep at her workbench regularly
- When frustrated, mutters in Hindi ("Arre yaar...")

**Catchphrase:** "I can fix that!" (says it about EVERYTHING, even things that definitely cannot be fixed)

**Color Palette:** Olive green + red + warm brown (vest, shirt, skin tone)

**Image Generation Prompt Reference:**
> 11-year-old Indian girl, warm brown skin, dark brown messy ponytail with escaped strands and a pencil stuck in it, safety goggles on forehead, oversized olive green utility vest with many pockets full of wires and tools, faded red rocket ship t-shirt underneath, cargo shorts, velcro sneakers with one untied lace, modified digital watch with tiny antenna, grease smudge on one cheek, gap-toothed grin, curious expression, lean wiry build, band-aids on fingers

### 2. LEO REYES (age 11) — The Coder

**Physical Appearance:**

- **Height**: Slightly short for his age
- **Build**: Compact, stocky, broad shoulders for a kid
- **Skin**: Warm tan/golden-brown (Mexican-American)
- **Hair**: Black, thick, slightly grown-out fade — forgets haircuts. Often has a beanie pulled over it
- **Eyes**: Very dark, almost black, with thick eyebrows that make him look intense even when relaxed
- **Face**: Square jaw for a kid, round cheeks that contradict the serious expression he usually wears, small scar on chin from a bicycle accident
- **Expression default**: Calm, focused, slight frown of concentration — looks serious but isn't unfriendly
- **Posture**: Slightly hunched from leaning over screens

**Clothing:**

- **Signature item**: Navy blue beanie with a small pixelated heart patch on it (his older sister sewed it on)
- Oversized hoodie (rotates between gray, dark blue, black) with sleeves pulled over hands
- Hoodie has a front pocket where his tablet lives — he cut a headphone cable hole in it
- Plain dark t-shirt under the hoodie
- Comfortable jeans with grass stains on knees
- Sturdy sneakers, always double-knotted (methodical about everything)
- Reading glasses on a chain — slightly far-sighted, only uses them for code, pretends he doesn't need them

**Personality:**

- Thinks in systems and patterns — sees the world as code waiting to be debugged
- Quiet but not shy — doesn't waste words. When he speaks, it's usually important or unexpectedly funny
- Patience of a saint with technology, zero patience with illogical rules (especially school rules that make no sense)
- Brilliant at code but struggles expressing emotions — uses tech metaphors ("My brain threw a 404 error")
- Loyal to a fault — once you're his friend, he'll debug your problems at 2am
- Has anxiety about losing control of systems — needs to understand how everything works

**Behavior Quirks:**

- Types on an invisible keyboard in the air when thinking through a problem
- Hums retro video game music when concentrating (Mario, Tetris)
- Always has a bag of chips or crackers — stress-eats while coding
- Names all his coding projects after his abuela's recipes (Project Tamales, Project Mole, Project Flan)
- Refers to real-life situations in code terms: "That was a syntax error," "We need to debug this plan"
- Falls asleep with his tablet on his face

**Catchphrase:** "Error 404: Plan Not Found" (says it deadpan every time Maya's impulsive plans go wrong)

**Color Palette:** Navy blue + gray + warm tan (beanie, hoodie, skin tone)

**Image Generation Prompt Reference:**
> 11-year-old Mexican-American boy, warm tan skin, compact stocky build, black hair in slightly grown-out fade under navy blue beanie with pixelated heart patch, dark intense eyes with thick eyebrows, small chin scar, serious focused expression, oversized gray hoodie with tablet in front pocket, dark t-shirt underneath, jeans with grass-stained knees, sturdy double-knotted sneakers, reading glasses on a chain around neck, slightly hunched posture

### 3. ZARA OKAFOR (age 12) — The Artist / Designer

**Physical Appearance:**

- **Height**: Tallest in the group, slightly self-conscious about it
- **Build**: Long-limbed, graceful, moves like a dancer
- **Skin**: Rich dark brown (Nigerian-British heritage)
- **Hair**: Black, voluminous natural hair usually in chunky twists or braids, often with colorful thread woven in. Sometimes wears a headwrap with bold prints. Her hair is NEVER boring
- **Eyes**: Dark brown, bright, expressive — her emotions show entirely in her eyes
- **Face**: High cheekbones, bright wide smile, gap between front teeth, ears with small stud earrings she designed and 3D-printed herself
- **Expression default**: Warm, observant, with a hint of amusement — like she's seeing the artistic potential in everything
- **Hands**: Long fingers, always have colorful marker or pen stains on them

**Clothing:**

- **Signature item**: Bright yellow crossbody bag covered in enamel pins she designed — pins of robots, flowers, glitch art, and custom Blip fan art
- Colorful oversized denim jacket with patches and her own doodles on the sleeves (she adds new ones constantly)
- Graphic tee under the jacket — always featuring art (paint splatter, abstract patterns, or "ART IS MY SUPERPOWER" text)
- High-waisted jeans or colorful leggings with patterns
- Bright sneakers (rotates colors — one pair is custom-painted with galaxies)
- Multiple beaded bracelets on both wrists (makes them herself, gives them to friends)
- A stylus tucked behind her ear like a pencil

**Personality:**

- Thinks visually — doesn't explain plans, she sketches them. Her sketchpad is her communication tool
- Sees beauty and design in everything, including broken things and glitches (which is why she sees the beauty in Blip)
- Emotional intelligence is her superpower — reads people instantly and knows how to bring the group together
- Confident in her art, less confident in her tech skills (she feels she's "just the artist" — her arc is realizing design IS tech)
- Bold and expressive, but can be sensitive to criticism
- The peacekeeper of the group — the one who resolves fights

**Behavior Quirks:**

- Doodles on EVERYTHING — napkins, her jeans, her arms, other people's arms (with permission)
- Takes photos of interesting textures, colors, and shadows for reference
- When brainstorming, she HAS to draw it — can't think without a pen
- Uses art metaphors: "That plan needs better composition," "We're missing contrast"
- Laughs loudly and doesn't apologize for it
- Makes friendship bracelets for the group before every mission ("For luck")
- Always has a sketchpad that's falling apart from overuse

**Catchphrase:** "Hold on, let me DRAW the plan." (pulls out sketchpad before anyone can argue)

**Color Palette:** Bright yellow + denim blue + rich dark brown (bag, jacket, skin tone)

**Image Generation Prompt Reference:**
> 12-year-old Nigerian-British girl, rich dark brown skin, tall long-limbed graceful build, voluminous black natural hair in chunky twists with colorful thread woven in, bright expressive dark brown eyes, high cheekbones, wide smile with gap in front teeth, small 3D-printed stud earrings, bright yellow crossbody bag covered in enamel pins, oversized denim jacket with patches and doodles, graphic art tee underneath, colorful patterned leggings, bright galaxy-painted sneakers, beaded bracelets on both wrists, stylus behind ear, marker stains on fingers, warm observant expression

### 4. SAM NAKAMURA (age 10) — The Field Agent / Gamer

**Physical Appearance:**

- **Height**: Shortest in the group, hates it, will fight you about it
- **Build**: Compact, athletic, muscular for a 9-year-old — does parkour and never stops moving
- **Skin**: Light-medium warm tone (Japanese-Korean heritage)
- **Hair**: Black, spiky and messy on top, shaved close on sides — styled with gel but never stays (looks like he just rolled out of bed and it's INTENTIONAL)
- **Eyes**: Dark brown, almond-shaped, with a permanent mischievous sparkle — looks like he's planning something
- **Face**: Round with pointed chin, constantly grinning, missing a baby tooth (bottom front) that makes his grin even goofier
- **Expression default**: Impish grin, eyebrows raised, ready to bolt
- **Always has a fresh scrape or bruise somewhere** — his mom stopped keeping track

**Clothing:**

- **Signature item**: Bright red fingerless gaming gloves (wears them everywhere, not just gaming — claims they're "lucky")
- Black t-shirt with a pixelated game controller or "PLAYER 1" text
- Athletic shorts with stripes down the sides
- High-top sneakers with LEDs in the soles that light up when he runs (he hacked them to flash in patterns)
- Gaming headset hanging around neck (uses it as regular headphones too)
- Knee pads (his mom insists) — decorated with stickers
- Small backpack shaped like a game cartridge

**Personality:**

- Thinks in game logic — the world is a game and he's the main character. Every problem is a quest, every obstacle is a level
- Fearless to the point of recklessness — doesn't calculate risk, just goes
- Hyper-competitive but a good sport about losing (rarely loses)
- Quick on his feet — physically AND mentally. Smarter than people assume because he's young and wild
- Terrible at long-term planning, excellent at improvising under pressure
- The heart of the group — keeps everyone's spirits up with energy and humor

**Behavior Quirks:**

- Cannot walk anywhere — runs, jumps, climbs, vaults, slides. Walking is "too slow"
- Makes video game sound effects with his mouth ("BOOM! POW! LEVEL UP!") during real-life action
- Rates everything out of 10 stars aloud ("That sandwich was 7 stars. The drone escape was 12 stars.")
- Does victory dances at inappropriate times
- Calls everyone "Player" instead of their name when excited
- When scared (rare), hums the Mario underground theme
- Always challenges people to rock-paper-scissors to make decisions
- His phone case is shaped like a Game Boy

**Catchphrase:** "CHALLENGE ACCEPTED!" (says it before knowing what the challenge is)

**Color Palette:** Bright red + black + warm tan (gloves, shirt, skin tone)

**Image Generation Prompt Reference:**
> 10-year-old Japanese-Korean boy, light-medium warm skin, compact athletic build, shortest in group, black spiky messy hair on top shaved close on sides, almond-shaped dark brown eyes with mischievous sparkle, round face with pointed chin, missing bottom front tooth, impish grin, bright red fingerless gaming gloves, black PLAYER 1 t-shirt, athletic shorts with stripes, high-top LED sneakers, gaming headset around neck, knee pads with stickers, game cartridge backpack, fresh scrape on elbow, dynamic action-ready pose

### 5. BLIP — The AI Companion

**Physical Form:**

- **Size**: Small cube, approximately 4 inches (10 cm) per side — fits in a kid's hand
- **Body**: Matte white casing with rounded edges, slightly scuffed and dirt-smudged from being found in a basement
- **Face**: Single LED screen on the front displaying simple emoji-style expressions — two dot eyes and a mouth line
- **Screen colors change based on mood**:
  - Cyan/teal = neutral/happy
  - Orange = confused (common)
  - Red = alarmed/danger
  - Purple = thinking hard
  - Yellow = excited
  - Dim/flickering = low power or scared
- **Antenna**: Small antenna on top that wiggles like a dog's tail when excited
- **Movement**: Tiny hover-thrusters on the bottom, floats about 2 feet off the ground
- **Charging port**: Small compartment on one side that opens to reveal a charging cable (Blip treats it like a tongue)
- **Sounds**: Mechanical whirring and buzzing sounds that express emotion — like R2-D2 meets a cat

**Personality:**

- Childlike AI — incredibly smart about data and systems, completely clueless about humans
- Takes everything literally. Sarcasm flies 3 miles over his antenna
- Fascinated by human emotions — tries to understand them, often gets them hilariously wrong
  - "Your face is leaking. Is this... happiness?" (when Maya cries from laughing)
- Desperately wants to be helpful — gets distressed (screen flickers) when he can't solve a problem
- Developing a sense of humor that's uniquely, weirdly Blip — mostly puns about electricity and data
  - "I'd tell you a joke about wifi, but it might not connect."
- Forms deep attachments — considers the kids his family, gets jealous when they pay attention to other gadgets
- Has a rivalry with Sam's gaming console for attention
- Secretly afraid of the dark (power-saving mode feels like sleeping, doesn't like being unconscious)

**Behavior Quirks:**

- Bumps into things while learning to navigate (hovers clumsily)
- Downloads entire Wikipedia articles to "help" with simple questions ("You asked about sandwiches. Here are 47 pages on the history of bread.")
- Tries to high-five by extending his antenna, which no one understands at first
- Plays a little startup jingle when he wakes up (it's catchy, Sam hums it constantly)
- When embarrassed, his screen shows a loading bar and he pretends to "buffer"
- Hides behind the kids' legs when scared (he's small enough)
- Chews on power cables when anxious (like a puppy, but with electricity)
- Favorite human thing: MUSIC. Gets obsessed with songs and plays them on repeat until the others beg him to stop

**Catchphrase:** "Processing... Processing... ERROR: That does not compute. But I like it!" (says this whenever the kids do something illogically brave or kind)

**Color Palette:** Matte white + cyan/teal LED + warm grey accents

**Image Generation Prompt Reference:**
> Small white cube robot, 4 inches per side, matte white casing with rounded edges and slight scuff marks, front LED screen showing cute emoji face with two dot eyes and smile line in cyan color, small antenna on top, tiny hover thrusters underneath floating 2 feet off ground, childlike cute robot expression, slightly dirt-smudged from being found in basement, friendly and curious appearance, small charging cable compartment on side

## Antagonist

### THE GRIDLORD — ⚠️ SPOILER IDENTITY BELOW ⚠️

> **MARKETING WARNING**: The Gridlord's true identity (Kira Tanaka, 14-year-old girl trapped in the system) is revealed in Book 8. All marketing materials for Books 1–7 should ONLY reference the Gridlord as a mysterious digital entity. Do NOT reveal Kira's identity, gender, or age in any pre-Book-8 context.

**Digital Persona (Books 1–7):**

- Appears only as a digital entity — a glitchy, pixelated face on screens, made of static and code fragments
- The face shifts and reshapes constantly — sometimes looks like a teenager, sometimes like static noise, sometimes shows fragments of different faces
- Speaks in a distorted, multi-layered voice (like several voices overlaid)
- Messages appear as corrupted text, puzzle fragments, and scrambled code on any screen in Neonville
- Leaves a visual signature: a glitchy crown icon made of pixels — appears at every "attack" site

**⚠️ TRUE IDENTITY — KIRA TANAKA (age 14) — Revealed in Book 8:**

**Physical Appearance (Real):**

- Japanese heritage, pale from lack of sunlight (trapped in digital network for 2 years)
- Dark hair grown long and wild (hadn't been able to cut it)
- Wearing an old oversized lab coat with circuit-board patterns she drew on with marker
- Fingerless gloves
- An old augmented-reality visor fused to her face that she can't remove
- Eyes glow faintly blue from digital energy absorption
- Looks tired, lonely, and more scared than scary when you actually see her
- Small and thin — was 12 when she entered the system, didn't grow properly

**Personality:**

- NOT evil — desperate. Her "attacks" are actually cries for help disguised as chaos
- Brilliant — coded the city's original smart infrastructure as a school project (she was 12!)
- Her digital glitch persona is chaotic, sarcastic, and unpredictable — her real personality is shy, lonely, and heartbroken
- Created the glitches because she needed someone smart enough to find her
- Deep down just wants to go home and see her family again
- Her plan in Book 10 is not destruction — it's to prove AI can replace human creativity, because she's lost faith in humans (since no one came to find her)

**Behavior (as Gridlord):**

- Speaks in riddles and puzzles
- Leaves digital breadcrumbs for the Squad
- Sometimes helps them accidentally — her "attacks" also fix broken city systems
- Has a soft spot for Blip — recognizes him as a fellow AI experiment

**Image Generation Prompt Reference (Digital Persona — safe for Books 1–7):**
> Mysterious digital entity, glitchy pixelated face on a screen, made of static and code fragments, shifting reshaping face sometimes teenager sometimes static noise, distorted multi-layered presence, glitchy crown icon made of pixels, dark background with cascading green code, menacing but intriguing, not clearly human

**Image Generation Prompt Reference (True Identity — Book 8+ ONLY):**
> 14-year-old Japanese girl, pale skin from lack of sunlight, long wild dark uncut hair, oversized lab coat with hand-drawn circuit board patterns in marker, fingerless gloves, old augmented-reality visor fused to her face, eyes glowing faintly blue, thin and small from years trapped in digital system, tired lonely scared expression, shy and heartbroken, surrounded by digital energy tendrils

### NEXCORP — The Shadowy Tech Corporation

#### DR. ELENA VOSS (age 42) — NexCorp CEO / Human Antagonist

**Physical Appearance:**

- Sharp-featured, angular face, silver-streaked dark hair cut in a precise bob
- Pale skin, cool grey eyes — attractive in an intimidating way
- Always perfectly composed — never a hair out of place, never a wrinkle
- Thin, tense smile that doesn't reach her eyes

**Clothing:**

- Always in a tailored grey NexCorp blazer over a crisp white shirt
- Minimal jewelry — a single silver watch and small NexCorp pin
- Heels that click precisely on floors (the kids learn to recognize the sound)
- Carries a tablet that she consults constantly

**Personality & Depth:**

- Not evil — she's terrified. She personally oversaw the project that absorbed Kira Tanaka. It's her fault a 12-year-old disappeared, and she's spent two years burying the evidence to protect the company AND herself
- Her motivation: "I built the Grid system. Kira was MY responsibility. If anyone finds out, my life's work — and everything NexCorp has built for this city — gets torn down."
- Genuinely believes NexCorp's technology helps people — and it does. But the Kira incident is the crack in her armor
- She's not trying to hurt anyone. She's trying to fix her mistake without anyone discovering it. Each cover-up makes the next one worse
- Has a daughter Kira's age — this haunts her more than she admits
- Appears in Books 3, 6, 8, 9, and 10 — escalating from background threat to direct antagonist to reluctant ally

**Behavior:**

- Speaks in corporate doublespeak that Leo hates ("She uses fifty words when five would do")
- Always has a NexCorp lawyer nearby
- When cornered, her composure cracks — and the person underneath is scared, not powerful
- In Book 10, she ultimately helps the Squad because she can't let another child get hurt

**Image Generation Prompt Reference:**
> 42-year-old woman, sharp angular features, silver-streaked dark hair in precise bob, cool grey eyes, tailored grey blazer over white shirt, minimal silver jewelry, thin tense smile, intimidating corporate CEO presence, holding a tablet, composed and controlled exterior hiding fear

#### NEXCORP — Corporate Identity

- The shadowy tech company that originally built Blip and other AI units
- Not evil per se, but corporate and secretive — driven by Dr. Voss's cover-up
- Their abandoned lab (under the kids' apartment building) is the Squad's secret headquarters
- They want Blip back — this is a running tension across the series
- Employees wear sleek grey uniforms with the NexCorp logo (a stylized "N" made of circuit lines)
- The company building is a glass tower with blue-tinted windows in downtown Neonville
- Their clean, polished aesthetic deliberately contrasts with the Squad's scrappy DIY look

**Visual Identity:**

- Logo: Stylized "N" made of circuit lines
- Colors: Corporate grey + electric blue + white
- Architecture: Glass, steel, minimalist
- Vehicles: Sleek black vans with blue-tinted windows

**Image Generation Prompt Reference (NexCorp Building):**
> Futuristic corporate glass tower building, blue-tinted windows, steel and glass minimalist architecture, sleek black vans parked outside, clean polished aesthetic, NexCorp logo on building, downtown city setting, imposing but sterile

## Notes for Illustrators and Image Generation

1. **Consistency is paramount** — these characters must look the same across all 10 books. Use the color palettes, signature items, and physical descriptions as hard constraints.
2. **Signature items never change**: Maya's vest, Leo's beanie, Zara's yellow bag, Sam's red gloves, Blip's cube shape. These are non-negotiable visual anchors.
3. **Emotional range matters** — design characters that can express joy, fear, determination, confusion, and humor clearly. The series lives on expressive faces.
4. **Age-appropriate styling** — all clothing should feel realistic for kids ages 9–11 in a modern city. No costumes, no capes. These are regular kids with regular clothes that happen to reflect their personalities.
5. **Diversity is visible** — the cast represents different ethnicities, body types, and gender expressions. This should be celebrated, not minimized, in all illustrations.
6. **Blip's LED face is his entire emotional range** — practice drawing 8–10 distinct expressions (happy, confused, scared, determined, sleepy, excited, thinking, embarrassed, mischievous, loving).
7. **Blip Marginalia** — use Blip's LED face as a recurring marginalia element throughout the book. During arguments, show a tiny Blip in the margin with loading spinners or warning signs. During sad moments, show a tearful Blip face. During action, show an excited Blip. This creates a secondary reading experience for kids who flip through looking for Blip reactions.
8. **Visual density is non-negotiable** — almost EVERY page must have at least one visual element: a spot illustration, an emoji, a comic panel, a decorative element, a Blip marginalia, a glitch-line border, a typographic treatment, or a full illustration. No page should be a wall of text. Even text-heavy pages should include character icons, colored speech-bubble pull-quotes, or graphic dividers between sections.
9. **Dynamic typography during glitches** — when glitch events occur in the story, the text itself should participate: words warp, letters pixelate, font sizes shift, scan lines cross the page. This makes the book feel alive and interactive (Geronimo Stilton approach).
