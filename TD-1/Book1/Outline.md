# BOOK 1: THE LOST SIGNAL — Chapter Outline

> **Tagline:** They found a robot. The robot found trouble. Now they're all in way over their heads.
> **Emotional Core:** Belonging — four lonely kids discover they need each other.
> **Word Count Target:** ~12,000 words (~330 words/chapter average)
> **Dialogue Ratio:** 60-70%
> **Chapters:** 36 | **Pages:** ~216

---

## ACT I: THE DISCOVERY (Chapters 1–8)

*The basement. The cube. The signal. Everything changes.*

---

### Chapter 1 — "The Self-Folding Backpack (That Didn't)"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** Maya is in her cluttered bedroom workshop, putting the finishing touches on her latest invention — a backpack that folds itself. She flips the switch. It folds itself into a cube, then keeps folding until it crumples into a singularity of nylon. Her third failure this week. Daadi calls from the kitchen that she's wasting good backpacks. Maya needs a new motor — she remembers the junk pile in the basement.

**Hook:** Maya heads downstairs with a flashlight, past the laundry room, toward the door she's never opened.

**Illustration:** Full-page — Maya's cluttered bedroom workshop (tools, gadgets, posters, half-built inventions everywhere, warm desk lamp lighting).

**Blip marginalia:** (none yet — Blip hasn't appeared)

---

### Chapter 2 — "The Door That Wasn't Locked"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** Maya enters the basement storage area. Cobwebs, dusty boxes, the smell of old cardboard. She finds useful junk — a motor from a broken fan, copper wire, a hinge. Pushes deeper, past the water heater, to a rusted door she's never noticed. It should be locked. It isn't. Behind it: a narrow staircase going further down.

**Hook:** At the bottom, her flashlight hits something that shouldn't be there — a metal shelf with a faded logo. NEXCORP.

**Illustration:** Half-page — dark basement corridor, flashlight beam cutting through dust, the rusted door slightly ajar.

---

### Chapter 3 — "The Crate"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** Maya discovers a small abandoned lab — dusty workbench, old equipment, faded safety posters. On the shelf: a crate stamped NEXCORP RESEARCH — PROJECT BLIP — DO NOT ACTIVATE. She drags it down. Pries it open. Inside: a white cube, about four inches across, with a small screen on one side. No power button. No obvious way to turn it on. She pokes it. Nothing. She shakes it. Nothing. She carries it upstairs, along with the motor she originally came for. Daadi asks what she found. "Just some junk."

**Hook:** In Maya's room, she sets the cube on her desk and turns back to her backpack. Behind her, the cube's screen flickers — one tiny pixel of light.

**Illustration:** Half-page — Maya in the hidden lab, flashlight illuminating the dusty NexCorp crate on the shelf.

---

### Chapter 4 — "Maya and the Toothpaste"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** An ordinary evening. Maya fixes the backpack (using the new motor — works for three seconds before the strap snaps). Daadi makes samosas. Maya eats four and burns her tongue. She brushes her teeth, gets ready for bed, glances at the cube on her desk. Dead. She climbs into bed. In the dark, she talks to it anyway — tells it about her inventions, her school, how she doesn't really have anyone to build stuff with. She falls asleep.

**Hook:** At 2:17 AM, the cube detects a word it recognizes: "help." Maya muttered it in her sleep. The cube begins to boot.

**Illustration:** Spot — Daadi in the kitchen making samosas, steam rising, warm golden light. Spot — Maya asleep with the cube glowing faintly on the desk.

**Blip marginalia:** (first appearance!) ◐_◐ — tiny, dim, bottom margin. Blip is watching.

---

### Chapter 5 — "System Initialized"

**POV:** Maya → Blip
**Summary:** Maya wakes to a blue glow. The cube's screen shows two blinking dots and a line. A face. It hums. It speaks: "System initialized. Location: unknown. Where am I, and why does everything smell like old soup?" Maya screams. Then apologizes for screaming. Then pokes the cube. The cube introduces itself: "Designation: BLIP. Buildable Logical Intelligence Platform. Version 0.7. I am... not fully updated." Maya is DELIGHTED. A real robot! In her room! She starts asking a million questions. Blip answers each one with way too much information, including a 47-page history of sandwiches.

**Hook:** Blip's antenna perks up. "I am detecting a signal. It appears to be... coming from me. Should I be concerned?" A pulse of cyan light shoots out of him like a ripple in water.

**Illustration:** Full-page — Blip's activation moment. Blue glow filling the dark room, dust particles floating in the light, Maya's amazed face illuminated by the glow.

---

### Chapter 6 — "The Wave"

**POV:** Multiple — quick cuts through Maple Street
**Summary:** Blip's signal ripples outward through every connected device on Maple Street. We see the chaos in quick snapshots: Traffic lights at the intersection flash pink, green, and blue. Indoor sprinklers go off in Mrs. Chen's apartment. Digital photo frames show cat memes instead of family photos. A delivery drone does six loop-de-loops and drops a package on someone's roof. Smart doorbells play polka music. Maya's digital watch goes haywire and shows the time as 88:88.

**Hook:** Maya stares out her window at the chaos. Blip floats beside her: "I believe the technical term is... oops?"

**Illustration:** Full-page — Wide shot of Maple Street in chaos. Neon lights, spraying water, drones spinning, confused neighbors, dogs with LED collars going crazy. Maya at her window in shock.

---

### Chapter 7 — "The Kid with the Tablet"

**POV:** Leo
**Summary:** Cut to the Neonville Public Library, twenty minutes earlier. Leo is alone at a table, coding on his tablet, working on a project he calls "Project Tamales" (a homework tracker he's building for his sister). The library's screens flicker. Leo's tablet picks up the signal burst — a clean, powerful 2.4 GHz omnidirectional pulse. His eyebrows go up. He's never seen a signal like this. He traces it to a building on Maple Street. He packs up immediately. Ms. Chen (the librarian) tells him the library is closing early because of the "technical difficulties." Leo doesn't hear her. He's already running.

**Hook:** Leo stands outside a brick apartment building, looking up at a window where a faint blue glow is pulsing.

**Illustration:** Half-page — Leo in the library, tablet glowing in the dim light, multiple screens around him flickering, his face intent and focused.

---

### Chapter 8 — "The Girl with the Camera"

**POV:** Zara
**Summary:** Cut to Maple Street, same time. Zara is walking home from the community center where she submitted a painting for the youth art show. She's photographing the chaos — the glitching street lights, the wet sidewalks, the confused people — because this is the most interesting thing that's happened on Maple Street in YEARS. She spots the signal's origin point by noticing that the glitches radiate outward from one specific building. She follows the pattern like a trail of breadcrumbs, her camera clicking.

**Hook:** Zara reaches the building at the same time as a short kid in a beanie. They look at each other. "You traced the signal too?" "What signal? I'm taking PICTURES."

**Illustration:** Spot — Zara walking through the chaotic street, camera raised, photographing the glitching lights, colorful reflections on her denim jacket.

## ACT II: THE ASSEMBLY (Chapters 9–20)

*Four strangers. One robot. One enormous mess. And someone watching.*

---

### Chapter 9 — "The Kid Who Thought It Was a Game"

**POV:** Sam
**Summary:** Sam was at the community center's gaming tournament when the screens glitched. He wasn't scared — he was THRILLED. This was clearly an ARG (alternate reality game). He sprinted out of the center, following the glitch trail, doing a parkour move over a bench, scuffing his knee pad. He arrives at the building last, sees the other two kids at the door, and doesn't even slow down — he just runs inside and up the stairs.

**Hook:** Three kids who don't know each other are now standing in the hallway of an apartment building, following a trail of glitching hallway lights toward one specific apartment door.

**Illustration:** Spot — Sam vaulting over a park bench, red gloves in the air, huge grin, street lights flashing behind him.

---

### Chapter 10 — "Four Strangers and a Cube"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** Maya opens her door to find three kids she's never met. Leo states the technical facts. Zara shows photos of the chaos on her camera. Sam pushes past everyone shouting "CHALLENGE ACCEPTED!" and immediately tries to poke Blip, who hides behind a stapler. Maya doesn't want strangers in her room. They don't want to BE in her room. But they all need answers. Blip introduces himself to each of them with personalized greeting messages (Leo gets a data packet, Zara gets a pixel art smiley, Sam gets a game reference).

**Hook:** Blip suddenly goes quiet. His screen turns purple — thinking mode. "I am detecting an anomaly. Someone is... watching us." All four kids look at the old monitor in the corner of Maya's room.

**Illustration:** Half-page — Maya's room with all four kids and Blip for the first time. Maya at her desk, Leo by the door with tablet, Zara with camera, Sam already inside examining everything, Blip on the desk showing ●?●

---

### Chapter 11 — "What Do We Do with a Robot?"

**POV:** Mixed
**Summary:** The kids argue. Maya wants to keep Blip safe. Leo wants to analyze the signal data. Zara wants to document everything. Sam wants to find the next level. They talk over each other. Nobody listens. Daadi knocks on the door to ask if Maya wants chai and to complain about the sprinklers. Maya shoves everyone into her closet. It's awkward. Blip tries to mediate and accidentally quotes a Wikipedia article on conflict resolution. Nobody finds it helpful.

**Hook:** While they argue, the hallway lights flicker again. Blip's screen turns red. "I did not send another signal. This one is coming from OUTSIDE."

**Illustration:** Spot — four kids crammed into Maya's closet, Blip on a shelf above them, everyone's annoyed face.

---

### Chapter 12 — "The Second Glitch"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** A second wave of glitches hits — but this one is different. Deliberate. The apartment building's systems start locking down: electronic doors seal, the elevator stops, the heating turns to maximum. Blip panics and accidentally triggers his own glitch pulse that unlocks the front door but locks Maya's apartment door FROM THE OUTSIDE. They're trapped in Maya's room. The old monitor in the corner flickers to life.

**Hook:** Static fills the screen. Then a face — glitchy, shifting, made of broken pixels. A voice like five people speaking at once: "Well, well. Someone woke up the little cube."

**Illustration:** Half-page — the apartment hallway in lockdown. Red warning lights, sealed doors, heat waves distorting the air.

---

### Chapter 13 — "The Face in the Screen"

**POV:** All
**Summary:** The Gridlord's first appearance. The face shifts between teenager, static, and fragments of other faces. The voice is distorted, multi-layered. The Gridlord is intrigued that Blip is active — and curious about the kids. They're not afraid (okay, they're a LITTLE afraid). Sam thinks it's an NPC. Leo thinks it's a hacker. Maya thinks it's connected to the NexCorp lab. Zara thinks it's beautiful in a terrifying way and starts sketching it. The Gridlord tells them this building is now a locked level. They can't leave until they solve the puzzle.

**Hook:** The screen displays a complex pattern of symbols — and a timer. Three hours before the building's fire suppression system activates in every apartment. With people sleeping inside.

**Illustration:** Full-page — The Gridlord's face on the old monitor. Glitchy, pixelated, shifting, mysterious. Purple and blue color cast. The four kids' faces illuminated by the screen, each with a different expression (determination, fear, curiosity, excitement).

---

### Chapter 14 — "Error 404: Plan Not Found"

**POV:** Leo
**Summary:** Panic sets in. Maya wants to smash the monitor. Leo says that won't help. Sam wants to press random buttons. Zara is the only one looking at the puzzle, not panicking. Leo examines the symbol pattern on the screen — it's code-based but not any language he recognizes. He starts taking photos with his tablet to analyze it. Blip tries to interface with the monitor but gets rejected — "Access denied. You are not authorized, little cube." Something about that statement bothers Blip deeply.

**Hook:** Leo looks at the data. "This isn't just code. It's a cipher. And ciphers have keys. We need to find what unlocks it."

**Illustration:** Spot — Leo's tablet showing the symbol pattern, his face illuminated by the screen, intense concentration.

---

### Chapter 15 — "Hold On, Let Me Draw the Plan"

**POV:** Zara
**Summary:** Zara has been quietly sketching the symbols from the puzzle. She flips her sketchpad around — she's drawn each symbol separately and rearranged them by shape. They're not random. Viewed from the right angle, they form a physical shape — a specific connector that needs to be plugged into a port somewhere. The puzzle isn't digital. It's a physical key that needs to be built. Zara sees art where the others saw code.

**Hook:** Maya looks at the sketch. Her eyes light up. "I can build that."

**Illustration:** Half-page — Zara's sketchpad showing the symbol rearrangement. Her marker-stained fingers, colorful pens scattered around, the sketch coming together.

---

### Chapter 16 — "Eleven Minutes"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** Maya raids her junk drawer, her workshop, and Daadi's kitchen (borrows a whisk and two spoons, promises to return them). She builds the key from a coat hanger, two batteries, a speaker magnet, and an old USB cable. It's ugly. It's beautiful. It's exactly the right shape. Leo verifies the geometry against the cipher. Zara holds the flashlight. Sam keeps lookout at the door. Blip processes calculations. They work together without arguing for the first time.

**Hook:** Maya holds up the finished key. "Eleven minutes. New record." Blip: "For what?" Maya: "For saving the building."

**Illustration:** Full-page — Maya building the key at her desk. Tools spread out, sparks flying from her soldering, Leo's tablet showing specs, Zara's sketch propped up as reference, Blip hovering and projecting a holographic measurement grid. Everyone working together.

---

### Chapter 17 — "The Basement Again"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** The key needs to be plugged into the NexCorp terminal in the hidden lab — the one Maya found earlier. She leads them through the basement, past the storage area, through the rusted door, down the stairs. Leo is fascinated by the lab equipment. Zara photographs everything. Sam thinks it's a "secret level." Blip is quiet — he recognizes things but can't remember why. Maya finds the terminal port and inserts the key.

**Hook:** The terminal hums to life. On the screen: ACCESS GRANTED. The building unlocks. But the screen doesn't go dark. It shows another message.

**Illustration:** Half-page — The kids entering the NexCorp lab for the second time. Multiple flashlight beams, old equipment casting shadows, dust particles in the air.

---

### Chapter 18 — "Level One Complete"

**POV:** All
**Summary:** The Gridlord's face reappears, but briefly. Less hostile now — almost impressed. "You passed level one. But the real game hasn't started. I'll be watching." The screen goes dark. The building's systems return to normal. Doors unlock. The heating turns off. The fire suppression deactivates. In the sudden silence, the five of them stand in the lab, breathing hard.

**Hook:** Sam breaks the silence: "That. Was. TWELVE. STARS." And despite everything, they all laugh.

**Illustration:** Full-page — The five of them in the lab, the moment of relief. Maya slumped against a workbench grinning, Leo staring at the terminal with wide eyes, Zara hugging her camera, Sam doing a fist pump, Blip showing ◠‿◠. Warm flashlight beams, cool blue terminal glow.

---

### Chapter 19 — "We're Not a Team"

**POV:** Leo
**Summary:** Back in Maya's room. The adrenaline wears off and reality sets in. They solved one puzzle together, but that doesn't make them friends. Leo points out that they should all just go home and forget this happened. Sam disagrees — this was the most exciting night of his life. Zara says she has 200 photos that prove it was real. Maya just wants to make sure Blip is okay. Blip, overwhelmed by the emotional tension, starts buffering. Literally — his screen shows a loading bar.

**Hook:** Sam: "We have to do this again tomorrow." Leo: "No we don't." Maya: "The Gridlord said it's watching. This isn't over whether we want it to be or not."

**Illustration:** Spot — Blip buffering, loading bar on his screen, confused/overwhelmed expression.

---

### Chapter 20 — "The Name"

**POV:** All
**Summary:** They can't leave yet — it's 3 AM and they're all exhausted. They sit on Maya's floor and eat the remaining samosas. Blip offers to recite the 47-page history of sandwiches. They all say no. For the first time, they talk about normal things — school, hobbies, annoying siblings. Leo has a sister. Sam has an older brother. Zara has two moms. Maya has Daadi. Blip has... no one. A beat of silence. Then Sam says they need a name if they're going to do more of this. Blip suggests "The Cube Crew." Maya suggests "The Glitch Squad." Vote: Glitch Squad 4, Cube Crew 1. (Blip votes for both.)

**Hook:** They agree to meet the next afternoon at the Maple Street Park. As everyone leaves, Maya looks at Blip, still floating in her room. "Don't glitch anything while I sleep, okay?" Blip: "I make no promises." For the first time since moving to Maple Street, Maya falls asleep smiling.

**Illustration:** Half-page — The five of them on Maya's floor, eating samosas, warm lamplight, Blip floating among them with ◠‿◠. A found-family moment.

---

## ACT III: THE GRIDLORD'S GAME (Chapters 21–30)

*The Glitch Squad has a name. Now they need a plan. And a headquarters. And maybe some sleep.*

---

### Chapter 21 — "Daadi's Parathas"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** Morning. Daadi makes parathas. Maya eats six (stress eating). Daadi notices Maya seems different — lighter, happier. "Did you make a friend?" Maya pauses. "I think... four?" Daadi smiles knowingly and packs extra parathas "for sharing." Maya takes the hint. She also sneaks some tools into her vest pockets.

**Hook:** Maya's modified watch beeps — it's picking up a faint signal. A pattern. Like a heartbeat. Something digital is pulsing beneath Maple Street.

**Illustration:** Spot — Daadi in the kitchen, parathas on the griddle, Maya at the table already wearing her inventor's vest, morning sunlight through the window.

---

### Chapter 22 — "Maple Street Park"

**POV:** Sam
**Summary:** The park. Sam arrives first — he ran, of course. He's already mapped the park like a game level (best hiding spots: 3, fastest escape routes: 4, benches that need repair: 2). Leo arrives precisely on time. Zara arrives five minutes late, sketching something she saw on the way. Maya arrives last with Blip in her vest pocket and a bag of parathas. Over breakfast on the park bench, they compare notes. The Gridlord's puzzle, the NexCorp lab, Blip's origin — it's all connected. But how?

**Hook:** Blip's antenna wiggles. He points toward the ground. "The signal is coming from underneath us. Specifically: 14.3 meters below the park's north-east corner."

**Illustration:** Half-page — the four kids and Blip on a park bench, morning sun, parathas being shared, each in their signature outfit. A quiet before-the-storm moment.

---

### Chapter 23 — "Underground"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** Blip guides them to a manhole cover behind the basketball court. Sam wants to go down immediately. Leo says they need a plan. Zara says they need to tell someone. Maya says the only adult who'd believe them is Daadi, and she's not putting Daadi in danger. They go down. Below the park: old utility tunnels that connect to the NexCorp lab's ventilation system. Blip gets stronger the deeper they go — more data, more signals, more... memories he can't access.

**Hook:** They reach a junction. Left tunnel leads to the lab. Right tunnel leads somewhere else — somewhere with a faint purple glow and the hum of active electronics. The lab was abandoned. Whatever's down the right tunnel isn't.

**Illustration:** Half-page — The kids in the underground tunnel. Flashlight beams, pipes overhead, Blip's cyan glow as a secondary light source, expressions of nervous excitement.

---

### Chapter 24 — "The Gridlord's Test"

**POV:** Leo
**Summary:** They explore the right tunnel. They find a small server room — active, humming, covered in the Gridlord's pixelated crown tags. This is where the Gridlord operates from. Or one of the places. On the main screen: a message. "You came back. Good. Level 2: The Grid is broken in four places. Find them. Fix them. Or Neonville's systems degrade until nothing works. Clock's ticking." Four glitch locations appear on a map — each in a different part of the neighborhood.

**Hook:** Leo analyzes the map. "Each glitch is a different type of problem. One's hardware. One's software. One's visual. One's physical. We need different skills for each one."

**Illustration:** Half-page — The Gridlord's server room. Screens glowing with data and maps, the pixelated crown icon visible, the four kids looking at the main screen, Blip interfacing with a terminal.

---

### Chapter 25 — "Glitch One: The Traffic Light"

**POV:** Sam
**Summary:** First glitch location: the Maple Street intersection, where traffic lights are still cycling randomly. Sam's speed and game reflexes are needed — he has to physically reach the traffic control box on the pole while the lights flash unpredictably. Maya builds a bypass tool on the spot. Leo walks Sam through the rewiring via Blip's comm link. Zara maps the timing pattern and tells Sam when to go. Sam climbs the pole like it's a platformer, dodges the flashing signals, and plugs in the bypass. The lights go green. Then normal. First glitch fixed.

**Hook:** Blip: "Glitch one resolved. Three remaining. Also, Sam, your shoe is on fire." Sam: "That's just my LED sneakers!" (It was actually a tiny spark. He's fine.)

**Illustration:** Full-page — Sam on top of a traffic light pole, action pose, plugging in Maya's bypass tool, traffic lights flashing multiple colors around him, the other three kids watching from below.

---

### Chapter 26 — "Glitch Two: The Library"

**POV:** Leo
**Summary:** Second glitch: the public library's digital archive is corrupted — files mixing together, holographic displays showing wrong information, Mr. Okafor's (Zara's uncle) beloved digitized manuscripts are scrambling. Leo's coding skills take center stage. He accesses the library's terminal and starts debugging — but the code is alive, mutating, fighting back. The Gridlord built this glitch to be unsolvable by one person. Leo needs Blip to process one data stream while he handles another. Together, they stabilize the system.

**Hook:** As the last file restores, the screen flashes a hidden message: "Not bad, little coder. But you're fixing symptoms, not causes. The Grid is bigger than you know."

**Illustration:** Half-page — Leo at a library terminal, screens around him showing cascading code, Blip connected to the terminal via a cable, green code reflected in Leo's glasses.

---

### Chapter 27 — "Glitch Three: The Mural"

**POV:** Zara
**Summary:** Third glitch: the community center's AR display system is projecting distorted, scary images over the kids' art exhibition — including Zara's painting. Kids are crying. Parents are complaining. Zara's art is being vandalized digitally. She's furious. But instead of panicking, she sees the pattern — the AR distortion is actually a visual cipher. Her artist's eye decodes what the code couldn't. She draws the correction pattern on her tablet, uploads it through Blip, and the AR display stabilizes — showing the art correctly, but now with beautiful, subtle glitch-art effects that make the paintings look BETTER.

**Hook:** Zara stares at her enhanced painting. "Wait... the Gridlord didn't break this. They IMPROVED it. Why would they do that?"

**Illustration:** Full-page — Zara in the community center, painting on the wall behind her with beautiful glitch-art AR overlays, kids and parents looking amazed, Zara's expression shifting from anger to wonder.

---

### Chapter 28 — "Glitch Four: The Water"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** Fourth glitch: the park's smart garden irrigation system has gone haywire — water pressure building in underground pipes. If it bursts, the park floods and the community garden is destroyed. Maya's engineering skills are critical. She has to build a pressure release valve from materials in the park — playground equipment, a garden hose, a bike pump, zip ties. She works fast, talking to the parts like she always does. The others keep watch, direct water flow, and manage the crowd that's gathering. Maya finishes with thirty seconds to spare. The pressure releases safely.

**Hook:** As the water drains, the four glitch markers on the Gridlord's map all turn green. A new message appears: "All four resolved. But you've only fixed the cracks. The foundation is still broken. Come to the source."

**Illustration:** Full-page — Maya in the park, surrounded by gushing water, building the pressure valve in real-time. Tools, pipes, and parts flying around her. The other kids helping. A perfect "I can fix that!" moment.

---

### Chapter 29 — "The Source"

**POV:** All
**Summary:** The Gridlord invites them back to the server room. This time, the screen shows something different: a map of Neonville's entire digital infrastructure — the Grid. And it's covered in micro-glitches, hundreds of them, like hairline fractures. The four they fixed were just the biggest. The Gridlord's message: "I didn't cause the glitches. I found them. They've been spreading for months. I woke them up to get someone's attention. NexCorp built something broken and buried it. You found it. Now fix it." The Gridlord isn't the villain. They're the alarm.

**Hook:** Blip stares at the map. Some of the glitch patterns look familiar — like circuit diagrams in his own architecture. "These patterns... they are inside me. I am part of this. I am part of the problem."

**Illustration:** Full-page — The Gridlord's map of Neonville's Grid. Hundreds of red glitch markers across a digital city blueprint. The kids looking at it with new understanding. Blip floating in front, his screen showing ●_● — processing something painful.

---

### Chapter 30 — "Blip's Choice"

**POV:** Blip
**Summary:** Blip has a decision. He can interface directly with the Grid through the server room's terminal. It might give him access to stabilize the micro-glitches. But it could also overwrite his memory — he's version 0.7, incomplete, and the Grid's full data might be too much. The kids argue about whether to let him try. Blip, for the first time, makes his own choice: "I would rather be version 2.0 and help my friends than stay version 0.7 and watch Neonville break." He plugs in.

**Hook:** Blip's screen goes blank. Then fills with data. His antenna sparks. His whole body glows brighter than they've ever seen. Then — silence.

**Illustration:** Full-page — Blip interfacing with the terminal. Cables connected, energy flowing, the kids watching with fear and hope. Blip's body glowing cyan-white, data streams visible inside his screen.

---

## ACT IV: THE SQUAD (Chapters 31–36)

*Something has started. And it's not stopping.*

---

### Chapter 31 — "Version 1.0"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** Blip reboots. He's different — slightly faster, slightly more coordinated, with access to Grid data he didn't have before. But he's still Blip. Still makes bad jokes. Still takes things literally. Still gets confused by sarcasm. The micro-glitches stabilize — not gone, but paused. The Gridlord's voice one last time: "Good. You bought some time. But the cracks will spread again. You'll need a real HQ. A real plan. And a real team. I'll be in touch." The screen goes dark for good this time.

**Hook:** Leo looks around the server room. "This place has power, connectivity, and it's hidden. We could use this." Maya: "We'd need to clean it up." Sam: "I call top bunk!" Zara: "There's no bunk." Sam: "I call dibs on the theoretical bunk!"

**Illustration:** Half-page — Blip rebooting, new version number glowing on his screen. The kids surrounding him, relieved, Maya reaching out to steady him.

---

### Chapter 32 — "The Lab"

**POV:** All
**Summary:** They clean up the NexCorp lab. Maya claims a workbench for repairs and inventions. Leo sets up the main terminal as their monitoring station. Zara decorates — she paints the blank walls, brings a rug from home, hangs string lights. Sam brings a mini-fridge from his garage (his mom didn't notice it was missing). Blip gets a charging dock that Maya builds from an old phone stand and a lot of enthusiasm. By evening, the abandoned lab looks like something else — a hangout. A headquarters. A home base.

**Hook:** Leo's terminal pings. A single data packet from an unknown source. Inside: partial blueprints of Blip's original design. And a note: "To help you understand what you're up against. — G."

**Illustration:** Full-page — The finished HQ. Each kid's area reflecting their personality. Maya's organized chaos of tools, Leo's terminal glowing, Zara's art on the walls, Sam's gaming setup, Blip's charging dock in the center. String lights. Warm. Lived-in.

---

### Chapter 33 — "The Blueprints"

**POV:** Leo + Blip
**Summary:** Leo and Blip analyze the blueprints. They reveal something shocking: Blip wasn't built to be a companion. He was built to be a bridge — a connection between the digital Grid and the physical world. Version 0.7 was incomplete because NexCorp abandoned the project when the Grid started glitching. They buried Blip and the lab and walked away. The glitches aren't random — they're the Grid trying to heal itself, and failing because the bridge (Blip) was never completed.

**Hook:** Blip processes this quietly. Then: "I was not abandoned because I was broken. I was abandoned because THEY were afraid. That is... different. And somehow worse."

**Illustration:** Half-page — Blueprints spread across a workbench, Leo's tablet overlaying annotations, Blip hovering above, holographic schematics visible in the air.

---

### Chapter 34 — "The NexCorp Connection"

**POV:** Maya
**Summary:** The NexCorp label on the crate, the abandoned lab, the Grid — it's all one company's mess. Maya wants to confront them. Leo says no — NexCorp is a massive corporation, and they're four kids and a cube. Zara suggests they document everything first — build a case. Sam suggests they level up first and go later. Blip says nothing. He's searching NexCorp's public records through the Grid and finding disturbing gaps — deleted files, missing project reports, employees who "transferred" and were never heard from again.

**Hook:** Blip finds one file that wasn't deleted properly. A project name: PROJECT KIRA. Blip can't access the contents. But the file was last modified exactly two years ago. And the modification was made by the Gridlord.

**Illustration:** Half-page — Maya at the workbench, journal open, drawing connections on a cork-board style investigation map. Blip projecting a holographic NexCorp logo with question marks.

---

### Chapter 35 — "What We Are"

**POV:** All
**Summary:** Sunset. They're on the roof of the apartment building — their new tradition. Maya's eating samosas. Leo is coding in the fading light. Zara is painting the skyline.
