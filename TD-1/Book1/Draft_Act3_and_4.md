## Chapter 26 — Glitch Three: The Mural

## Chapter 27 — Glitch Four: The Water

The park's smart garden was flooding.

Maya talked to the parts as she always did. "Okay, little valves. You're overwhelmed. I get it. We've all been there. But I need you to hold on just a little longer while I build you a release."

## Chapter 35 — The Signal

Maya stood at the edge of the roof, facing downtown.

The sun was gone. Neonville's lights were coming on — thousands upon thousands of them, each one a node in the Grid, each one a potential point of failure. The city was beautiful and broken and alive, and somewhere beneath its digital skin, fractures were slowly, quietly spreading.

She should have been scared. She wasn't.

She had a team. Leo, who saw the code in everything. Zara, who saw the beauty. Sam, who saw the adventure. And Blip, who saw it all for the first time and thought every bit of it was wonderful.

She had a headquarters — a secret lab full of junk and hope and string lights.

She had a mission — fix the Grid, find the truth about NexCorp, figure out who Kira was, and help the Gridlord.

She had a junk journal full of sketches and a head full of ideas.

She had Daadi's parathas.

She had everything she needed.

Behind her, the rooftop door opened. Three silhouettes climbed through — one short and bouncy, one tall and graceful, one compact and methodical. And above them, a faint cyan glow.

"Took you long enough," Maya said without turning around.

"Sam got stuck in the door," Leo's voice said.

"I did NOT get stuck. I was strategically pausing."

【He was stuck. I have video evidence.】

"DELETE THAT."

【Absolutely not. It is my first piece of memorabilia.】

Maya smiled. She could feel them gathering behind her — her team, her friends, her family.

The four kids and a robot stood on the rooftop of an old apartment building on Maple Street, looking out at a city that didn't know it needed them.

Yet.

## Chapter 36 — Level 2

In the server room beneath the park, the terminal Blip had connected to flickered.

No one was there to see it.

On the dark screen, a cursor blinked. Then typed, one character at a time:

Level 2 loading...

A pause.

Welcome back, Glitch Squad.

The cursor blinked again.

This time, you're going to need more than a coat hanger and some kitchen spoons.

Another pause.

I'll be waiting.

The screen went dark.

And somewhere in the vast digital nervous system of Neonville, a micro-glitch — one of the hundreds still spreading, slowly, quietly — pulsed.

It pulsed near NexCorp Tower.

Downtown.

And it was growing.
