## A1 — Europol vs INTERPOL (mandate & powers)

### What Europol actually is and can do
- **Full name / legal basis:** "European Union Agency for Law Enforcement Cooperation" (Europol), HQ The Hague, established by **Regulation (EU) 2016/794** (in force 1 May 2017). It is an **EU agency**, not a police force. Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europol (key doc cited: `data.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/794/oj`).
- **No executive powers — this is the single most important fact for the book:**
  > "The Agency has no executive powers, and its officials are not entitled to **arrest** suspects, conduct independent **investigations**, or act without prior approval from competent authorities in the member states."
  Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europol (¶ "Tasks and activities"; cross-referenced to the Regulation).
- **Mandate = support + analysis + coordination:** Europol is "a centre for law-enforcement co-operation, expertise and criminal intelligence" against serious/international crime (drugs, human trafficking, cybercrime, money laundering, counter-terrorism). It facilitates information exchange, provides **analytical support**, runs **Joint Investigation Teams**, and produces threat assessments (SOCTA, TE-SAT).
- **Operational units (real, usable as "expert tells"):** O2 **ESOCC** (European Serious & Organised Crime Centre), O3 **EC3** (European Cybercrime Centre), O4 **ECTC** (European Counter Terrorism Centre, launched 2016), and the **EFECC** (European Financial & Economic Crime Centre, 2020). Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europol (Organisation / EFECC).
- **Europol Liaison Officers (ELOs):** seconded national officers (≈201 in 2016) who operate the information exchange — they facilitate; they do **not** arrest in the host state. Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europol (Organisation).
- **Third-country & Interpol reach:** Europol holds **operational** cooperation agreements with, among others, the US, Switzerland, Norway, and **Interpol**, and **strategic** agreements with **Turkey**, China, Brazil, Russia. Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europol (Governance/relations). ⇒ Europol's Turkey link is *strategic*, not operational.

### What INTERPOL is and can do
- **Red Notice (the thing writers get wrong):** "A request to law enforcement worldwide to locate and **provisionally arrest** a person pending **extradition, surrender, or similar legal action**… **it is not an arrest warrant.** INTERPOL **cannot compel** the law enforcement authorities in any country to arrest someone who is the subject of a Red Notice." Source (verbatim): https://www.interpol.int/How-we-work/Notices/Red-Notices
- **Membership:** 196 member countries **including Turkey** (Europol's mandate is EU-only). So the worldwide wanted-person / Turkey-facing channel runs through **Interpol**, not Europol.
- Interpol also issues **diffusions** (a direct member-to-member alert) and runs databases (Stolen & Lost Travel Documents, etc.).

### Verdict for the book (A1)
- Vesna's "Europol evidence channel" is **plausible as an evidence/analysis/intelligence pipeline** (Europol receives material via its secure **SIENA** channel, runs analysis, feeds a JIT and the national prosecutor) — but it is **NOT** a route to an arrest or to a "Europol court." Europol has neither.
- For the wanted-person dimension toward Turkey, the realistic instrument is an **Interpol Red Notice** (worldwide, Turkey is a member), not a Europol action.
- Both bodies are, in the end, **hand-off bodies**: the actual arrest and trial always happen in a **national** jurisdiction.

## A2 — Jurisdiction over Crimes on a Cross-Border Train

### The territorial principle is the bedrock
Criminal jurisdiction over an offence normally belongs to the state on whose territory the offence was committed — the *lex loci delicti* / **territorial principle**. This is the default in every European legal system. The problem for a *moving* vehicle is pinpointing that place.

### There is NO "flag-state" rule for trains (critical contrast)
- **Aircraft** solved the moving-vehicle problem by treaty: the **Tokyo Convention 1963** (187 parties) **Art. 3(1)** confers jurisdiction on the **State of Registry** of the aircraft, with *concurrent* jurisdiction of the overflown territorial state, the state of landing, etc. Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tokyo_Convention (the article expressly discusses the impossibility, at modern speeds, of "establish[ing] the exact geographic location where the act actually took place").
- **Ships** have flag-state jurisdiction under UNCLOS.
- **Trains have no equivalent international treaty.** There is no "registration state" rule for a train, no COTIF criminal-jurisdiction provision (COTIF governs *civil* carriage). ⇒ Jurisdiction defaults to **pure territorial**: whichever state's soil the train is on at the moment of the act — and because a heist spans the journey, **multiple states have concurrent jurisdiction** (every state crossed can lawfully claim it). **[This "no flag-state for trains" point is itself UNVERIFIED by treaty citation — I could not find a treaty; the absence is the claim. Treat as strong-but-flag-it.]**

### What this means operationally
- Because jurisdiction is territorial and concurrent, **the decisive practical fact is where the train physically stops and where the suspect is arrested.** That state exercises territorial jurisdiction, arrests, and can charge.
- European states layer several **additional** jurisdiction bases (so even a stop outside the "main" state rarely creates a loophole): **nationality** (offender's state), **passive personality** (victim's state), **protective principle**, plus EU directives on trafficking in human beings and organised crime that *require* member states to assert and harmonise jurisdiction.

### Cross-border pursuit mechanics (colour, not load-bearing)
- The **1990 Schengen (CISA) Convention Art. 41** regime of cross-border **surveillance and hot pursuit** is the tool for a rolling chase across Schengen states. **[UNVERIFIED at article-number precision — flag; the existence of Schengen hot-pursuit is well established.]** Bulgaria's Schengen status and Turkey's non-membership shape which side of the border pursuit can lawfully continue.

### Extradition realism for a wealthy, well-lawyered suspect
- **European Arrest Warrant (EAW)** — the intra-EU fast-track:
  - "An arrest warrant valid throughout all member states… requires another member state to **arrest and transfer** a criminal suspect… to the issuing state." Replaces lengthy extradition; in force since **1 Jan 2004**. Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Arrest_Warrant
  - **Double criminality is removed** for **32 categories** that read like this book's charge sheet: *murder; grievous bodily injury; kidnapping, illegal restraint and hostage-taking; trafficking in human beings; participation in a criminal organisation; terrorism; fraud; money laundering; organised or armed robbery; extortion.* Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Arrest_Warrant (Double criminality list).
  - Minimum offence threshold: max penalty ≥ **1 year**; **time limits: 10 days** (consent) / **60 days** (contested). Average surrender ≈ 16 days (consent) / ≈ 49 days (contested). Source: ibid.
  - **Lawyer friction points (realistic delay, not defeat):** proportionality challenge; **specialty** rule (can't be prosecuted for other pre-surrender offences without consent); **human-rights refusal** (Art. 1(3), Recital 13 — no surrender where serious risk of torture/inhuman treatment or unfair trial). Source: ibid.
- **Turkey is NOT in the EAW system** (not an EU member). Reaching Turkey or a non-EU national requires **classical extradition** under the **1957 European Convention on Extradition** + bilateral treaties — slower, treaty-based, with political-offence and nationality exceptions.

## A3 — The Realistic "Court" Destination (where, which body)

### Why "a Europol court near Istanbul" fails on every axis
1. **Europol has no court** and no arrest powers (A1).
2. **Turkey is outside the EU**, outside the Europol *operational* mandate (strategic agreement only), **outside the EAW**, and outside Schengen. A "Europol court near Istanbul" is therefore implausible structurally, geographically, and legally.

### Where the EU boundary actually is on this corridor
- The EU's south-eastern land frontier on the Lisbon→Istanbul axis is the **Bulgaria–Turkey border**. **Bulgaria is an EU member (2007), in Europol, party to the EAW,** and Schengen-associated. Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Arrest_Warrant (Bulgaria implemented the EAW on accession in 2007).
- ⇒ **The realistic "jurisdiction with a court" is in Bulgaria** — the train is rerouted/stopped on **Bulgarian** soil before it can exit the EU.

### Concrete, defensible staging options
- **Stop point:** the **Kapitan Andreevo / Kapıkule** border complex, or a station short of it (**Svilengrad**, **Harmanli**), or pull all the way back to **Sofia**. Any of these is **Bulgarian territory** → Bulgarian territorial jurisdiction attaches.
- **Arresting body:** **Bulgarian National Police / Border Police (Ministry of Interior — МВР).** They make the physical arrest on Bulgarian soil, seize the ledger/witness, and refer the case to a **Bulgarian prosecutor and district court**.
- **Uniforms — "blue-and-white":** Bulgarian police uniforms are **predominantly dark blue**. **[UNVERIFIED at exact current shade/insignia — verify against Ministry of Interior / Border Police imagery before locking the visual.** The book's "blue-and-white" is in the right family but should be confirmed or nudged to "dark navy with light-blue chevrons/insignia" for accuracy.**]**
- **Supporting cast (the honest "international" layer that does NOT replace the national court):**
  - **Europol** = the analysis/evidence/intelligence backbone (SIENA channel, JIT support, ESOCC/EFECC analytical products) — feeding the **Bulgarian** prosecutor, not trying the case.
  - **Eurojust** = coordinates cross-border judicial cooperation if several states charge concurrently.
  - **Interpol** = any **Red Notice** and the **Turkey-facing** wanted-person interface (Turkey is an Interpol member, not an Europol operational one).
- **What happens to Reinhart:** arrested in Bulgaria on serious charges that are double-criminality-exempt under the EAW (murder/kidnapping/trafficking/organised crime). Bulgaria can **charge and try him domestically**, or, if another state (e.g., the victims'/ledger's state) issues an **EAW**, **surrender** him there under the 60-day track. A wealthy defendant's lawyers can raise proportionality/specialty/human-rights objections — delaying, not defeating, a clean factual case.

### One trap to avoid: the EPPO
The **European Public Prosecutor's Office (EPPO)** is the only genuinely "EU-level" prosecutor, but its **mandate is restricted to offences affecting the EU's financial interests** (the **PIF Directive 2017/1371**: EU-budget fraud, related corruption/money-laundering, large cross-border VAT fraud ≥ €10m, subsidy fraud). Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Public_Prosecutor%27s_Office. ⇒ **EPPO is the WRONG court for a murder/kidnapping/trafficking heist.** Do not invoke it as the destination.

## B — Con / Heist Crew Roles (real history)

### The source everyone in con-lore defers to
**David W. Maurer, *The Big Con: The Story of the Confidence Man and the Confidence Game* (1940).** This is the foundational ethnography of the American confidence game (Maurer was a linguist who studied the argot of real hustlers) and the direct source for *The Sting* (1973). It is the spine of "real" con taxonomy, as opposed to the *Leverage* / *Ocean's* pop version. Source for what follows: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confidence_trick (¶ "In a Long Con", citing Maurer 1999 [1940]) and https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scam.

### Maurer's *real* role vocabulary (map the crew onto these)
- **The inside man (insideman):** the expert who runs the rigged "big store" / betting room and shows the mark how to make dishonest money. This is the **operator at the heart of the con**. → Maps to the **Architect/Mastermind (Sloane)** + the **Forger (the Maker)** together (the people who build the false reality the mark is inside).
- **The roper (roper-in / roper-out):** the front-man who **locates and gains the mark's confidence**, then **steers (ropes) him** to the inside man. Pure social-engineering role. → Maps cleanly to the **Grifter (Theo)** — the confidence man / social engineer. (See **B2** for "putting the mark up" and "roping the mark.")
- **The shill:** an accomplice posing as an uninvolved, initially sceptical bystander who *corroborates* the story and "invests" too (the "in-and-in"). → This is a *function* any crew member (or a planted Naïma/Gianni) can perform in-scene.
- **The fix (the fixer):** Maurer's 10th stage is literally **"putting in the fix"** — forestalling police/law. The real historical role "fixer" = the person who **manages corruption, lookouts, bent cops, escape routes**. → Maps cleanly to the **Fixer (Gianni)**. (Note: in modern heist fiction "fixer" has drifted to mean " procurer/supply guy"; Maurer's original is about neutralising authority — worth keeping.)
- **The mark / sucker / rube / gull:** the victim. ("You can't cheat an honest man" — the mark's own greed/dishonesty is the hook.)

### Where the modern "heist crew" taxonomy is pop-culture, not Maurer
Maurer's world is the **long con** (marks fleeced of fortunes over days in fake betting rooms). The crisp modern role-set — **hacker / wheelman / hitter / thief / grifter / mastermind / fixer / forger** — is largely a **film/TV composite** crystallised by *Ocean's Eleven* (2001) and *Leverage* (2008–). Each modern role *does* map onto a real criminal specialism, but the clean taxonomy is pop, not ethnographic:
- **Hacker / "box man":** "box man" is **real argot for a safecracker** (the person who "does the box"), historically distinct from a modern cyber-intruder. Jae = "box man" (physical vault-cracking) + hacker (digital) is a *fictional fusion*; a purist would split them. **[The "box man" argot is widely attested; I did not pull a single primary citation — flag if the reader demands one.]**
- **Wheelman:** real argot — the **getaway driver**. Rafa is a wheelman/pilot variant.
- **Hitter / muscle + medic:** "muscle" is real; "medic" is a *Leverage*-style character-engineering fusion (real crews don't usually carry a combat-medic; it's a competence-porn invention). Honest framing.
- **Thief / cat-burglar (Naïma):** the infiltration/entry specialist — real (second-storey work, bypass), though "cat burglar" is colourful/pop.
- **Forger (the Maker):** real — document/identity forgery is a standalone criminal specialism.

### Social-engineering realism (for Theo)
The genuine craft vocabulary, all attested in the confidence-trick literature: **pretexting** (inventing a plausible identity/scenario to elicit action), **elicitation** (drawing information out conversationally without asking directly), **the convincer** (letting the mark "win" small to build trust), **the blow-off / cooldown** (dispersing the mark quietly afterward). The honest core insight from the literature: cons exploit **greed, dishonesty, credulity, vanity, desperation** — "you can't cheat an honest man" is the operative thesis (Maurer; Huang & Orbach 2018). Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confidence_trick (Vulnerability factors).

## B2 — The Long Con Structure (the canonical stages)

Two canonical stage-lists exist; both are sourced and both are usable as a beat-map for the crew's con-against-Reinhart.

### Maurer's 10 stages of the "big con" (from *The Big Con*, 1940)
Verbatim list as quoted on Wikipedia (citing Maurer):
1. Locating/investigating a well-to-do victim — **"putting the mark up."**
2. Gaining the victim's confidence — **"playing the con for him."**
3. Steering him to meet the inside man — **"roping the mark."**
4. The inside man shows how to make money dishonestly — **"telling him the tale."**
5. Letting the victim make a substantial (small) profit — **"giving him the convincer."**
6. Determining how much he will invest — **"giving him the breakdown."**
7. Sending him home for the money — **"putting him on the send."**
8. Fleecing him in the rigged "big store" — **"taking off the touch."**
9. Getting him out quietly — **"blowing him off."**
10. Forestalling the law — **"putting in the fix."**
Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confidence_trick (¶ "In a Long Con").

### Smith's 6 stages (Edward H. Smith, *Confessions of a Confidence Man*, 1923)
Foundation work → approach → build-up → **pay-off/convincer** → **the "hurrah"** (manufactured crisis forcing an instant decision) → **the "in-and-in"** (accomplice "invests" alongside the mark to legitimise). Source: ibid. (¶ "Stages").

### How to use this (and respect the book's "plan you're shown isn't the plan" rule)
- The crew's **audience-facing plan** = the long-con stages above (mark = Reinhart). But the book's house rule — *the plan you're shown isn't the plan* — means the **"tale/convincer/send" sequence is itself misdirection**: the audience (and Reinhart) think the con is about *the prize*, while the **true objective** is to surface the **ledger + the witness** and deliver them to the Bulgarian court. The con's "blow-off" is the arrest.

## C1 — Dynamic-Pressure Force on a Body at 600 km/h (with numbers)

### The governing equation (sourced)
NASA Glenn, *Drag Equation*: **D = Cd · (ρ·V²·A)/2**, where D = drag, Cd = drag coefficient, ρ = air density, V = velocity, A = reference (frontal) area. Source: https://www1.grc.nasa.gov/beginners-guide-to-aeronautics/drag-equation/

### Inputs
- **V = 600 km/h = 166.7 m/s** (≈ 167 m/s).
- **ρ ≈ 1.2 kg/m³** (sea-level air; use 1.225 for a stricter value).
- **Human effective (Cd·A):** derived from terminal velocity (Wikipedia *Terminal velocity*, citing Elert, *The Physics Factbook*): a skydiver **belly-to-earth has Vt ≈ 55 m/s**; limbs-in/head-down ≈ **90 m/s**; competition speed skydivers ≈ **150 m/s**. Using **Vt = √(2mg/(ρ·Cd·A))** with m = 80 kg, ρ = 1.225:
  - belly-to-earth (Vt 55): **Cd·A = 2·80·9.81 / (1.225·55²) ≈ 0.42 m²** (broadside-to-flow human ≈ 0.42–0.5 m²).
  - head-down (Vt 90): **Cd·A ≈ 0.16 m²** (edge-on / streamlined ≈ 0.15–0.25 m²).
  Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terminal_velocity

### Dynamic pressure (the "wall")
- **q = ½ρV² = 0.5·1.2·(166.7)² ≈ 16,700 Pa ≈ 16.7 kPa** (≈ 0.165 atm). With ρ=1.225: ≈ 17.0 kPa.
- *Physical feel:* that is a modest *pressure*, but it acts over a human-scale *area* — and drag scales with **V²**, so doubling speed quadruples force. At 167 m/s the air is, mechanically, **a wall**.

### Drag force on an exposed human (broadside)
- Broadside, Cd·A ≈ 0.42 m²: **F = q·(Cd·A) ≈ 16,700·0.42 ≈ 7,000 N ≈ 7.0 kN ≈ 714 kgf.**
- Using the brief's stated **0.5 m²** assumption: **F ≈ 16,700·0.5 ≈ 8.3 kN ≈ 850 kgf.**
- **Headline: ≈ 7–9 kN ≈ 0.7–0.9 tonnes-force** on a person standing broadside to the flow. The book's "~8–9 kN" is correct.

### Is this survivable unrestrained? No.
- On an 80 kg person, **a = F/m ≈ 8,000/80 ≈ 100 m/s² ≈ 10 g** instantaneous. The body is peeled off the hull and accelerated toward free-stream in well under a second; limbs flail (dislocations), and an unrestrained human is then detached at speed — lethal blunt-trauma. **[The specific human g-tolerance thresholds (sustained >~20 g lethal; aortic/organ rupture) are standard aerospace-medicine knowledge but UNVERIFIED against a primary citation here — flag; verify against a NASA/USAF biodynamics source before printing the exact numbers.]**
- **Compressibility caveat (makes it worse, not better):** M = 167/343 ≈ **Mach 0.49**. Compressibility effects begin ~Mach 0.3; near Mach 0.5 any bluff protrusion locally accelerates flow toward transonic, producing shocklets, violent unsteady **buffeting**, and a drag rise above the incompressible estimate. So **~8–9 kN is a conservative lower bound**; real transient peaks on an unstreamlined body are higher.

## C2 — Boundary-Layer Survival Near the Hull

### What the boundary layer is (sourced)
NASA Glenn, *Boundary Layer*: at the surface the fluid **sticks (no-slip)**; velocity rises from 0 at the wall to the free-stream value across a thin "boundary layer"; its character (laminar vs turbulent) depends on the **Reynolds number Re = V·x/ν**; Prandtl (1900s). Source: https://www1.grc.nasa.gov/beginners-guide-to-aeronautics/boundary-layer/

### Boundary-layer thickness at Rafa's station (~3 cars aft ≈ 75 m from the nose)
- ν(air) ≈ 1.5×10⁻⁵ m²/s. **Re_x = V·x/ν = 167·75 / 1.5×10⁻⁵ ≈ 8.35×10⁸** — fully turbulent.
- Turbulent flat-plate thickness **δ ≈ 0.37·x / Re_x^0.2 ≈ 0.37·75 / 60.8 ≈ 0.46 m** (≈ 46 cm).
- Velocity profile (1/7-power law, u(y)/V = (y/δ)^(1/7)) at this station:
  - y = 5 cm off hull → **u ≈ 0.73 V** (dynamic-pressure factor ≈ 0.53).
  - y = 15 cm → **u ≈ 0.85 V** (factor ≈ 0.73).
  - y = 25 cm → **u ≈ 0.92 V** (factor ≈ 0.84).
  *(1/7-power law is a standard textbook turbulent-profile approximation — Schlichting. **[UNVERIFIED against a single citation here; flag for a textbook anchor.]**)*

### Honest finding (this is a correction to lean-on-the-BL framing)
The underside boundary layer is **real and helpful but is NOT a magic shield.** Pressed dead flat, a body sits in the lower portion of the BL and the **local dynamic pressure is cut by roughly 15–50%** — a meaningful but *not* order-of-magnitude reduction. The book should **not** present the boundary layer alone as the thing that saves him.

### The BIGGEST physical lever is frontal-area reduction
Lying **flat and edge-on** (long body axis parallel to the flow, thin profile to the hull) drops the effective **Cd·A from ~0.42 → ~0.15–0.25 m²** (the skydiver belly vs head-down data). That is a **3–4× force cut** — larger than the boundary layer's contribution.

### Combined survivable load during a flat, edge-on traverse
- Effective Cd·A ≈ 0.2 m², local dynamic-pressure factor ≈ 0.5–0.7:
  **F ≈ (0.5–0.7)·16,700·0.2 ≈ 1,700–2,300 N ≈ 1.7–2.3 kN ≈ 170–235 kgf continuous.**
- So the **steady** restraint load during traversal is **~1–3 kN** — well within a magnetic clamp. The danger is **not** the steady load; it is the **snatch (jerk) loads** when clamps release/re-engage or the leash snaps taut, which can spike to the **5–9 kN** range for milliseconds. That snatch — transmitted through the harness to the shoulder — is the injurious event (glenohumeral dislocation / soft-tissue damage at multi-kN distractive load).

### A free realism win: he traverses AFT = WITH the relative wind
The drag vector points **aft**, the direction he is travelling. So the wind **assists** the traversal rather than resisting it; the hazards are (a) **uncontrolled acceleration**, (b) **yaw/roll instability** of a body that is only restrained in translation, and (c) the **snatch** when the leash catches. Moving *forward* (into the wind) would be near-impossible. And: **anything that protrudes** (a loose strap, an elbow) catches flow, yaws the body broadside, and spikes the force 3–5× — so "nothing protrudes; stay dead flat" is physically mandatory, not flavour.

## C3 — The Magnetic-Leash Restraint Realism

### Would a "magnetic leash" hold a human against ~8–9 kN? Yes — trivially.
- **Magnetic pressure (the governing number):** for a magnet clamped to a steel hull, holding force/area = **F/A = B²/(2μ₀)**. Wikipedia *Electromagnet* gives the practical limit at core saturation (B ≈ 1.6 T): **F/A ≈ 1,000 kPa ≈ 145 lbf/in²**, and notes **"at 1 T the magnetic pressure is approximately 4 standard atmospheres (≈ 4.1 kg/cm²)."** Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnet (¶ Force exerted by magnetic field; ¶ Magnetic circuit).
- **Sizing:** at a working ~1 T (~4 kgf/cm²), a clamp needs only **~225 cm² of pole area to hold 9 kN (≈ 900 kgf)**; at saturation (~10 kgf/cm²) only ~90 cm². A pod-scale mag-clamp (hundreds of cm² of pole face) holds the full aerodynamic drag **with large safety margin**.
- **Industrial proof of scale:** electromagnets are "employed in industry for picking up and moving heavy iron objects such as scrap iron and steel" (ibid.) — i.e., routine tonnage. Commercial **permanent magnetic lifters are rated 100 kg to several tonnes (≈ 1–30+ kN)**. **[Specific tonnage ratings are a product-category fact; UNVERIFIED against a named manufacturer datasheet here — verify a model/rating if the text cites a number.]**

### Dramatic-but-survivable framing (reframe of Ch38 to *only* what physics permits)
Rafa survives the under-belly traverse **only because all four hold at once**:
1. **The mag-leash carries the restraint load** (steady ~1–3 kN; snatch peaks ~5–9 kN). The clamp is rated in tonnes, so the *continuous* load is easy and only the *snatch* is dangerous.
2. **He stays dead flat and edge-on** to minimise Cd·A (the dominant lever) and to keep his thickness inside the slower near-hull boundary layer (a real secondary cut).
3. **Exposure is seconds** — he moves *with* the wind; the violent snatch loads are sub-second.
4. **He pays the physical price: the shoulder.** The snatch transmits multi-kN distractive load through the harness to the glenohumeral joint → dislocation / ligament damage. That is the honest cost of survival; without the leash, 10 g peels him off and kills him.

## Expert Tells (~10 realistic details a cop / ex-con / aerodynamicist will clock)
1. **"Europol can't arrest."** The single most reliable cop-tell — Europol has no executive powers; its officials cannot arrest or run independent investigations (Regulation (EU) 2016/794).
2. **"A Red Notice isn't a warrant."** Interpol itself says so verbatim — it's a *request to locate and provisionally arrest*, and Interpol "cannot compel" any country to act.
3. **EAW double-criminality list = the charge sheet.** Murder, kidnapping/hostage-taking, human trafficking, participation in a criminal organisation, terrorism, fraud, money-laundering — all explicitly listed and exempt from the double-criminality check.
4. **No flag-state for trains.** Aircraft have the Tokyo Convention's "State of Registry"; ships have UNCLOS; **trains have no equivalent treaty** — jurisdiction is territorial/concurrent. A legal-reader tell that the author did the homework.
5. **The 60-day / 10-day EAW clock.** Contested surrender ≈ 60 days; consent ≈ 10; real averages ≈ 49 / ≈ 16 days. Specific, verifiable, and dramatic for a ticking-clock book.
6. **"Putting in the fix."** Maurer's literal 10th stage of the big con — the real historical origin of the word "fixer."
7. **"The convincer" and "the send."** Maurer's stage-names; an ex-con/grifter-literate reader will smile at the authentic taxonomy vs the *Ocean's* shorthand.
8. **Dynamic pressure q = ½ρV² ≈ 16.7 kPa at 600 km/h**, drag on a broadside human **≈ 7–9 kN ≈ 0.8–0.9 t-f** — an aerodynamicist will check this in their head and it's right.
9. **Boundary layer ≈ 0.46 m thick at 75 m aft, Re ≈ 8×10⁸, 1/7-power profile** — the correct order of magnitude and the correct (modest) near-wall velocity reduction, not a Hollywood "wind shadow."
10. **Magnetic holding pressure ≈ 4 kgf/cm² at 1 T** — so a pod-clamp holding tonnes against a few-kN drag is *under*-stressed; the leash is the easy part of the physics, the shoulder is the hard part.
