# THE FOURTH STEP — Chapter-by-Chapter Outline

## Story Overview

**Working Title:** THE FOURTH STEP
**Tagline:** *Four women. One house. A dead girl. Twenty-five years of lies.*
**Genre:** Psychological Thriller / Domestic Suspense
**Target Word Count:** 85,000–90,000 words
**Target Chapters:** 32 + Epilogue
**POV Structure:** Multi-first-person, rotating (Neve, Saskia, Imogen, Rowan, Aisling)
**Tense:** Present tense (primary), past tense (flashback fragments)
**Setting:** Sorrento, Mornington Peninsula, Victoria, Australia
**Timeframe:** Friday 5 PM to Sunday 9 AM (36 hours of real time)

## The Truth (Author Reference Only)

On December 14, 25 years ago, five 12-year-old girls had a sleepover at the Sorrento holiday house owned by Elaine Cassidy. Elaine was having an affair. Kiera Doyle went upstairs, overheard Elaine on the phone, and confronted her. Elaine grabbed Kiera to stop her from telling the others, pushed her away from the stairs, and Kiera went through the open window. Neve Cassidy (Elaine's daughter, age 12) followed Kiera upstairs and witnessed the push. Elaine immediately began gaslighting Neve, replacing her memory with a constructed narrative. The death was ruled accidental. Elaine sold the house, moved Neve away, severed contact with the other families, and maintained the lie for 25 years. Now dying of cancer, Elaine has orchestrated a reunion at the renovated house — simultaneously wanting to confess and wanting to control the narrative.

## Prologue (Optional — 500 words)

A single page. The fall, told in second person, present tense, stripped of identity.

> You are twelve years old. The hallway is cold. The window is open and the night is warm and the air moves through the house like breathing. You hear your own heartbeat. You hear a voice — low, female, not yours. You see two shapes at the end of the hallway. One is smaller. Then the window is empty. Then the air changes. Then you are at the window and you are looking down and the grass is dark and wet and everything is quiet and will stay quiet for twenty-five years.

No names. No context. Plants the sensory template the rest of the novel fills in.

**Hook type:** Foreboding
**Tension:** 3/10

## CHAPTER 1 — NEVE: "The Drive"
**Word count:** 2,500–3,000 | **Time:** Friday 5:00 PM | **Tension:** 2/10

Neve drives from Melbourne to Sorrento. We learn she's a family therapist. She's left her 12-year-old daughter Lily at home with husband Liam. She calls her mother Elaine at the hospice during the drive. Elaine says: "You don't have to do this, sweetheart. Sometimes it's better to leave things in the past." Neve notices her hands shaking on the wheel. She doesn't know why. Flashback fragment: salt air smell, creaking floorboards, golden light — dismissed as anticipation.

**Hook:** Neve turns onto the street and sees the house. Renovated, different color. But the roofline is the same. Her chest tightens. She can't catch her breath. She sits in the car five minutes before opening the door.

**Clue planted:** Elaine's "leave things in the past" — a gaslighter discouraging the return to the trauma site. Neve's shaking hands — somatic response preceding conscious awareness.

## CHAPTER 2 — SASKIA: "First Arrival"
**Word count:** 2,500–3,000 | **Time:** Friday 6:00 PM | **Tension:** 3/10

Saskia is already at the house. She chose this rental specifically — tracking the property for years. She maps the house with forensic precision. She observes Neve sitting in her car through the window. Saskia's luggage contains the police report. She hasn't decided whether to use it. Flashback: Saskia at 14, at the Sorrento police station, being told "It was an accident, love. Let it go." She didn't let it go.

**Hook:** Saskia looks at the upstairs window — new, smaller, fixed-pane — and thinks: *The window is different. But the frame is the same. And the frame is what matters.*

**Clue planted:** Saskia has the police report (hidden). Her observation that "the frame is what matters" foreshadows the fingernail scratches.

---

## CHAPTER 3 — IMOGEN: "Fine"
**Word count:** 2,500–3,000 | **Time:** Friday 6:30 PM | **Tension:** 2/10

Imogen arrives with excessive cheerfulness and too much food. Performing normalcy. She surveys the house and pronounces it "completely different" — but only looks at the ground floor. She doesn't go upstairs. Mentions her daughter Sophie compulsively. Flashback: only the good parts of THE FOURTH STEP — games, laughter, pizza. Memory stops before the end of the night.

**Hook:** Imogen looks out the kitchen window toward the back garden. "The garden's the same," she says. Then turns away quickly, as if she's said something she shouldn't.

**Clue planted:** Imogen won't look upstairs. Her fixation on Sophie (same age Kiera was). "The garden's the same" — she can't stop comparing present to past.

---

## CHAPTER 4 — ROWAN: "Arrival"
**Word count:** 2,000–2,500 | **Time:** Friday 7:00 PM | **Tension:** 4/10

Rowan arrives last, with camera bag and small backpack. She sees the house and photographs it immediately — reflex. Her gaze goes to the staircase. She doesn't go up. Imogen notices her silver ring (Kiera's ring). Rowan says "A friend gave it to me" — no elaboration. Flashback: the hallway in sensory fragments — cold, light, sound. Not a story.

**Hook:** Rowan looks at the staircase and says: "The fourth step still creaks." She hasn't been in this house in 25 years. She shouldn't know that. Everyone turns to look at her. Rowan doesn't notice she's said something wrong.

**Clue planted:** Rowan's spatial memory is intact despite 25 years away. She wears Kiera's ring. She remembers the fourth step — context-dependent memory firing.

---

## CHAPTER 5 — NEVE: "The First Evening"
**Word count:** 3,000–3,500 | **Time:** Friday 8:00 PM | **Tension:** 4/10

Dinner. Wine. Forced conversation. Four women performing adulthood. Kiera's name comes up once — three seconds of silence. No one mentions how she died. Cate texted Saskia — arriving Saturday morning. No one clearly remembers Cate. Flashback: vivid sense-memory of the house smell 25 years ago. Neve says nothing.

**Hook:** Neve takes the downstairs bedroom (refuses upstairs — doesn't acknowledge why). Lying in the dark, she hears the house settling. Floorboards creak. Then, from upstairs: the fourth step groans. No one should be upstairs. She holds her breath. It doesn't happen again.

**Clue planted:** Neve refuses to go upstairs — unconscious avoidance of the trauma site. The fourth step sound — spatial memory activating.

## CHAPTER 6 — SASKIA: "Night Watch"
**Word count:** 2,500–3,000 | **Time:** Friday 9:30 PM | **Tension:** 5/10

Saskia stays up after the others. Kitchen, laptop, case notes. File on each woman. Timeline of the night. List of inconsistencies. She reads the police report again: "minor bruising on the upper back and shoulders." She's spent 24 years trying to get someone to take that detail seriously. Flashback: the funeral. Elaine Cassidy in the front row, composed, arm around Neve. Saskia thought: *She doesn't look sad. She looks tired.*

**Hook:** Saskia hears footsteps in the upstairs hallway. She goes to the foot of the stairs and looks up. Dark. The sound stops. She thinks: *Someone else is awake in this house. Someone else is thinking about that night.*

**Clue planted:** Elaine at the funeral — "she looks tired, not sad." Saskia's 24-year obsession with the bruising detail.

---

## CHAPTER 7 — IMOGEN: "The Dream"
**Word count:** 2,500 | **Time:** Friday 11:00 PM | **Tension:** 5/10

Imogen can't sleep. Extra anxiety pill. She tries to think about Greg, Sophie, lesson plans — anything but this house. But the ceiling is the same height. The window faces the same direction. The air smells the same under the new paint. Flashback (more vivid): standing in the living room 25 years ago, holding a mug of hot chocolate. A woman's voice, sharp, brief — from upstairs. Then the sound of the fall. The mug shattering. She doesn't remember after that. Imogen tells herself: *It was the TV. A movie. That's what I heard.*

**Hook:** Imogen passes the staircase and looks up. And thinks, for the first time in 25 years: *What if it wasn't an accident?* She pushes it down. She goes back to bed. She doesn't sleep.

**Clue planted:** The voice — the real memory buried under the "TV" explanation. The mug shattering — a sensory fragment that's accurate but displaced.

---

## CHAPTER 8 — ROWAN: "Night Vision"
**Word count:** 2,000–2,500 | **Time:** Friday 2:00 AM | **Tension:** 6/10

Rowan sleepwalks to the upstairs hallway. Wakes standing at the spot where she stood 25 years ago. The new window is smaller, fixed-pane. Moonlight different. But the space is the same. The length is the same. The cold is the same. She takes a photograph. Flash of the camera illuminates the hallway. She photographs the window frame. Later, zooming in on her laptop, she sees behind the new frame: the original woodwork. And scratches. Fingernail scratches. Still there. Under the new frame.

Flashback (full sensory): standing here at 12. Two shapes at the end of the hallway. One tall. One small. The tall one moved. The small one — the small one went through the window. Not fell. Not jumped. *Went.* As if the air took her.

**Hook:** Rowan zooms in on the photograph. The scratches are real. Five parallel lines. Downward motion. Someone held on. Someone lost their grip.

**Clue planted:** First hard physical evidence. Rowan's description "she went through the window" — not a fall word, not a jump word. An evasion word. Her memory recorded the event but her brain can't categorize it.

## CHAPTER 9 — NEVE: "Morning"
**Word count:** 2,500–3,000 | **Time:** Saturday 7:00 AM | **Tension:** 4/10

Neve wakes early. Coffee. Garden. The garden IS the same — the stone wall, the path, the lawn. She can see the upstairs window from the garden. She looks at it and feels pressure behind her eyes. She looks away. Cate arrives — warm, friendly, says she was "a couple of years behind" the others. Neve's therapist instincts fire: the smile is perfectly calibrated. Too perfect. Flashback: standing in this garden at 12, ambulance in the driveway, lights on the stone wall. Neve grips her coffee mug so hard it cracks.

**Hook:** Neve picks up the broken mug pieces and cuts her finger. She watches the blood and thinks, without knowing why: *The last time I was in this garden, someone was dying.*

**Clue planted:** Cate's too-perfect demeanor (Aisling performing). Neve's mug cracking under unconscious stress. The garden as a trigger site.

---

## CHAPTER 10 — SASKIA: "Observation"
**Word count:** 2,500–3,000 | **Time:** Saturday 8:30 AM | **Tension:** 5/10

Saskia watches the group dynamics. Notes: Neve performs calm but won't go upstairs. Imogen performs cheer but won't look at the back of the house. Rowan is upstairs — Saskia follows and finds her photographing the window frame. Rowan shows the scratches photograph. Saskia's pulse spikes but her expression doesn't change. She asks Rowan what she remembers. Rowan: "I remember the cold. I remember the light. I remember two shapes. One was small. One was tall. The tall one moved. The small one went through the window." Saskia photographs the scratches with her phone.

**Hook:** Saskia goes downstairs and sees Cate in the kitchen, asking questions about the house, the group, the history. Questions a journalist would ask. Saskia thinks: *Who are you?*

**Clue planted:** Saskia's forensic approach to Rowan's memory. Cate asking investigative questions. The "two shapes" detail is now shared between Rowan and Saskia.

---

## CHAPTER 11 — IMOGEN: "Breakfast"
**Word count:** 2,500 | **Time:** Saturday 9:00 AM | **Tension:** 4/10

Imogen cooks elaborate breakfast (needs busy hands). Cate asks about the old days. Imogen performs nostalgia. Key moment: Cate asks "What was Kiera like?" Imogen stops cooking. "She was the best of us. She was brave and funny and she — she fell. It was an accident. She leaned out the window and fell. It was summer and the window was open and it was just — just an accident." Rehearsed for 25 years. Cate's face, for one second, shows raw grief — gone before Imogen registers it.

**Hook:** Imogen notices she's been gripping her fork so hard her knuckles are white. Four red marks on her palm. She doesn't remember gripping.

**Clue planted:** Cate's mask slip — micro-expression of grief too intense for a casual acquaintance. Imogen's rehearsed "it was an accident" speech.

---

## CHAPTER 12 — ROWAN: "The House Speaks"
**Word count:** 2,500 | **Time:** Saturday 10:00 AM | **Tension:** 7/10

Rowan photographs every room. Drawn to specific details: scratch on bannister, discoloration on ceiling, crack in plaster. In the former girls' bedroom (now a study), she finds an uneven floorboard. She lifts it. Underneath: a cavity containing Kiera's disposable camera from that night. The film is still inside — undeveloped. The last photographs Kiera ever took are on this film. The camera was placed deliberately — not dropped.

**Hook:** Rowan holds the camera. She thinks: *If I develop this film, I'll see what she saw. The last things she saw.* She hasn't decided whether to tell the others.

**Clue planted:** The undeveloped film — a ticking time bomb of evidence. Who placed the camera under the floorboard? (This thread pays off in the Epilogue.)

---

## CHAPTER 13 — NEVE: "The Hallway"
**Word count:** 3,000 | **Time:** Saturday 11:00 AM | **Tension:** 8/10

Neve goes upstairs for the first time (has been avoiding it). Fourth step groans. She knows the sound — her body knows it. She walks the hallway. Grey walls (were cream). New carpet (was blue). But dimensions identical. She reaches the window. Something happens: her vision narrows. Grey walls become cream. New carpet becomes old blue. Window becomes large and open. Warm air. Salt and eucalyptus and something metallic. Her hands tremble. She sees — not remembers, sees — two figures at the end. One tall. One small. The tall one's arm moves. The small one reaches for the frame. Fingernails scrape paint. Then — Neve comes back. She's on the floor. Trembling. Saskia is beside her. "I felt dizzy. I haven't eaten."

**Hook:** Neve sits in the kitchen afterward. She thinks: *I saw something. In the hallway. Two people. One of them was — no. No, I was downstairs. I was always downstairs. Mum said I was asleep.* For the first time, the mantra doesn't work.

**Clue planted:** First significant memory breakthrough. Neve's hippocampus reactivating the cognitive map. The visual flash of two figures. Her denial mechanism failing.

---

## CHAPTER 14 — SASKIA: "The Report"
**Word count:** 2,500 | **Time:** Saturday 12:00 PM | **Tension:** 6/10

Saskia processes Neve's collapse. She reads the police report again: "Minor bruising on the upper back and shoulders, bilaterally. Consistent with impact injuries from fall." She knows the bruising pattern is wrong for a fall — it's bilateral, specific, on the shoulders. Consistent with hands gripping from behind. She decides to reveal the report tonight. Then: she finds a manila envelope slid under her door. Inside: a crime scene photograph of the upstairs hallway, 25 years ago. In the corner, reflected in the window glass, a figure standing at the top of the stairs. Watching. She doesn't know who left it.

**Hook:** Saskia looks at the photograph. In the reflection: a figure at the top of the stairs. Someone was there. Someone watching. Someone who isn't in any official account.

**Clue planted:** The crime scene photo was left by Elaine (orchestrating from the hospice). The reflected figure is Neve — standing at the top of the stairs, watching. Evidence that contradicts "I was downstairs."

---

## CHAPTER 15 — IMOGEN: "The Game"
**Word count:** 2,500–3,000 | **Time:** Saturday 2:00 PM | **Tension:** 7/10

Someone suggests a game. Truth-or-dare echoes the original sleepover (ghost technique). Loaded questions: Cate asks "What's the one thing you've never told anyone?" Saskia asks "Do you think memories are reliable?" Imogen deflects with humor. Rowan answers with sensory descriptions. Then Saskia asks directly: "What do each of you remember about the last time we were all in this house?" Imogen: "I don't see the point." Rowan: "I remember the cold." Neve: "I don't remember anything." Saskia drops the bomb: "You were in the hallway, Neve. When the others got there, you were already standing in the hallway. You were looking at me. And your face wasn't surprised. It was already terrified."

**Hook:** Neve says: "No. I was downstairs. On the couch." Saskia: "I was the first one up the stairs. You were already in the hallway, Neve. Under the window. Looking at me." Long silence. Neve: "I don't remember that." Her voice cracks on "don't."

**Clue planted:** Saskia's observation from 25 years ago — Neve was already in the hallway, already terrified. This contradicts the official story and Neve's own account. Direct challenge to the gaslit narrative.

---

## CHAPTER 16 — ROWAN: "The Frame"
**Word count:** 2,500 | **Time:** Saturday 3:00 PM | **Tension:** 7/10

Rowan goes upstairs alone after the confrontation. She prys back the new window frame and sees the original wood. The scratches are clear: five parallel lines, depth and spacing of a child's fingernails, downward motion. Someone held on. Someone was pulled away. She photographs them. Flashback (vivid): Rowan at 12, standing in the hallway, looking up. She sees a hand — a small hand — reaching for the window frame. Fingernails scraping paint. The hand is Kiera's. She knows those hands. She's held them a hundred times. The fingers splay, grasp, and then — they're gone. The hand slides down and disappears. First time Rowan has formed this memory into a coherent image.

**Hook:** Rowan shows the scratch photographs to Saskia. Saskia looks at them, then takes out the police report and the crime scene photograph.

**Clue planted:** The scratches confirm grip, not fall. Rowan's specific memory of Kiera's hand. The convergence of Rowan's sensory evidence and Saskia's documentary evidence.

## CHAPTER 17 — NEVE [MIDPOINT]: "What I Saw"
**Word count:** 3,500 | **Time:** Saturday 4:00 PM | **Tension:** 9/10

Neve is alone. Saskia and Rowan are walking (discussing evidence privately). Imogen is in her room. Cate is in the village. Neve goes upstairs. Fourth step groans. She doesn't stop. Walks the hallway. Stands at the window. The memory hits — not a fragment. A full, immersive, panoramic memory. She is 12. The hallway is warm (summer). The window is open. Salt and eucalyptus and metallic fear. She is standing at the top of the stairs. Two figures at the end. One is tall — a woman. Her back is to Neve. Her hand is on the shoulder of the smaller figure. The smaller figure is Kiera. The woman's arm moves — push — and Kiera stumbles backward. Kiera's hands reach for the frame. Fingernails catch paint. And then Kiera is gone. Through the window. Into the dark. The woman turns. She sees Neve. She crosses the hallway in three steps. She kneels. She takes Neve's face in her hands. She says: "You didn't see anything. You were asleep. You were downstairs."

Neve comes back to the present. She's on the floor. Not trembling — still. She's looking at the window. She knows what she saw. She knows whose arm it was. *Mum.*

**The midpoint shift:** Everything changes. The question becomes: "My mother killed Kiera, and she made me forget."

**Hook:** Neve's phone rings. Elaine. She doesn't answer. Text appears: "Call me, sweetheart. We need to talk about this weekend." Neve stares at the text: *You knew. You knew about this weekend. You knew I'd remember.* She puts the phone face-down and sits in the dark.

**Clue planted/confirmed:** Elaine's text confirms she knew about the weekend (orchestrating). Neve's memory names the pusher. The midpoint reframes the entire novel.

## CHAPTER 18 — SASKIA: "Revelation" [Saturday 6 PM, Tension: 8]
Saskia gathers the group (minus Cate) and reveals the police report. Reads the injury description aloud. Explains what it means — grip marks, not fall injuries. Shows the crime scene photograph with the reflected figure at the top of the stairs. "Someone pushed Kiera. And someone in this house knows who." **Hook:** Cate, returning from the village, stands in the doorway and hears the last sentence.

## CHAPTER 19 — IMOGEN: "The Voice" [Saturday 7 PM, Tension: 8]
Imogen breaks. The minimization collapses. She tells the group about the voice she heard — sharp, female, from upstairs, before the fall. She's never told anyone. "The voice. I heard a voice. A woman's voice. And I knew — even then, I knew it wasn't the TV. I knew." **Hook:** Cate, from the corner: "What did the voice say?"

## CHAPTER 20 — ROWAN: "Two Figures" [Saturday 8 PM, Tension: 8]
Rowan reveals what she saw — two figures, the hand on the shoulder, the push, the hand reaching for the frame. First time she's narrated this coherently. "The tall one was a woman. Taller than any of us." Saskia: "Tall like an adult?" Rowan: "Tall like an adult." **Hook:** Cate asks: "Was there an adult in the house that night?" Everyone turns to Neve.

## CHAPTER 21 — SASKIA: "The Confrontation" [Saturday 9 PM, Tension: 7]
Saskia confronts Cate directly. Asks probing questions about her background. Catches the micro-hesitations. "I've been corresponding with a journalist investigating Kiera's death. Someone using the name A.M. Doyle. Would you like to tell me who that is?" **Hook:** Cate's face goes completely still.

## CHAPTER 22 — AISLING: "Unmasked" [Saturday 9:30 PM, Tension: 8]
Aisling drops the pretense. She's Kiera's younger sister. Was 8 when Kiera died. Has spent her adult life investigating. Reveals autopsy photographs showing grip-mark bruising. Reveals anonymous tips she's been receiving. "Someone in this house pushed my sister. I've spent twenty-five years trying to find out who." **Hook:** The group is reeling. And then — headlights in the driveway.

## CHAPTER 23 — NEVE: "Elaine" [Saturday 10 PM, Tension: 9]
Elaine arrives from the hospice. Gaunt, coat too large, but commanding. She walks in and sits in the best chair. "I see you've been busy." She says she wants to talk about that night. **Hook:** Elaine looks at the assembled group — the police report, the autopsy photos, Rowan's camera, Imogen's tears — and says: "I think it's time we all talked about what happened."

## CHAPTER 24 — SASKIA: "The Confession" [Saturday 10:30 PM, Tension: 8]
Elaine tells her controlled version: "I was upstairs. Kiera came up. She startled me. There was a struggle. She stumbled backward. The window was open. It was an accident." Controlled tears. "I was trying to protect you, sweetheart." She reaches for Neve's hand. **Hook:** Neve pulls her hand away and says: "That's not what happened."

## CHAPTER 25 — NEVE: "What I Remember" [Saturday 11 PM, Tension: 9]
Neve tells the group her recovered memory. The hallway. Elaine's back. Elaine's hand on Kiera's shoulder. The push. Kiera's hands reaching for the frame. The fall. Then: Elaine kneeling, holding her, telling her she didn't see anything. The gaslighting — not clinical, but raw. "She told me I was asleep. She told me every day. She took me to doctors and told them I was anxious. She moved us away. She erased that night and replaced it with her version." Elaine: "You were twelve, Neve. You were confused." Neve: "I saw you push her."

## CHAPTER 26 — IMOGEN: "The Command" [Saturday 11:15 PM, Tension: 7]
Imogen describes the voice in full. "I think it was a command. I think she told Kiera to do something and Kiera refused. I think they argued." First account of a confrontation, not a startled reaction. **Hook:** Aisling: "My sister was not the kind of person who backed down. If someone told her to be quiet, she'd do the opposite."

## CHAPTER 27 — AISLING: "The Evidence" [Saturday 11:30 PM, Tension: 8]
Aisling presents the full forensic case. Bruising pattern inconsistent with fall — consistent with grip from behind, push with force. Landing distance inconsistent with slip — required horizontal force. "My sister didn't fall. She was pushed with enough force to send her body two meters from the building. That's not a startled reaction. That's a deliberate push." Elaine: "The science is wrong." But her voice has lost its steadiness for the first time.

## CHAPTER 28 — NEVE: "The Truth" [Saturday 11:45 PM, Tension: 10]
Final confrontation. Neve asks: "What were you doing upstairs? Who were you talking to?" Elaine's composure cracks fully. She admits: the phone call, the affair, Kiera overhearing, Kiera threatening to tell, Elaine grabbing her arm, the push. "It was two seconds. Two seconds and everything changed. And I couldn't take it back." Neve: "And then you made me forget." Elaine: "I was protecting you." Neve: "You were protecting yourself." Elaine: "Both."

## CHAPTER 29 — NEVE: "Aftermath" [Sunday 12:15 AM, Tension: 7]
Processing. Saskia thinks legally. Aisling processes confirmation. Imogen grieves — not just Kiera, but 25 years of denial. Rowan is silent. Neve calls Liam: "I need you to tell Lily I love her. I'll be home soon." **Hook:** Elaine collapses in the next room.

## CHAPTER 30 — SASKIA: "The Decision" [Sunday 1 AM, Tension: 6]
Saskia lays out options. Evidence is enough for coronial reinvestigation. But Elaine is dying. A trial would take years. "Justice and truth are not always the same thing." **Hook:** Aisling: "I spent twenty-five years wanting to know who killed my sister. Now I know. And I don't feel anything."

## CHAPTER 31 — NEVE: "The Last Conversation" [Sunday 2 AM, Tension: 8]
Ambulance for Elaine. Neve rides with her. In the ambulance, Elaine gives the real apology — not controlled, not strategic. A dying woman with nothing left to manage. "I'm sorry I took that night from you. I'm sorry I made you doubt your own eyes." Neve holds her mother's hand. She doesn't say "I forgive you." She doesn't say she doesn't.

## CHAPTER 32 — NEVE: "Morning" [Sunday 7 AM, Tension: 5]
Morning. Quiet. Neve in the garden, looking up at the window. Aisling joins her with Kiera's camera (film still inside). Neve: "You should develop it. They're the last things she saw. They're hers." Neve goes back inside, climbs the stairs, walks the hallway, puts her hand on the frame — over the scratches. Then walks back down. Calls Liam: "I'm coming home. I need to talk to you about my mother. And about what happened when I was twelve."

## EPILOGUE — NEVE [Three months later, Tension: 3]
Elaine died six weeks after the weekend. Neve was with her. In the final days, Elaine told the full uncontrolled truth. Neve recorded it. Coronial inquest opened — finding expected to change cause of death from "accidental" to "homicide." DPP reviewing. Given Elaine's death, charges unlikely, but the record will be corrected.

Aisling published her investigation as a long-form article. Neve is in therapy — real therapy, not what she performs for others. EMDR. Recovering memories in fragments. Learning to trust her own perception.

**Last line:** *The window is closed. The scratches are still there.*
