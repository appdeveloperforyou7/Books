## 6. KEY SCENE BEATS

### Scene 1: The Midpoint (Chapter 17) — Neve's Memory Returns

**Beats:**
1. Neve alone in the house. Quiet. The house is louder in absence — creaks, ticks, wind.
2. She climbs the stairs. Fourth step groans. Heart rate spikes before conscious thought.
3. She walks the hallway. Ceiling height. Wall length. Light quality from window. All triggers.
4. She reaches the window. Puts her hand on the frame — new frame, over old scratches.
5. **Memory hits.** Present tense shifts. Grey becomes cream. Small window becomes large and open. She is 12.
6. Two figures. One tall. One small. Tall one's arm extends. Push. Small one reaches for frame. Fingernails on paint. Empty air.
7. Tall one turns. Sees Neve. Crosses hallway. Kneels. Holds her face. "You didn't see anything."
8. Back to present. On the floor. Not trembling — still. She knows.
9. She names the figure. Not aloud. *Mum.*
10. Phone rings. Elaine. She doesn't answer. Text: "We need to talk about this weekend."

**Prose approach:** Short staccato sentences during the memory. Fragmented. Present tense throughout — memory is NOW. After the memory, longer sentences for processing. Rhythm mirrors the shattering and reassembly of consciousness.

### Scene 2: The Final Confrontation (Chapter 28) — Neve vs. Elaine

**Beats:**
1. Neve: "What were you doing upstairs?"
2. Elaine: "I had a headache. I went to lie down."
3. Neve: "You were on the phone. Who were you talking to?"
4. First real crack in Elaine's composure. The affair — the secret she's protected for 25 years.
5. Neve: "What was so important you'd kill a twelve-year-old to keep it secret?"
6. Elaine: "I didn't kill anyone." Voice wavers.
7. Aisling shows autopsy photos. Grip marks. Physics.
8. Elaine looks at the photos. She recognizes them — she sent them to Aisling originally.
9. Elaine breaks. The real truth. Phone call. Kiera overhearing. Threat. Grab. Push. Window. Fall.
10. Elaine: "It was two seconds. Two seconds."
11. Neve: "And then you spent twenty-five years making me forget those two seconds."
12. Elaine: "I was protecting you."
13. Neve: "You were protecting yourself."
14. Elaine: "Both."
15. Silence.

**Prose approach:** Dialogue-driven. Short exchanges. No dialogue tags for the key back-and-forth — just words. The absence of tags creates speed and intensity. Physical beats between lines: Elaine gripping chair arms. Neve's jaw clenching. Others silent. Wind. House groaning.

### Scene 3: The Game (Chapter 15) — First Group Confrontation

**Beats:**
1. Someone suggests truth-or-dare (echoing original sleepover).
2. Questions start light, escalate.
3. Cate's question: "What's the one thing you've never told anyone?" — projection.
4. Saskia's question: "Do you think memories are reliable?" — testing.
5. Saskia goes direct: "What do each of you remember about the last time we were in this house?"
6. Imogen deflects. Rowan gives sensory answer. Neve claims no memory.
7. Saskia's bomb: "You were in the hallway, Neve. You were already standing there. Your face wasn't surprised. It was already terrified."
8. Neve denies. Voice cracks. The gaslit narrative is challenged for the first time.

**Prose approach:** Rapid dialogue. Short. Interruptions. Characters talk over each other. Neve's internal narration fragments as the challenge lands.

## 7. UNRELIABILITY PATTERNS — QUICK REFERENCE

| Narrator | Unreliability Type | "Tell" Pattern | What They Withhold |
|---|---|---|---|

| Aisling (as Cate) | Fabricated identity | Over-controlled prose; too-complete backstory; micro-grief at Kiera's name | Everything about herself |
