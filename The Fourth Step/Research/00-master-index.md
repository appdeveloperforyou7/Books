## Story Summary

Four women return to the house where their childhood friend Kiera Doyle fell from a second-floor window and died at their sleepover, twenty-five years ago. Each carries a different memory of that night. Each has built a different life on top of a different lie.

Neve, a family therapist, doesn't remember being upstairs — she believes she was asleep on the couch. Saskia, a barrister, has spent two decades hunting the truth with a stolen police report. Imogen, a teacher, has minimized the event into "it was an accident." Rowan, a photographer, carries sensory fragments she can't assemble into a story.

And Cate — the fifth guest — isn't who she claims. She's Aisling, Kiera's younger sister, a journalist who has spent her adult life investigating the death.

Over one long weekend, the house triggers buried memories. The women's accounts contradict each other. Physical evidence surfaces. And Neve begins to remember what she saw that night: her mother, Elaine, pushing Kiera through the window.

The twist isn't just who killed Kiera. It's that Elaine has been gaslighting her own daughter for 25 years — and she orchestrated the reunion herself, from her deathbed, to control the confession.

**Core thematic question:** *Can you build a life on a foundation of lies and still call it living?*
